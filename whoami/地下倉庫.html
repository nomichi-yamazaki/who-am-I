地下倉庫

Darkness struck before the sounds did.
The hatch I had just slammed shut glowed faintly in the blackness above me, its narrow edges outlined by a thin white rectangle.
Those slivers of light were the only remaining outline of the world.
Take even a single step away from them, and the darkness became absolute.
I couldn't see what was there.
I couldn't even tell whether anyone else was.
Something crashed and thrashed overhead.
Within the pale outline of the hatch, dark shadows writhed and lunged across the light.
"OHHHHH!!... O-OOOOHHHHH—AAAHHH!!"
Takizawa-san's scream echoed through the floor above me.
Takizawa-san...
They're getting him...
Takizawa-san...
There was nothing I could do.
Darkness.
My breathing came in ragged gasps.
A high-pitched ringing filled my ears.
I placed a hand against the cold floor and simply felt the fact that I was still alive.
Takizawa-san had lamented that he couldn't save Yohei—that he had failed as a leader.
And yet, in the end, he had sacrificed himself to protect me.
Maybe that guilt had driven him to make that choice.
That was what I found myself thinking.

I pressed myself against the floor beside the hatch.
I could feel my entire body trembling uncontrollably.
Even when I tried to stop it, I couldn't.
I would draw a breath, yet the air never seemed to reach my lungs.
It felt as though I had forgotten how to breathe.
My heartbeat had grown so loud that I genuinely feared it was echoing through the darkness.
That somehow, impossibly, it might leak out and give me away.
They're coming.
Sooner or later, they're coming.
At any moment, another crash and another scream would erupt directly above me.
The hatch would burst open.
And that maddened swarm would come pouring down into the darkness.
The thought alone left my throat burning with dryness.
My knees shook.
I couldn't move.
If I moved now, I felt certain I would touch something.
In this darkness, there was no way to prove that nothing was there.
If I made a sound, it would be over in an instant.
Even my breathing felt dangerous.
So I remained frozen in place, staring at the faint sliver of light outlining the hatch above me.

Uchida-san... Morishita-san... Takizawa-san...
They were all human.
And they were all taken.
They had been right in front of me.
Maybe there had been something I could have done to save them.
Maybe.
The helplessness of having done nothing twisted painfully in my chest.
I'm finished...
I'm finished.
Someone like me...
The words surfaced on their own.
Even if I stayed hidden here, sooner or later one of those things would find me.
And when it did, I would end up just like everyone else.
The thought chilled me to the core.
Everyone's gone.
There were thirty-one of us, and now hardly any humans are left.
Mari-senpai too...
She's probably been taken as well.
The moment that thought crossed my mind, something inside me seemed to snap.
What was my life, anyway?
Born.
Studied.
Worked.
And that's it.
What an empty life it had been...
My thoughts circled endlessly, going nowhere.
Only the anxiety remained, growing heavier, darker, sinking deeper and deeper inside me.
I felt heat building behind my eyes.
Tears spilled out one after another, tracing the edges of my eyelids.
At the same time, I felt moisture gathering deep inside my nose.
A moment later, it began to run down my face.
I rubbed at my eyes and wiped at my nose with both hands.
The tears and mucus smeared across my cheeks in wet streaks.

I had assumed the crashing noises and screams from the kitchen above were still continuing.
Then suddenly, I realized something.
The sounds had stopped.
Whether the chaos had moved elsewhere, whether they were lying in wait, or whether everyone up there had been overwhelmed—any of those possibilities seemed plausible.
I hated every one of them.
Now, all I could hear was the distant sound of rain and wind.
The murderous frenzy that had filled the world only moments ago felt strangely unreal, as though it had never happened at all.
But that wasn't comforting.
It was too quiet.
The silence that followed the chaos only tightened its grip around my mind.
In the darkness, my thoughts began moving on their own.
Where are they waiting?
What if the silence beyond that hatch isn't silence at all?
What if they're simply listening?
What if the moment I move, they'll know exactly where I am?
My own thoughts had become my enemy.
Heat gathered in the back of my throat.
My lips trembled.
My breathing grew shallow.
After a while, I finally understood something.
I wasn't merely frozen by tension anymore.
I was shaking, even though I wasn't cold.
My palms were slick with sweat.
My chest ached.
My legs felt weak and unsteady.
And yet, I wanted to run.
I wanted to bolt from this darkness without making a single sound.
But the moment I imagined running, I could see it clearly—
I would be caught.
I couldn't move.
Yet I wanted to run.
I wanted to run.
Yet I couldn't move.
Down here in the pitch-black darkness beneath the lodge, my heart reached a point it had never reached before.
For the first time, the instinct to survive had become stronger than hope.
