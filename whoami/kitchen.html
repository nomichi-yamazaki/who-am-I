Kitchen

The dining hall in the Old Wing was completely different from the lively atmosphere of the New Wing.
There were no tables or chairs, only a vast, empty space stretching before me.
A thick layer of dust covered the floor, and each step I took produced a dry scritch... scritch... beneath my feet.
Three sides of the room were lined with lattice windows.
Through the windows on the corridor side, I could just make out a row of wooden doors on the far side of the corridor.
The other two sides had been sealed off from the outside with plywood sheets and black-and-yellow hazard rope, completely blocking the view beyond.
The remaining wall was occupied by a serving counter.
Naturally, the kitchen lay on the other side.
There was no one there.
No sound.
No sign of movement.
The air was cold, carrying the mingled scent of dust and old wood that stung my nose.
There was no fire, no human presence—only silence, and the sound of my ragged breathing echoing through the room.

I stood motionless in the center of the dining hall.
The room was so vast that it felt less like a refuge and more like a cage with no escape.
—Beep.
Whenever that sound rings out, the Things seem to gather.
That was the conclusion I had reached.
As if summoned.
As if drawn toward it.
They always moved toward that sound.
The people I had hoped were still safe.
The people I had believed I could rely on.
Tsuruoka-san and Yohei had already been taken.
Maybe there was no one left besides me.
The moment that thought crossed my mind, something inside my chest collapsed with an audible crack.
The friends I thought I could depend on.
The friends I thought I could escape with.
I had trusted Yohei.
I had believed Tsuruoka-san would somehow be safe too.
But I was wrong.
Tsuruoka-san was gone.
Yohei was gone.
Then what about Takizawa-san?
What about Yabe-san?
And Mari-senpai...?
My chest tightened painfully.
Please... let Mari-senpai be safe.
She had always looked out for me.
She had always been kinder than anyone else.
She could be strict when she needed to be, but in the end she always smiled.
Whenever I was frightened, she somehow found her way to my side.
The backs of my eyes began to burn.
Before the tears could roll down my cheeks, I hurriedly wiped them away with my sleeve.
But they wouldn't stop.
My throat felt so tight that it hurt to breathe.
What if Mari-senpai has been taken too...?
The instant the thought surfaced, I felt as though I might suffocate.
My chest ached.
My stomach turned cold.
I was alone.
Alone in these mountains.
Alone in the Old Wing.
With nowhere to run.
No allies left.
No way to call for help.
What am I supposed to do?
How am I supposed to survive this?
My thoughts spun in circles, chasing themselves endlessly.
My knees trembled.
The strength drained from my body.
My vision blurred.
Maybe it would be easier if they got me too.
The thought surfaced from somewhere deep inside me, like a whisper spoken by someone who wasn't me.
Then I wouldn't have to feel this loneliness anymore.
Or this fear.
Become one of them... and the suffering ends.
For the briefest moment, my mind almost drifted toward the idea.
The air in the empty dining hall was cold and thick with dust.
The tears that fell at my feet slowly soaked into the pale layer of powder covering the floor.
And there, in the middle of the deserted dining hall,
all I could do was stand frozen in place.

At that moment, a faint sound echoed from deep within the kitchen.
Clink...
Something small had rolled across the floor.
My heart instantly lurched into my throat.
Is someone there...? Or is it one of the Things?
Yohei had hidden himself while still wearing the form of a Thing.
That meant I couldn't let my guard down, even if what I found looked human.
Maybe it was already there...
Lurking in that stage before the transformation became obvious.
I froze where I stood and held my breath.
I listened carefully.
No second sound followed.
Silence.
Not the comforting kind.
The kind that only made everything feel more unsettling.
I stood motionless in that silence, desperately trying to decide what to do.
If the source of that sound was a Thing—
then getting closer would mean the end.
I needed to run.
I needed to get away from this place as fast as possible.
But if it was a human being—
if someone was still alive—
then maybe we would have a chance to survive together.
I'd somehow managed to make it this far alone,
but if I was honest, I wasn't sure I could keep doing it much longer.
So what should I do?
Stand here in the middle of the dining hall and wait, trembling, for something to happen?
No.
That wasn't going to save me.
The Things outnumbered me overwhelmingly.
If they truly wanted to find me, they probably could.
Yohei had come close enough to grab me, yet he hadn't attacked.
Most likely, they already considered nearly everyone to be one of their own.
The remaining survivors were probably just a matter of time.
They could add me to their ranks whenever they chose.
If I stayed here, I would become nothing more than prey waiting to be collected.
And running blindly without knowing what lay ahead was just as dangerous.
If someone was there—
if that person was human—
then that single fact could change everything.
There was only one way to know.
Fear and hope twisted together inside my head until I could barely tell them apart.
I argued with myself again and again, turning the possibilities over endlessly.
At last, I reached a decision.
If I stayed here, I would die.
So I had to move forward.
To learn the truth behind that sound,
I had to enter the kitchen.
Slowly, I began walking toward the serving counter.

(If I just stand here, I'll die...)
I told myself that and reached for the small door beneath the serving counter.
Creeeak...
The hinges groaned, the sound stabbing into my eardrums.
Peering inside, I crouched down and carefully slipped through the opening.
The kitchen was lined with white tiles. Unlike the new building, it still carried traces of the days when people had actually worked there.
In the center stood a stainless-steel preparation table.
The kitchen lights should have been off, yet the dim light filtering in from outside reflected off the tiles, filling the room with a cold, pale glow.
And in the shadow of that table—there was a figure.
I tensed instinctively.
A person... or one of those things?
My heart was still pounding wildly.
Then the figure cried out in a trembling voice.
"Don't come any closer! Please... stay away..."
A voice filled with terror.
A voice that sounded as though it were choking on tears.
The moment I heard it, it felt as though electricity shot through my chest.
(That voice... It's Takizawa-san.)
"Namu Aminadabu... Namu Aminadabu..."
Takizawa was chanting Namu Aminadabu.
Did that mean he was a follower of Banrikyo?
I had never known that.
Had he invited me on this mountain trip because he eventually wanted me to join Banrikyo too?
At this point, I couldn't tell who was a believer and who wasn't.
...Strangely enough, it felt a lot like not knowing who was human and who was one of those things.
The thought crossed my mind.
But in a situation like this, whether Takizawa belonged to Banrikyo or not simply didn't matter anymore.
The figure huddled in the shadows, arms wrapped tightly around his chest, trembling uncontrollably.
Not hostility.
The voice of a human being whose throat had been crushed by fear.
"T-Takizawa-san...?"
The moment I spoke his name, the figure jerked violently.
His face remained lowered, but I could easily imagine the terror written across it.
Would one of those things chant Namu Aminadabu...?
And... he's afraid of me?
Which means...
Everyone who had become one of those things had tried to approach me.
None of them had ever tried to run from me in fear.
(Takizawa-san... might still be human.)
Warmth rose in my chest.
Whether it was relief or hope, I couldn't tell.
All I knew was that there might still be someone here I could act together with—and that possibility alone pushed my feet forward.

“Takizawa-san... I-I’m not coming any closer. I’ll stay right here and talk. Just... please listen to me. You may not believe me, but...”
Breathing heavily, I forced the trembling words out.
“I ran into Yohei earlier. Yohei... he'd already been taken by one of those things. Whenever that high-pitched sound goes off... they seem to gather in one place. Yohei... left when it happened. He just... went somewhere.”
Takizawa-san's face remained tense, but I couldn't stop talking. If I stopped, I felt like I would lose my mind.
“Tsuruoka-san was taken too... Ever since I got locked in that storage room, I've been escaping through the crawlspace. I saw everything that happened in the dining hall from above. Those three young people who came in from outside... they transformed. They attacked people. The one who held a knife on me... a lot of people got taken...”
My voice began to speed up as I spoke.
“After that, I met up with the people hiding in the staff room. I escaped with Tsuruoka-san, Suzuki Sho-san, Morishita Aya-san, and Uchida Ayako-san. Then when we got to the second floor... there was Kobayashi Ren-san, the two women in their twenties, the couple who'd come here for tent camping, and the man in jeans...”
The memories came rushing back all at once, and my mouth moved faster than my thoughts.
“Then I noticed... there was an eye inside Kobayashi Ren-san's ear... and then the one who'd held a knife on me and locked me in the storage room dropped down from the ceiling and started transforming... Even then, everyone should have still been alive at that moment. But after that... I don't know what happened. I had to run into the crawlspace again... I think Tsuruoka-san was taken then...”
The last words escaped almost as a breath.
I slowly raised my hands, trying to show that I meant no harm, and continued desperately.
“I understand... if you can't believe me. But it's true. I didn't make this story up just because I wanted to save myself. If you're still... if you're still really Takizawa-san...”
I swallowed hard.
“I want us to help each other.”

Takizawa-san's shoulders began to shake, and he lowered his head as tears started to fall.
"...I couldn't save Yohei... I... I'm a failure as a leader..."
His voice was hoarse, and the tears wouldn't stop.
Seeing him like that, I felt a painful tightness in my chest.
"Takizawa-san... but that doesn't mean..."
The words died in my throat.
Takizawa-san said nothing more.
He simply sat there, his shoulders trembling slightly, seeming to regain his composure little by little.
Watching him, I found myself thinking:
Takizawa-san was human.
He really was human.
Quietly, I sat down a short distance away from him.
The floor was cold, and dust rose into the air, but the terror-strained tension in my nerves eased ever so slightly.
"What are we supposed to do now...?" I murmured softly.
Takizawa-san didn't answer, but there was a calmness about him that made me feel that, at least for the moment, he had regained his human reason.
"...For now, all we can do is wait until morning... wait until the typhoon passes. If we're going to survive, we'll have to stay hidden like this..."
I spoke as much to myself as to him.
Neither of us met the other's eyes.
We simply sat in silence, holding our breath and waiting for time to pass in the depths of the dining hall.
Outside, the sound of the raging wind echoed coldly through the dim room.

The typhoon winds shook the building without mercy.
Rain hammered against the roof in a steady rhythm, while every so often the sharp creak of straining timber drifted through the darkness.
In one corner of the dim kitchen, Takizawa-san sat huddled behind the preparation table.
I had taken a seat against the wall a short distance away.
A small space remained between us.
We were aware of each other's presence, yet neither of us moved any closer than necessary, unwilling to let our guard down completely.

Thirty-one people, including the lodge staff.
Of those, the only ones who might still be human were Suzuki Sho-san, Morishita Aya-san, Uchida Ayako-san, Takizawa-san here beside me, Mari-senpai, Yabe-san...
...and me.
I had believed everyone in my group was safe.
But Tsuruoka-san and Yohei had already been taken.
Out of thirty-one people, only seven still had any chance of being human.
Judging by how terrified Takizawa-san was, there was a good chance he was still human.
At the very least, there were two of us left.
Takizawa-san and me.
But even if all seven were still human, that meant twenty-four people had already become one of those things.
The odds were overwhelmingly against us.
I mentally reviewed the roster, trying to keep track of everyone who might still be human.
Mixed with the roar of the typhoon outside, I found myself listening for every tiny sound within the kitchen.
Takizawa-san was doing the same. Each time the building shuddered beneath the force of the storm, his body stiffened.
Silence stretched between us.
Neither of us watched the other.
Our attention remained fixed on the sounds outside and the creaking of the building around us.
This was the old lodge.
Sealed off.
If we stayed here...
Maybe we could survive for now.
By morning, when the typhoon passed...
Maybe we'd be able to move.
Takizawa-san said nothing.
In the darkness, he simply sat there, listening to the rain.
Even so, without speaking, we shared the same understanding:
Stay hidden.
Stay alive.
"Saku-chan..." Takizawa-san suddenly said.
"Do you think there are any other survivors?"
"I don't know," I answered.
"But I don't think we can say everyone's gone."
"How many of those things are there? From what you've seen?"
I swallowed.
"The humans are badly outnumbered."
"As far as I can tell, the only people who might still be human are you and me... Mari-senpai, Yabe-san, Suzuki Sho-san from the lodge, Morishita Aya-san, and Dr. Uchida Ayako-san."
"Everyone else has already been taken."
"And even among the people I just named... some of them might already be gone too."
Mari-senpai...
Please be safe.
Takizawa-san drew a sharp breath.
"Do you think we're safe here?"
The question caught me off guard.
The group's leader was looking to me for answers.
Me.
Someone this frightened.
Someone who could barely hold himself together.
"It's quiet right now," I said.
"But they could show up at any time."
Silence returned.
Both of us listened carefully for the slightest sound.
"...There's no way to call for help," Takizawa-san muttered.
"No signal. The radios are dead."
"And if we go outside..." I said, "...we're more likely to get spotted."
"I want everyone to be alive."
His voice cracked.
"But honestly...
I don't even know what's left anymore."
He lowered his eyes and clenched his teeth, trying to swallow his frustration.
"What if it's just us?" he whispered.
The question made me realize something.
Takizawa-san wasn't made of strength alone.
He was scared too.
"...We don't know that yet," I said quietly.
He didn't answer.
But I thought his breathing became just a little steadier.
Takizawa-san sat huddled behind the preparation table and let out a small sigh.
"...If only I'd stopped Tsuruoka back then...
Maybe none of this would've happened."
Normally he called him Tsuru-san.
Now he used only his surname.
The regret ran that deep.
Listening to him, I felt something tighten painfully in my chest.
"I'm sorry..."
My voice came out barely above a whisper.
"If I hadn't found that thing..."
The image of the black fetus refused to leave my mind.
Takizawa-san sat in silence for a while before finally lifting his head and looking around the dark kitchen.
"There's no point talking about that now."
"Right now, we need to focus on surviving."
Inside the abandoned kitchen, dark and forgotten, neither of us met the other's eyes.
And yet, in that shared silence, we carried the same determination.
To survive this reality together.
