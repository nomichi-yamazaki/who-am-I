天井裏

—I wonder if Mari-senpai is okay…
…What about Yohei? Takizawa-san?
…Not that I care about Tsuruoka-san…
No. I shouldn’t think like that.
Tsuruoka-san is still a human being too…
Right now, everyone in this lodge is in danger.
Uchida-san, the doctor… Takamura-san, who had lost Akane-san…
The lodge owner I’d joked around with about Bon Curry…
Suzuki Sho-san, who I’d first thought was kind of an asshole because he kept complaining, but who had helped me build the weapons and had even defended me earlier, saying I wasn’t one of the “Somethings”…
Kobayashi Ren-san, the mountain photographer…
And Morishita Aya-san, who looked a little like Mei Nagano…

Keeping myself small, I stared blankly down the narrow passageway between the shelves in the storage room.
Canned food. Dried supplies. Old cooking utensils.
Just imagining that one of those strange “Somethings” might be hiding even in a place like this made it impossible to sit still.
—I have to warn Mari-senpai somehow…
The thought of Mari-senpai becoming something else… no. I couldn’t bear it.
With that fear circling through my head, I slowly looked around the storage room again.
Shelves stacked with cardboard boxes and oversized cookware.
Then I looked up.
A wooden ceiling.
And in the corner where the ceiling met the wall, there was a square opening framed in aluminum with a metal trim around it.
An access panel.
—That’s it. The crawl space.
Back when I worked at Sansei Denshi Seiki, I’d crawled through spaces like that searching for electrical insulation faults.
Dust. Heat. Itchy insulation fibers. The low electrical hum of wires running through the darkness.
I could still remember how suffocating those spaces felt.
If this lodge was built the same way, I might be able to crawl through the space above the ceiling all the way to the dining hall.
And if I could do that, maybe I could warn Mari-senpai.
It was the only option I had.
If I opened that access panel, I could get into the crawl space.
The shelves on both sides nearly reached the ceiling, and if I climbed onto the top shelf, I should be able to reach it easily enough.
To pry the panel open, I’d need a flat tool.
At the bottom of the shelf to my front right, there had been what looked like a blue metallic toolbox.
Kneeling down, I pulled it toward me and undid the rusted latch.
Inside were sealing tape, pliers, an adjustable wrench, water-pump pliers, a Phillips screwdriver… and a flathead screwdriver.
“…There.”
I picked it up carefully with my fingertips.
The metal felt cold.
This should be enough to force the panel open.
I’d also need some kind of light.
Then I remembered — when they shoved me into this storage room, I’d seen a flashlight mounted on the wall to the left.
I had no idea whether the batteries still worked, but it was better than nothing.
Slowly exhaling, I whispered to myself.
—I have to go. I have to warn Mari-senpai.
If I crawl through the ceiling space, I should be able to reach the dining hall.
And if I can get my voice to them from up there… maybe there’s still time.
Standing in front of the shelves, I tightened my grip around the screwdriver in my hand.

I slipped the flathead screwdriver into the pocket of my shorts.
Taking the flashlight from its holder on the wall, I shoved it into the other pocket.
I kicked off the slippers at my feet, then peeled off my socks as well.
Honestly, I hated the thought of getting filthy… but slipping and falling would be far worse.
Placing my hands into the narrow gaps between the cardboard boxes, I began climbing the three-tier shelf.
I could feel the hard wooden edges digging into my toes as I climbed higher.
The higher I went, the warmer the air near the ceiling became, brushing heat against my face.
Reaching the access panel, I braced myself carefully against the edge of the shelf with my hips and feet, holding the panel shut with my left hand while pressing the tip of the flathead screwdriver against the screw fastened into the aluminum trim.
As I twisted the screw, the latch released.
The panel gave way to gravity and quietly swung downward.
I jerked my head aside to avoid being hit as it dropped open.
The moment the opening appeared, a strange smell drifted down from the crawl space above.
Dust. Old wood.
And mixed faintly among them… gasoline. Something burned.
Naoki-kun…
I recognized the smell instantly.
A wave of hesitation passed through my chest.
I didn’t want to crawl into that dust-filled space.
But if I had to, there were spare clothes down here.
This was for Mari-senpai.
Steeling myself, I pushed my head up into the opening.

The four corners of the access opening were suspended from above by thin metal rods.
In the darkness,somewhere deeper off to the right, I could make out a faint light.
Beyond the beam immediately to my left was a concrete wall, and behind it, I could picture the generator room.
The place where Suzuki Sho-san and I had built the weapons…
Just thinking about it made my body tense.
The dim yellow light from the storage room below only reached this far.
Everything beyond that was swallowed in darkness.
In a situation like this, peering into a crawl space felt like the kind of thing that might reveal something staring back at you, and a chill crawled up my spine before I could stop it.
When I switched on the flashlight, the narrow but sprawling space above the ceiling emerged faintly in its yellow beam.
Some distance off to the right, a spiderweb stretched between the beams glimmered softly in the light.
For some reason, I found myself thinking that even up here in the mountains, spiders still lived their lives.
Above me spread the underside of the second floor.
Thick beams ran overhead, and between them, rows of narrow wooden joists had been laid side by side at even intervals, each piece roughly 45 by 60 millimeters in cross-section, with the longer side set vertically.
A little higher above the opening, thick beams stretched one after another into the distance at regular intervals, like the rungs of a giant ladder laid across the ceiling.
On top of them stood vertical posts supporting the larger crossbeams above.
And here and there, thick support pillars rose through the darkness, probably running all the way from the first floor to the second.
