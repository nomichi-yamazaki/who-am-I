Restroom

The reflected shapes wavered and distorted, and for an instant it sometimes looked as though someone had moved behind me.
The illusion made me catch my breath every time.
Looking outside, the rain droplets clinging to the glass made it difficult to focus.
But when I narrowed my eyes, I could make out the long restroom building stretching to the left beyond the passage between the lodge and the toilets.
Beyond the orange glow of the outdoor lights, the open entrance of the restroom building glowed with bright white fluorescent light, forming a tall rectangle in the darkness.
Nothing moved within sight.

I really need to hurry.

It looked safe.

The covered passage stretching to the left disappeared into darkness, its far end completely hidden from view.

A sharp pressure tightened deep in my abdomen.
I knew I was running out of time.

Between them hung a faded mountain poster, warped by moisture, its surface gently swelling and sinking as though it were breathing.
The sliding door at the back appeared to be slightly open.

My breathing grew shallow.
The pressure in my gut had grown beyond the point where willpower alone could hold it back.

Right here.

Rattle...
