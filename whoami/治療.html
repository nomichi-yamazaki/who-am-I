As the dining hall came into view, Mari-senpai stepped forward and hurried to the entrance.
Grabbing the sliding door, she pulled it open with a sharp clatter and held it aside, turning her body so everyone could pass through more easily.
“Hurry, get inside!”
Still carrying the stretcher, we moved into the dining hall.
As I entered, the stretcher between me and Naoki-kun made it hard to maneuver, and my body bumped against the edge of the doorway.
The moment we passed through the entrance, the wide space opened before us, filled with the faint scent of wood.
Bare light bulbs hanging from the ceiling cast a dim yellow glow.
The light reflected softly off the wooden ceiling and walls, but the shadows falling across the face of the boy in the black tracksuit on the stretcher were strangely deep, making the writhing fragment of tentacle look even more disturbingly vivid.

Thick beams ran across the high ceiling, and both the walls and the floor were made of aged wood.
The room was spacious, with pillars standing in rows.
Between the pillars, long tables were arranged in neat lines, each surrounded by wooden chairs with backrests packed closely together.
At one corner of the tables, Takizawa-san and Tsuruoka-san were already standing.
Takizawa-san stood with his chest slightly puffed out, looking at us with a faint expression of relief.
Tsuruoka-san had his arms crossed, fixing us with a sharp gaze—but behind his eyes, there was also a trace of concern.

Along both the outer wall of the dining hall and the wall facing the corridor, a row of glass windows with fine lattice patterns stretched across.
In the outer windows, the lined-up pillars and tables were faintly reflected—along with the four of us carrying the stretcher bearing the boy in the black tracksuit, and the people surrounding us, all blurred together in the glass.

“Hey, hey… this turned into quite a mess, didn’t it? What the hell did you guys bring back?” Tsuruoka-san said, looking toward us as we carried the stretcher.
—What do you mean, what did we bring back…? You were the one who ran off first… I thought.
I remembered Tsuruoka-san yelling at me when that thing had gone missing.
Because he had gone after those two—the jock and the guy in glasses and jeans—I ended up getting blamed for everything… I thought.
“Oh, good, good—everyone’s safe. That’s a relief!” Takizawa-san said cheerfully as he walked alongside us, following the stretcher.
“…For someone who’s supposed to be the leader, you sure ran off fast,” Mari-senpai cut in.
At that, Takizawa-san’s smile faded, and he lowered his eyes, as if reflecting on himself.
“…You’re right. I’m sorry. I couldn’t think clearly back then. Pathetic, really,” Takizawa-san said.
“Not like Taki to mess up a call like that… well, everyone’s alive, so I guess that’s what matters for now,” said Tsuruoka-san—the one who had run first—as he followed alongside the stretcher carrying the boy in the black tracksuit.
—Not Takizawa-san… what about you? I thought.

“All right… here should do.”
The lodge owner, carrying one end of the stretcher at the front, stopped near the center of the dining hall and looked around as he spoke.
—This is the table where we ate dinner earlier… I thought.
Yohei and I, together with the owner and Ren Kobayashi-san, stopped in unison and slowly lowered the stretcher onto the table.
Takizawa-san quickly stepped forward as well, lightly supporting the head side of the stretcher so we could set it down safely.
“All right, just a little more. Hang in there, Saku-chan.”
Maybe because Takizawa-san’s voice sounded so calm, some of the tension inside me eased a little instead.
The stretcher was placed with Naoki-kun’s head toward the outer windows and his feet toward the corridor side.
The moment the stretcher settled securely onto the table, everyone let out a quiet breath of relief.
The face of the boy in the black tracksuit lying on the white canvas was illuminated by the dim yellow light hanging from the ceiling, and the writhing fragment of tentacle glistened faintly.
The people who had surrounded us also entered the dining hall and now stood at the ends of the long tables or between the chairs, silently watching to see what would happen next.
Suzuki Sho-san—the receptionist who had complained to me earlier—came hurrying back, breathing hard.

“Okubo-san! …The radio’s not getting through!”
The lodge owner looked up sharply.
“What? …Might be because of the lightning… My phone lost signal a while ago too.”
—He means when that lightning struck while I was in the drying room earlier… I thought.
I pulled my own smartphone from the right pocket of my shorts.
In the upper-right corner of the rounded rectangular screen, the words No Service were displayed.
—Yeah… mine’s dead too… I thought.
Ren Kobayashi-san spoke in a calm voice.
“Maybe the Wi-Fi antenna got hit. That lightning earlier was pretty close.”
The owner nodded and let out a sigh.
“Probably… communications are completely down then. That’s bad. If we can’t contact the outside…”
—Does that mean nobody’s phone works…?
If the radio’s dead too, then we can’t even call for help…
Well… even if we could, there’s a storm raging outside…
As I thought that, I felt a tight heaviness spreading through my chest.

“She’s… gone.”

“Right… We should still have some face shields left over from the COVID days. I’ll go get them. It’d be better to have face shields too, right?” said Suzuki Sho-san from the front desk—the same one who had complained to me earlier—before hurrying out of the dining hall.

The wind rattled the windowpanes.
Rattle… rattle… clatter…
Even from inside, the sound of the rain could now be heard clearly.
The way it came and went in uneven bursts was probably because of the wind.
A little while later, Suzuki Sho-san returned carrying several transparent face shields and two white kappogi aprons in his hands.
“Here—face shields. And you should probably use these too,” Suzuki-san said as he handed the shields and aprons to Uchida-san and the slender woman.
“Thank you. That helps,” they replied, quickly putting them on.
Uchida-san and the slender woman slipped the bands of the transparent face shields over their heads, the clear plastic covering their entire faces.
An improvised “medical team” had formed in the middle of the dining hall.
—It really looks like a medical scene now… I thought.
Naoki-kun—the young man in the black tracksuit, who had been groaning in pain until moments ago—glanced over at Uchida-san and the slender woman as they put on the aprons and face shields. His face twisted in pain, yet he still managed a weak little smile.
“…Ahh… so I’m gonna get cooked after all…” he muttered faintly.
For a brief moment, the entire room fell silent.
Then Suzuki Sho-san raised his voice.
“Don’t be stupid—we’re not making nikujaga outta you!”
The lodge owner added, “Naoki, if you’ve still got enough energy to joke around, you’ll be fine.”
The tension filling the room loosened just a little.
Still… was it my imagination?
The severed end of the tentacle attached to Naoki’s cheek looked even rounder now… and somehow shorter than before.
“Hold him steady while we observe it. And whatever you do, do not touch the tentacle…” Uchida-san said.
Holding the COVID face shield in place against her face, she stared intently at the tentacle attached to Naoki-kun’s cheek.
The slender beautiful woman we had met at Hakubajiri also stood on the opposite side of the table, protecting herself with gloves and a white kappogi apron while gently supporting Naoki-kun’s body.
Her eyes were serious, but there was also a determined look on her face, as though she were trying to steel herself.
Suzuki Sho-san took out a black flashlight, switched it on, and raised it toward Naoki-kun’s cheek.
Naoki-kun’s cheek was illuminated by a round white beam, and within that light the writhing tentacle could be seen squirming grotesquely.
“That helps. Thank you,” Uchida-san said with a small smile toward Suzuki Sho-san.
She exhaled softly and leaned forward slightly as she continued her examination.
“Ugh… it hurts…” Naoki-kun groaned.
“What kind of pain is it?” Uchida-san asked him.
“The place where it attached doesn’t hurt anymore. It feels more like… the pain’s spreading through my whole head… Sensei… am I gonna be okay? What’s gonna happen to me…?” Naoki-kun said.
“For now, stay still. Moving will only make it worse.”
Uchida-san gently placed a gloved hand on his shoulder, speaking in a calm voice meant to steady him.
“We’re all right here with you. It’s okay—we’re only observing it for now,” the slender woman added softly while resting a reassuring hand near him.
Naoki-kun tried to writhe again, but Uchida-san carefully used one hand covered in a translucent disposable cooking glove to gently press down the reddish, intestine-like tentacle attached to his cheek, bending it slightly so she could peer at the base where it connected.
Suzuki Sho-san raised the flashlight higher, illuminating the root of the thing.
“Stay still… I need to examine it carefully,” Uchida-san said.
Her voice was calm, and little by little the terror locking Naoki-kun’s body in place seemed to ease.
“Hang in there, Naoki…” Suzuki Sho-san said while keeping the light fixed on his face.
“Stay with us, Naoki-kun…” Morishita-san said as well.
“Ahh… Aya-chan… look what happened to me…”
Naoki-kun, lying on the table in his black tracksuit, opened his eyes slightly and looked toward Morishita-san as he spoke.
“You’ll be okay. It’s not… it’s not that bad…” Morishita-san started to say, but the rest of the words would not come out.
“Hang in there, Takahashi-kun,” Ren Kobayashi-san said too.
It was horrifying… and yet I couldn’t look away.
