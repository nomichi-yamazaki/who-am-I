Dining Hall

Grrr… clatter. The moment we slid open the dining hall door, every eye in the room turned toward us.
“Oh… you’re back.”
The lodge owner’s voice carried across the room.
He sounded calm, but he couldn’t completely hide the trembling in his shoulders.
That small movement alone sent another ripple through the heavy air of the dining hall.
As we stepped inside, our companions immediately looked up.
“You’re back!”
“Nice work!”
Mari-senpai hurried over to me first, letting out a relieved breath.
“How’d it go? Were you able to make them?”
“Yeah. We did. They’re set up at the crossroads.” I answered, then let my gaze move across the room.
For just a moment, a trace of brightness returned to everyone’s faces—
but it quickly faded back into the same grim, suffocating tension.
Aya Morishita-san, the university student who had studied environmental science, was quietly crying.
The others gathered around Naoki-kun’s table stepped aside to let me in.
And the instant I saw his face, a scream rose inside my head.
—What is happening to Naoki-kun…?
…How is he still alive in a state like this…?
A wave of terror washed through me, and before I realized it, my legs were trembling.
Naoki-kun’s hair was gone, as though it had dissolved into the slime itself.
The entire surface of his face had melted into something like soft white cheese, leaving only the rough shape of a human head barely recognizable beneath it.

“We made spears that can deliver an electric shock. He and I will use them, so we were thinking maybe we should post guards at the main entrance and the washroom exit in shifts…”
Suzuki Sho-san explained to the lodge owner, pointing at me.
His words cut off abruptly when Naoki-kun suddenly moved his hand.
Naoki-kun lay on the stretcher atop the table, dressed in his black tracksuit.
His face had melted into something like soft cheese, to the point where it was hard to tell he was even human anymore.
Slowly, he raised his right hand to his face and touched the place where the tendril had latched onto him.
Then he tried to pull it away.
The skin of his cheek stretched along with his fingers as he tugged.
“...!? W-What is this…? I—I can’t get it off…!” Naoki-kun groaned.
His hand remained stuck to his face.
He desperately tried to tear it free, but instead his skin stretched painfully with it, drawing a scream from him.
Uchida-san, Takamura-san, Ren Kobayashi-san, Aya Morishita-san, Mari-senpai, Yohei, Yabe-san, Takizawa-san, and Tsuruoka-san — everyone who had been standing around the table to shield Naoki-kun from view — recoiled all at once and backed away from the table.
Everyone was panicking.
Of course, me too.
My chest tightened, and chills crawled up my spine.
…That cold terror you get late at night when your mind wanders into something horrifying.
That feeling just kept going and going.
The sound of a chair toppling over.
Someone screaming.
No one remained near Naoki-kun’s table anymore.
“Ah… ahh… What’s happening to me…? What’s gonna happen to me…?”
Naoki-kun cried and moaned, his hair gone, his face melted into a wet, shapeless ruin.
Morishita-san was crying.
Even Mari-senpai’s eyes had filled with tears.
The slender, beautiful Takamura-san exchanged a glance with Doctor Uchida-san.
“……What do we do?”
Uchida-san grimaced and slowly shook her head.
“……This is beyond us. If we touch him carelessly, we could make it even worse.”
At those words, everyone fell silent for a moment.
Even Doctor Uchida-san and nurse Takamura-san were shaken.
—Even the doctors can’t do anything…
What is going to happen to him…?
I realized my body had curled inward instinctively, as though trying to protect itself.
I wanted to run away from this place.
But outside, the storm still raged.
And this was a mountain lodge near the summit.
There was nowhere to run.

Creaaak… creak… creak-creak…
Only Naoki-kun’s groans and the creaking of the table legs echoed through the dining hall.
I had completely forgotten about the weapons Suzuki Sho-san and I had made.
All I could do was stand there frozen.
Deep inside my chest, the sense of accomplishment I had felt only moments ago collapsed without a sound.
“Sensei… what’s happening to me…? …What’s gonna happen to me…? …I’m scared… ahh…”
Naoki-kun cried as he spoke, his voice barely forming proper words anymore.
If things had gone even slightly differently, the one lying there right now might have been me.
If I had grabbed it before Naoki-kun did…
If Mari-senpai hadn’t pulled my arm away, and those tendrils had reached me instead…
If I were the one who had ended up like this…
The thought alone sent a violent shiver through me.

“Maybe… we should move him to our room or something?”
Unable to stay gathered around Naoki-kun any longer — and perhaps conscious of the eyes of the people who had arrived later — Suzuki Sho-san spoke to the lodge owner, momentarily forgetting all about keeping watch.
“Yeah…”
That was all the owner could manage to groan in reply.
Everyone standing at a distance around Naoki-kun had tears welling in their eyes.
Then, in the next instant, the places where Naoki-kun’s eyes had once been suddenly thudded inward twice.
His entire head had become something like melted pale-yellow cheese, and now only two deep hollows remained.
“KYAAAH!”
“KYAAAH!”
“KYAAAH!”
“Ahhh!!”
“Uwaaah!!”
“Aaahhh!”
The young women screamed.
Morishita-san screamed.
The pair of girls who had earlier been talking about which movie actor was handsome screamed too.
The lodge staff, Mari-senpai, Yohei, Takizawa-san, Tsuruoka-san — all cried out all at once.
I felt as though my chest were being crushed tight.
My legs shook so badly they felt ready to give out beneath me.
“Sensei…”
Takamura-san looked toward Uchida-san.
Uchida-san silently shook her head.
At first glance, both Uchida-san and Takamura-san appeared calm.
But looking closer, I could see both of them trembling.

Naoki-kun’s body suddenly swayed.
The left knee of his black tracksuit pants — marked with three white stripes down the side — slowly lifted and jerked awkwardly toward his chest.
—What is he trying to do…?
What is Naoki-kun trying to do?
I thought.
Everyone could only watch him from a distance.
Every face was rigid with fear.
Their eyes were filled with terror — and tears.
Morishita-san was crying openly now.
Even Ren Kobayashi-san, the mountain photographer, wiped at his eyes with one hand.
The lodge owner stood frozen with deep wrinkles carved between his brows.
On the white canvas stretcher, streaks of slime that had seeped through Naoki-kun’s socks remained smeared across the fabric.
Naoki-kun twisted his body to the left and slowly slid the raised leg over the edge of the table.
Schlop…
A wet, sticky sound echoed out as liquid dripping from the soles of his socks splattered onto the wooden floor, leaving behind a dark stain.
“Ahh…”
“Aah…!”
“Ah…”
Every time Naoki-kun moved, frightened voices escaped from the people around him.
Uchida-san and Takamura-san instinctively stretched both hands toward him.
But although they reached out, their fingertips never touched him.
Their hands froze helplessly in midair.
Both women bit their lips, grimacing, able to do nothing but watch.
Naoki-kun’s left hand pressed against the table.
His thin fingers trembled as they slowly forced strength into themselves, raising his upper body little by little.
His right leg dropped heavily off the table as well, and the body that had lain on the stretcher gradually shifted into a sitting position.
—What is he trying to do…?
What on earth is Naoki-kun trying to do?
I thought again.
Sitting on the edge of the table, Naoki-kun swayed unsteadily as he tried to shift his weight and place both feet onto the floor.
—Is he trying to stand…?
…In that condition?
The slime pooling beneath his feet slowly spread wider, more fluid seeping out through the fibers of his socks.
“Naoki-kun… no, don’t stand up…” Uchida-san said.
Her face was completely overcome with fear.
“Please… stay lying down…” Takamura-san pleaded too.
Her expression trembled with terror.
Creeeak…
The table legs let out another awful groan.
—What is Naoki-kun trying to do by standing up?
My chest tightened, cold shivers crawling down my spine.
—What am I supposed to do…?
…Should I grab him and force him back down?
But I didn’t have the courage to reach out my hand.
God… what is happening to him…?
At last, Naoki-kun slid himself off the table and stood upright.
With his right hand almost fused into his melted face, the figure before us no longer looked fully human.
A cold chill shot through my spine.
“He stood up…!?” someone cried out in a trembling voice.

Gyuuuuuu…… Kyuuuuu…… Goro… goro…
Some kind of horrible sound echoed from inside Naoki-kun’s body.
The area around the stomach of his black tracksuit suddenly swelled outward.
Then he bent forward sharply.
The next instant—Naoki-kun’s mouth opened wide, and a thick half-liquid mass spilled out from inside.
The colors mixed together in a revolting swirl of brown, black, red, and filthy green.
Gobogobogobo…
What he was vomiting slithered from his mouth like snakes writhing out of him.
A stench like rotting flesh exploded through the dining hall.
Bot… bot… bot…
Bechaa… bechabecha… becha…
The vomited mass splattered onto the wooden floor with wet impacts, spraying everywhere.
“Uwah!”
I jumped backward instinctively as droplets splashed all the way to my feet.
Ah…
I could hear my own heartbeat pounding in my ears.
“UWAAAAAH!!”
“NOOOOO!!”
Screams erupted throughout the room.
“Stay back!” Uchida-san shouted.
“Get away from him…” Kobayashi Ren-san said sharply.
Black strands of hair glistened wetly within the vomited mass.
Mixed into it was a half-melted eyeball.
The eyeball dropped to the floor, burst slightly on impact, then rolled across the dining hall floor with a wet korokoro sound.
—An eyeball!?
…There are eyes and hair mixed in what he threw up!
…What the hell does this mean?
…Does this mean the thing standing here isn’t Naoki-kun anymore!?
The people surrounding him all screamed and staggered backward at once.
Somebody tripped over a chair, and the sound of them crashing to the floor echoed through the dining hall.
“That’s… not vomit…”
Even Doctor Uchida-san stepped backward as she said it.
“Ahh…!”
“Aaah!!”
Voices of shock rose from every direction.
Everyone was trembling.
“Ooohh… ooohhh…”
Naoki-kun groaned in a low voice, his shoulders heaving up and down.
The horrible stench completely filled the room.
“Naoki-kun… calm down…”
Takamura-san said shakily, clearly panicking herself.
“Naoki-kun! …Stop it!”
Morishita Aya-san screamed, her voice nearly breaking apart.
“Naoki-kun… stop, don’t throw up anymore…! You’ll lose yourself!”
The lodge owner’s voice cracked into tears as he shouted.
Even in this situation, the phrase you’ll lose yourself struck me as strangely absurd.
But Naoki-kun didn’t stop.
“OOOHHH!!”
Gobogobogobogobo…
Still struggling desperately to tear the hand stuck to his face away, he continued vomiting half-melted chunks of flesh and slime from deep inside his throat.
“Th… this is horrible…”
Takamura-san gagged, covering her nose as she staggered back.
“W-what the hell… is this…?”
Tsuruoka-san muttered.
He no longer seemed able to stand on his own feet.
Both hands were planted on the table as his waist pulled backward in terror.
His entire back shook violently.
“Daddy, I’m scared!”
A child’s crying voice rang out.
“This is… not human…”
I heard some man mutter those words.
I stood there speechless.
My mind had gone completely blank.
All I could feel was my body curling inward as it shook uncontrollably.
What was spilling out from Naoki-kun’s body was far too grotesque to be called the inside of a human anymore.
I wanted to look away, but I couldn’t.
Naoki-kun remained bent forward, his right hand fused against his face as he gasped harshly for breath.
Then he slowly straightened his waist.
Standing upright, Naoki-kun thrust the right hand stuck to his face outward.
The flesh of his hand and cheek stretched grotesquely between them with a wet, elastic pull.
Still trapped in that fused, inhuman shape, he staggered forward unsteadily.
The hollows where his eyes had been looked empty… yet somehow filled with hatred.
“It’s your fault… your fault…”
Naoki-kun’s voice trembled, low and dull, as he moved toward us.
The places where his eyes had been were now two deep, swollen cavities.
And yet… could he still see?
I stepped backward, moving out of his path.
Even so, Naoki-kun kept shambling forward.
Each step made the flesh stretched between his face and the hand extended before him swing sickeningly back and forth.
And directly in his path… stood Tsuruoka-san.

…?
Something strange entered the edge of my vision, throwing my thoughts into confusion.
That ominous middle-aged man… was smiling.
Only moments earlier, he had been screaming in grief, convinced he had lost Kishida-san…
Behind him, partially hidden in his shadow, I caught glimpses of the strong-willed middle-aged woman — Kishida-san.
Huh…?
…Was I just seeing things?

Right before my eyes, the melting, ruined figure of Naoki-kun staggered past me toward the left.
Tsuruoka-san stood directly in his path.
“It’s your fault… your fault…”
The voice carried hatred, grief, and rage sharp enough to pierce the ears.
Tsuruoka-san instinctively stumbled backward.
Step by step, he was driven against the wall.
“Hiiieeeeeeeeh!!”
With a ridiculous scream, Tsuruoka-san grabbed something nearby and hurled it.
It looked like one of those old Sakuma Drops candy tins.
At that instant, time itself seemed to slow.
The can spun through the air—
…No, not a candy tin.
It was the fuel can Takizawa-san had set down earlier while refilling the camping stove.
Transparent liquid splashed from its round opening as it flew.
The can struck Naoki-kun and fell to the floor, skidding across the wood while more liquid spilled out, spreading around his feet.
The next instant, Tsuruoka-san yanked a lighter from his pants pocket and flipped it open with a sharp click.
It was the lighter Tsuruoka-san had proudly shown off back at the shelter hut — a special Zippo engraved with the symbol of the Ark of the Covenant.
His hand trembled violently, but after several attempts he finally ignited the flame… and threw the lighter toward Naoki-kun.
“BOF—!!”
Flames instantly erupted from around Naoki-kun’s feet and swallowed his body whole.
A fierce—almost explosive—wave of heat slammed into me, scorching the front of my body.
“KYAAAAH!”
“AAAHH!”
“UWAAAH!!”
“AHHH!”
Screams exploded throughout the lodge.
The fire blazing before us made my face burn with heat.
The liquid he had thrown was white gas — the same fuel Takizawa-san had been using for the camping stove earlier.
The flames consumed Naoki-kun in an instant, filling the lodge with the stench of burning flesh and blistering heat.
“W-What the hell are you doing!? Are you trying to burn the lodge down!?”
The lodge owner roared furiously, his eyebrows twisted upward as he shouted.
His voice shook with both rage and terror as he stamped across the floor.
“Naoki’s gonna die!” Suzuki Sho-san shouted.
Mari-senpai staggered backward as if pushed by the flames, covering her mouth with her hand as she groaned.
“Naoki-kun!!”
Tears welled in Morishita Aya-san’s eyes as she screamed and slammed her hand against the corner of a table.
“H… hot… s-so… hot…”
Engulfed in flames, Naoki-kun continued trying to move forward even as he groaned in agony.
At this point, he no longer looked human.
He looked like some grotesque monster.
The liquid spilled across the floor ignited one patch after another, and red light and black smoke swirled throughout the wooden mountain lodge.
I stood frozen in place, crushed by a sensation of heat and terror that seemed to press all the way into my chest.
The screams of the others and the roar of the flames echoed around me like a vision of hell.
The air reeked of the thing the creature had vomited, mixed with gasoline and the smell of burning flesh.
What made it even worse was that beneath all of it lingered something strangely similar to the smell of grilled meat — almost appetizing if taken out of context.
That only made the nausea and revulsion even more unbearable.
Uchida-san and Takamura-san looked at each other in panic, completely at a loss.
—I have to help him… we have to save Naoki-kun… the fire… we have to put out the fire…
But I remained rooted to the spot, my legs refusing to move.
As Naoki-kun burned before my eyes, the surging pressure of heat forced me to stagger backward instinctively.
My whole body trembled violently.
It felt hard to breathe.
The flames climbed all the way to the ceiling, blackening the wood above.
Suzuki Sho-san hurried toward the kitchen in frantic steps, crouched down, opened the cabinet beneath the dish return shelf, and disappeared inside.
Kobayashi Ren-san threw open the dining hall door and dashed outside.
“It burns… it’s your… your fault… damn you… damn you… damn you…!”
Naoki-kun’s voice echoed hollowly from beyond the flames.
Whether any human will still remained inside him — or only rage — I couldn’t tell anymore.
The firelight reflected across the lodge ceiling, staining the windows red.
Smoke thickened through the room, and some people began coughing violently.
Combined with the storm winds outside, it felt as though the entire lodge might be swallowed by fire at any moment.
All I could do was stand there and stare at Naoki-kun.
—If we don’t do something now… everything is going to end.

Jiririririri…
Without warning, a piercing alarm rang throughout the dining hall.
The shrill sound stabbed into my ears, making the trembling in my body even worse.
People all around cried out at once, their voices mixing with screams.
Even I let out an involuntary, “Hiee…!” under my breath.
It sounded like the fire alarm bell.
At that very moment—Naoki-kun’s knees buckled with a sickening creak, and his body finally gave out beneath him.
Still fused into that grotesque shape where his right hand and face had become one, he dropped to his knees on the floor, hunching forward.
Around the collapsed Naoki-kun, the flames gradually began to shrink.
The towering fire that had reached the ceiling slowly lost its height.
The red glow and black smoke still swirled through the room, but the overwhelming force of the blaze weakened little by little.
Naoki-kun’s fallen body blocked the center of the fire, preventing the flames from surging upward anymore.
Seeing this, everyone held their breath.
Amid the shrieking fire alarm, the shouting, and the screams, people finally felt—if only for a brief instant—that the crushing pressure of the flames had eased.
Naoki-kun remained collapsed on the floor, breathing heavily through ragged shoulders.
He no longer tried to move forward.
He simply stayed there, hunched over and motionless.
That monstrous figure remained a symbol of terror before our eyes—yet at the same time, as the flames weakened, those around him were finally able to catch a breath.
Tsuruoka-san, Takizawa-san, Mari-senpai, Yabe-san, Yohei, Uchida-san, Takamura-san, the lodge owner, Morishita-san—everyone stared in stunned horror as events unfolded before them.
Jiririririri…
The fire alarm continued to ring.
The moment Naoki-kun finally collapsed face-down into the flames, Suzuki Sho-san came rushing back from the kitchen.
A fire extinguisher was clutched in his left hand.
He approached Naoki-kun’s burning body, yanked out the yellow safety pin from the extinguisher, squeezed the handle, and thrust it forward toward him.
Pshhhhh… Brrrraaaakakakaka…
From the tip of the black hose attached to the red extinguisher, a cloud of white spray burst forth.
The smoke and white chemical mist mixed together, swallowing the burning Naoki-kun from view.
A harsh odor—something like scorched ammonia—stabbed at my nose, leaving a rough bitterness clinging to the back of my throat.
Jiririririri…
The fire alarm kept screaming.
Kobayashi Ren-san returned through the dining hall entrance, which had been left hanging open.
When I looked toward him, I saw him desperately pulling a hose across the floor.
Watching all this, I felt crushed once again by the realization that I was completely useless.
I still couldn’t do anything.
As the smoke in the air gradually thinned, the room finally became visible again.
The drifting white chemical powder slowly settled over Naoki-kun’s body.
The orange flames shrank smaller and smaller, while the charred black surface beneath them turned white with extinguishing powder.
The scorched ammonia-like stench, the smell of burning, the gasoline fumes—and mixed among them, horribly enough, a scent almost like grilled meat—all of it seemed to stab directly into my lungs along with the heat.
The fire had been extinguished by the extinguisher Suzuki-san brought, but black scorch marks remained burned deeply into the tables and ceiling.
The hose Kobayashi Ren-san had dragged in had no valve attached to its nozzle, so to release water someone would have had to run all the way back to the hydrant.
In the end, it accomplished nothing.
Jiririririri…
Only the relentless ringing of the fire alarm remained.
On the floor lay Naoki-kun’s body, covered completely in white powder.

I noticed Tsuruoka-san crouched down small against the floor.
His eyes darted wildly, and his breathing was ragged.
Even from where I stood, I could tell he was shaken by what he had done.
“Murderer!”
A scream cut through the still-ringing fire alarm.
When I looked, it was Morishita Aya-san.
—Come to think of it, earlier I’d seen Morishita Aya-san walking beside Naoki-kun, the two of them talking about giant typhoons and global warming.
Had she… liked him?
The thought crossed my mind.
Jiririririri…
The fire alarm continued to shriek.
I saw Suzuki Sho-san and Kobayashi Ren-san leaving the dining hall.
“Murderer…! You killed him!”
Another sharp voice rang out.
Maybe it was one of the athletic-looking men.
At the same time, something dark and restless stirred deep inside my own chest.
Tsuruoka-san shrank back and raised his voice.
“That ain’t true! That thing weren’t human no more! It was a monster! I… I—!”
His words broke apart, and he covered his face with his hands, still trembling violently.
Jiri—
The blaring fire alarm suddenly stopped.
Tsuruoka-san’s whole body jerked in surprise.
—Ah… Suzuki Sho-san and Kobayashi Ren-san went to shut it off.
That realization crossed my mind.
“What are we supposed to do now…? With all this…”
The lodge owner spoke in a panicked voice—not accusing anyone, but clearly overwhelmed.
“It’s this thing’s fault…! It came close to me, that’s what’s wrong!”
Tsuruoka-san muttered frantically.
His body wouldn’t stop shaking.
Takizawa-san quietly placed a hand on his shoulder.
“Calm down, Tsuruoka. Nobody’s trying to blame you…”
But the instant he touched him, Tsuruoka-san violently flinched and slapped the hand away.
“Don’t touch me! I… I burned him—!”
His voice cracked apart into a scream tangled with terror and anger.
Everyone around him fell silent.
They could only stare.
Every face had gone stiff.
Even the smallest movements looked awkward and unnatural now.
Some people were visibly trembling.
And of course, I was no different.
A crushing heaviness tightened deep around my stomach.
If possible, I didn’t even want to exist in this place anymore.
If possible, I wanted all of this to be a dream.
But what was happening here was unmistakably real.
Damp wind drifted in from outside, while the smell of burning and the roar of the storm only sharpened the tension further.
“Namu Amina-dabu… Namu Amina-dabu… Namu Amina-dabu…”
Tsuruoka-san muttered the prayer over and over beneath his breath.
Near Naoki-kun’s powder-covered body lay the Zippo lighter engraved with the Ark of the Covenant symbol.
Tsuruoka-san had bragged about it so proudly before, yet now he wouldn’t even go near it.
Instead, he stared at it as though it were something filthy.
All I could do was stand there, trying to suppress the pounding of my heart while watching the trembling in Tsuruoka-san’s hands and the distortion in his face.
I understood the urge to lash out at someone.
To blame someone—anyone—to release the unbearable pressure building inside.
But seeing the man who had started the fire now trembling like this made the boundary between reality and monstrosity begin to waver inside me too.
Mari-senpai steadied her breathing and quietly murmured,
“But… if he hadn’t set him on fire… we don’t know what would’ve happened…”
Takizawa-san also gave up trying to comfort Tsuruoka-san, and an awkward distance quietly formed between them.
Staring at Naoki-kun’s body lying there beneath the extinguisher foam, I couldn’t even look away.
I could only stand frozen in place.
Storm winds battered the windows, and the smell of burning stung my nose.
The line between human and monster… between justice and fear… was swaying violently before my eyes.

“What are we supposed to do with this…?”
The lodge owner’s low, trembling voice echoed heavily through the room.
His eyes were fixed on Naoki-kun’s body, now covered in white fire-extinguisher powder.
“You murderer! What the hell are we supposed to do now?!”
The athletic-looking man glared at Tsuruoka-san, his face twisted with fear and rage, fists clenched tight.
“What the hell were you thinking?! Setting him on fire—!”
Glasses-and-jeans guy shouted as well.
Both of them kept their eyes locked on the trembling Tsuruoka-san.
“Calm down. This isn’t the time to start blaming people.”
Yabe-san spoke in a low voice as he stepped between the two sides.
It surprised me.
Yabe-san was usually the first to badmouth Tsuruoka-san, yet now she was the one stopping the others.
“That’s right. Now isn’t the time to blame anyone. We need to work together and figure out how to survive this situation first.”
The lodge owner pointed toward Naoki-kun’s powder-covered body on the floor and continued, trying to organize everyone’s panic with forced calmness.
“First, we need to decide what to do about Naoki-kun. We can’t just leave him here like this.”
“There’s no way we’re leaving something like that in here!”
The athletic-looking man stepped forward again, still unable to calm down, baring his teeth as he shouted.
“We oughta drag it outside and burn it or bury it already!”
“You can’t say something so horrible…”
Mari-senpai’s voice trembled.
“How can you talk about Naoki-kun like that?!”
Morishita Aya-san shouted, her face tightening with anger.
“He’s one of us.”
Suzuki Sho-san’s voice carried both grief and fury.
“No matter what he looked like in the end, we can’t just throw him away!”
“If we leave a monster like that lying around, there could be another disaster!”
The athletic-looking man snapped back stubbornly.
“What did you say?!”
Suzuki Sho-san raised his voice instinctively.
—Another disaster…?
That guy’s completely confused.
Naoki-kun was already dead.
The real danger was the creature still outside.
That was what mattered now.
“Arguments can wait. First we secure everyone’s safety.”
Kobayashi Ren-san spoke up, trying to calm the group.
But the way he spoke made it seem as though he was desperately forcing down his own emotions.
“Acting purely on emotion is dangerous, don’t you think?”
Yabe-san said quietly.
“Whatever this thing is, we shouldn’t go touching it carelessly. Safety has to come first.”
