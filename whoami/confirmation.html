Confirmation

Avoiding the pillars and tables inside the dining hall, I hurried toward the exit.
“Bon Curry! Check from inside first――don’t unlock the door!” the lodge owner’s voice called out from behind me.
“Okay! Got it!”
I answered while turning back with my hand still on the dining hall door.
Rattle…
I pushed the door open and stepped out into the damp hallway.
The smell of rain brushed past my nose, while the low howl of the wind echoed through the lodge.
When I reached the intersection in the corridor, I broke into a run straight toward the washroom.
With the rows of pillars and the stairway entrance passing on my left, my feet never slowed.
I arrived at the washroom entrance.
Looking through the lattice window, I could barely make out the long rectangular washroom beyond the rain-speckled glass, illuminated faintly by white light.
The droplets on the window blurred the focus, but when I strained my eyes, I saw it clearly.
Nothing remained on top of the wooden slats.
――It’s gone…
My chest tightened painfully.
Did that mean it had escaped somewhere else?
Or was it simply hidden somewhere I couldn’t see?
…And what happened to the people it attacked…?
Did they melt away and disappear…?
I wasn’t even fully sure anymore whether my own mind was still working properly, so I squinted and checked again.
No matter how many times I looked, there was nothing inside the washroom.
Every time, the result was the same.

I turned and ran back down the corridor toward the dining hall, weaving between the pillars, cutting through the intersection, then rounding the corner toward the dining hall entrance before pulling the rattling door open and rushing back inside.
The moment the door opened, everyone’s attention snapped toward me.
The two women in their twenties.
The man in his sixties dressed entirely in beige.
The father and his son.
The athletic-looking man and the guy in glasses and jeans.
The lodge owner.
Suzuki Sho-san from reception.
Mountain photographer Ren Kobayashi-san.
Morishita Aya-san.
Doctor Uchida-san and nurse Takamura-san.
Mari-senpai.
Yabe-san.
Yohei.
Takizawa-san and Tsuruoka-san…
Naoki-kun on the table was hidden behind the people standing around him, swallowed by shadow.
“There’s nothing there anymore,” I said under the weight of everyone’s gaze.
“Nothing? You mean the people who got attacked too?” the lodge owner asked.
“Yes. The black mass… and the people it attacked both seemed to be gone. Unless they’re hiding somewhere out of sight…” I answered.
“So does that mean the victims were eaten…? And if that’s the case… has it gotten bigger?” Suzuki Sho-san from reception said grimly.
“I… don’t know,” I replied.
“Either way, with the storm raging outside, it’s hard to imagine it’s gone very far from this lodge…” Suzuki Sho-san muttered.
“Yeah…” the lodge owner agreed quietly.
The windows rattled violently in the wind.
Every so often, a stronger gust struck the building hard enough to make the lodge creak with a sharp cracking sound, as though the whole structure itself were swaying.
Patter patter patter patter…
Large raindrops hammered loudly against the tin roof overhead.

I returned to the table where Naoki-kun lay and squeezed back into the line of people surrounding it.
Wondering what condition he was in now, I looked toward him――and saw the blisters on his head slowly swelling as though they were pulsing.
Everyone watched in breathless silence.
Then――Pop!
With a dry bursting sound, the first blister ruptured.
Sticky translucent green fluid splattered violently outward, spraying across Naoki-kun’s face, hair, and the tabletop.
“Whoa…!”
“Eek…!”
Takizawa-san, Tsuruoka-san, Mari-senpai, Yabe-san, Yohei, Suzuki Sho-san, Ren Kobayashi-san, Morishita Aya-san――every one of us instinctively recoiled and stumbled back from the table for a moment.
Then the remaining two blisters burst one after another――Pop! Pop!――spraying more slime as thick mucus spread over Naoki-kun’s entire head, dripping down with wet squelching sounds.
Left lying there on the table, Naoki-kun’s head was now coated in translucent green slime.
His black hair glistened as though soaked through, while his mouth opened and closed soundlessly as he struggled to breathe.
Everyone suddenly snapped back to themselves and crowded around the table again despite the fear twisting across their faces.
Uchida-san.
Takamura-san.
Mari-senpai.
Yabe-san.
Takizawa-san.
Yohei.
Suzuki Sho-san.
Tsuruoka-san.
Ren Kobayashi-san.
Morishita Aya-san.
Every single one of them had sweat forming on their faces, their expressions rigid with terror.
I wanted to look away from what was happening to Naoki-kun.
But I couldn’t tear my eyes away.
That was how it felt.
Takamura-san could no longer bring herself to keep holding Naoki-kun’s hand, and slowly let go.

“Just in case… could you tell me where the AED is kept?” Uchida-san quietly asked the lodge owner.
“It’s by the corner near the reception desk, right after you enter the lodge.”
Apparently Suzuki Sho-san at reception overheard as well, because he answered almost at the same time.
I heard the sound of quiet sobbing.
When I looked over, it was Morishita Aya-san.
I lowered my eyes toward my own hands.
They wouldn’t stop trembling.
As I stared at the faint rise and fall of Naoki-kun’s chest, accusing thoughts kept circling endlessly through my head.
If I hadn’t found that thing, none of this would have happened――
“Don’t you think we should figure out some way to kill it and lock it up somewhere?” Suzuki Sho-san said.
“It’s too dangerous to go outside. Right now, I think our only option is to stay here and wait…” Ren Kobayashi-san replied quietly.
“But there ain’t no guarantee that thing won’t smash through the windows and come inside.”
The one who spoke was Tsuruoka-san.
Suddenly, I found myself thinking that maybe Tsuruoka-san――despite always acting so overbearing――really was the kind of weak, frightened person Yabe-san had described.
After all, he had been the first one to run away in the washroom too…
“Still… no matter what, I think we should prepare something we can use as weapons,” Suzuki Sho-san said again.
This time, everyone gave small silent nods.
Meanwhile, left out of the discussion, the skeptical man in his sixties and the elementary school boy’s father exchanged grim looks with one another, their lips tightly pressed together.
Watching them, I realized they too had finally begun to understand how serious the situation really was.
—In the end, only the people who actually witness reality can truly accept it…
I murmured that quietly to myself before turning my attention back toward the others.
No one was letting their guard down anymore.
Every single person there was serious now.
And at the center of all their gazes, Naoki-kun’s face atop the stretcher glistened eerily like melted white cheese.
“You really are fixated on taking it down, huh? Guess that military background still shows,” Ren Kobayashi-san said with a faint smile.
“Well, that’s the kind of training I had. But right now, securing everyone’s safety comes first.” Suzuki Sho-san answered with a small laugh.
――Wait… he used to be in the Self-Defense Forces?
That was what I thought.
