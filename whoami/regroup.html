Regroup

I listened carefully.
Several people were crying.
A man and a woman.
"Why is this happening to us...?"
—That voice... one of those two women in their twenties, the one who'd been with the woman who kept talking about some actor she liked during the movie.
"Misaki..."
—Misaki. That was the guy from the pair who had planned to camp in a tent. The one who always seemed to be getting pushed around by his girlfriend.
Both of those women and that guy had fled the dining hall relatively early, when the transformations first began.
They were probably okay.
Still human.
There were still people alive.
The moment I realized that, something inside my chest felt a little lighter.
But then the other young woman shouted.
"It's pitch black outside, and there's a storm! Where exactly are we supposed to run?"
There was something beneath her words.
Suspicion.
The desperate uncertainty of not knowing who to trust anymore.
—That was the one who'd been talking about her favorite actor during the movie.
Tsuruoka-san glanced back at me.
"...Sounds like they're arguing out there."
"Can't really blame them. Not after everything that's happened..."
The voices from the corridor kept growing louder.
"If those things can imitate people, what's stopping them from getting to the second floor too?... You really are Saya... right?"
"What are you talking about!? We've been together this whole time!"
—Were those two women actually starting to suspect each other now...?
A cold bead of sweat ran down my back.
They can imitate humans—

"…Hey, those people... they're still human, right?"
Suzuki-san blurted out the question before he could stop himself, and Morishita-san turned toward him.
"Sho, you mean the people outside?"
"Probably. In the second-floor corridor. Just on the other side of the wall," Sho Suzuki-san replied.
—It felt strangely disappointing hearing Morishita-san say "Sho" and knowing she wasn't talking about me.
...I wonder if Mari-senpai is okay.
Where are Mari-senpai and the others now?
I couldn't help but wonder.
Beyond the wall, separated from us by only a thin barrier, someone was quietly sobbing.
"Let's all try to stay calm," a voice said from outside.
It was Kobayashi Ren-san.
"Ah... that's Ren-san's voice."
Suzuki-san spoke almost nostalgically, like someone who had finally found a person he'd been separated from for a very long time.

"…Ren-san," Suzuki-san called out toward the people outside.
"What was that!?"
"Someone spoke from in there!" a startled voice shouted back.
My heart skipped a beat.
Run?
No—it was too late for that now.
"Ren-san, it's me. Sho."
Suzuki-san raised his voice toward the people outside.
For a moment, there was silence.
Then Ren-san's low voice came back.
"Sho? Is that you? What the hell are you doing in there?"
"We were hiding in the staff room, but those things apparently keep the memories of the people they copy. The lodge owner got taken and turned into one of them. Then he started trying to lure us out, telling us to come outside and everything..."
"The owner knew where the keys were kept. We figured it was only a matter of time before they got in, so we escaped up into the crawl space."
—A brief silence followed.
　"...You're still human, right?" Ren Kobayashi-san asked.
"Ren-san, if I wasn't human, I wouldn't be running around hiding in a crawl space like this," Sho Suzuki-san replied.
"We'll come out. Just wait there."
As he spoke, Suzuki-san pushed against the edge of a vertical plywood panel beside him from the inside.
MEEEK—CRACK!
The plywood split along its edge with a sharp groan, and the right side bowed outward, allowing light from outside to spill into the crawl space.
A hand from the corridor grabbed the bent edge and helped peel it away with a ripping sound.
"A little more... yes, you've got it!" Ren-san called from outside.
When the panel finally came away, a long rectangular opening appeared.
In the yellow light, I could make out several figures standing in the second-floor corridor.
Green lodge slippers, stamped with the gold words "Hakuba Grand Lodge," were visible at their feet.
Their shadows stretched across the wooden floor behind them.
The light came from incandescent bulbs and shouldn't have been particularly bright.
But after spending so long in the darkness, it felt almost blinding.
I narrowed my eyes.
Ahead of me, Sho Suzuki-san twisted his body and slipped through the opening first.
As Suzuki-san's head emerged through the opening, the corridor light spilled across his face.
"Whoa... that's bright," he muttered, squinting as he placed a hand on the floor.
As Suzuki-san kicked off from the beam, a cloud of dust burst into the air from the crawl space.
"Cough, cough..."
Behind him, Morishita-san started coughing.
"I made it out..." Suzuki-san breathed, and Morishita Aya-san followed after him, coughing as she came.
She skillfully worked her slender body along the beam and reached an arm through the opening.
I saw Ren Kobayashi-san crouch down and take her hand from the corridor.
"Easy. Don't rush."
Morishita-san was helped up into the corridor and turned back, brushing dust from her clothes.
Next came Uchida-san.
"It's easier if you hold your breath," she said in a voice that sounded as though she were reminding herself as much as anyone else, before slipping through the narrow opening.
Then came Tsuruoka-san.
Bracing himself against the beam with his thick arms, he groaned, "Ngh... this is tight..." as he slowly forced his way through.
As he squeezed through the gap, I couldn't help noticing part of his backside flatten against the upper edge of the opening.
I instinctively pulled back a little and frowned.
—Yeah... I'd rather not go through that exact spot.
The air in the crawl space was still damp, carrying a faint sour odor.
Taking a deep breath, I reached for a different section of the beam with my right hand.
"...All right. Here goes."
Tensing my arms and stomach muscles, I shifted my body, carefully trying to avoid the exact place Tsuruoka-san had just passed through.
Hooking a knee over the beam, I slid myself toward the opening.
My back scraped against the upper edge, and my clothes snagged for a moment.
Dust fell across my face, making me squint.
Finally, my head emerged into the corridor.
Ren Kobayashi-san immediately reached out a hand.
"This way!"
Grabbing it, I hauled myself out and practically tumbled into the second-floor corridor.
The corridor was bathed in yellow light.
Under normal circumstances it probably would have felt dim, but compared to the crawl space it seemed bright and spacious.
My eyes still hadn't adjusted, and I squinted against the light.
As I stood up, my gaze took in the entire room spread out before me on the second floor.
Wooden walls.
Wooden floors.
Exposed wooden rafters overhead.
On either side of the corridor, the floor rose to form large tatami-covered sleeping areas where dozens of people could sleep side by side.
At the head of each sleeping space stood orderly rows of shelves and partitions where backpacks could be stored. Beneath them were individual sleeping areas, each prepared with a folded futon, a blanket, and a pillow.
Above them, long rectangular clerestory windows ran at regular intervals beneath the roofline.
There was no ceiling. The underside of the roof lay completely exposed, and the beams and support posts cast dark shadows across the room.
This was the far end of the communal sleeping area where, earlier, the athletic guy and the guy with glasses and jeans had been lying down when we were heading for our rooms.
But now the athletic guy was gone.
One of those things had gotten him.
As I rose to my feet, I saw several figures staring back at us, their eyes filled with a mixture of relief and suspicion.
Among them stood Kobayashi Ren-san.
His face was drenched in sweat as he looked at Suzuki Sho-san.
"Is it really... you, Sho?"
"Yeah. It's me. Look—I’m human. I’m really human."
Even Suzuki-san's voice trembled slightly.
For a moment, Kobayashi-san hesitated, then slowly held out his hand.
But before Suzuki-san could take it, the man in glasses and jeans cut in from behind.
"H-Hey, hold on a second..."
He raised a hand as if to block them.
"Who says 'I'm human' about themselves like that?"
The atmosphere froze instantly.
I felt my throat tighten.
"Please calm down. We escaped from the staff room. Back there, the lodge owner... wasn't human anymore," Suzuki-san said.
"That... that thing... Even after it takes a human form, it keeps the person's memories..." Morishita Aya-san said, her voice trembling.
"Huh? It keeps their memories? What the hell does that mean? Then you guys could be—"
One of the women in her twenties took a step backward.
"Weren't you locked in the storage room because you were one of those monsters?"
Her finger pointed at me, trembling.
The other woman quickly grabbed her hand.
"Stop it! What good is saying something like that going to do?"
But the man in glasses and jeans muttered quietly.
"If they're human... they'll bleed."
He raised the beam of his flashlight toward us.
Our pupils. Our skin color. Our breathing.
Like a doctor performing an examination.
"Please stop."
Uchida-san, the doctor, stepped forward.
"Doing this now will only create more confusion."
But the man in glasses and jeans spoke up.
"No... we have to make sure.
Those things look human."
Silence.
In that moment, a powerful gust slammed against the wall, and the entire building seemed to shudder.
　 "...Then what would convince you we're human?" I asked.
"Show us your bodies."
The man in glasses and jeans muttered the words.
"Take your clothes off. If you can't do that, that's suspicious all by itself."
No one moved.
Suzuki-san clenched his fists.
Tsuruoka-san's face stiffened.
Morishita Aya-san's shoulders trembled.
Kobayashi Ren-san spoke up.
"Stop it... Right now, getting away from here is more important."
Then, as if crushing the silence beneath the force of his voice, Tsuruoka-san suddenly shouted.
"Well then, maybe you're the monsters! If you're gonna say that, why don't you strip down and show us too!"
—Tsuruoka-san... Yeah, you'd have a problem with that, wouldn't you...? Your underwear's probably brown by now...
I thought.
The silence grew even heavier.
The man in glasses and jeans tensed.
No one moved.
"Hey, staff member!"
The man in glasses and jeans turned to Kobayashi Ren-san and raised his voice.
"Can you really trust them? What happens if even one more person gets taken out?"
As I listened, a thought crossed my mind.
—Everyone here has already lost the means to trust another human being.

The tension in the corridor remained unbroken, and for a moment no one spoke.
I took a deep breath and slowly looked around at everyone.
"Are you really staying together with people you think aren't human?"
Several uneasy gazes turned toward me.
"You're all together because you have some reason to believe the others are human, right? Because you didn't actually see them get taken, or because you have some kind of proof."
Pausing between sentences, I began describing what I had witnessed from the crawl space.
"I saw everything that happened in the dining hall from above."
Someone drew in a sharp breath.
"That man, and him."
I pointed toward the man in glasses and jeans, then toward the man from the female-led couple.
"Those two were near the exit by the entrance and got out first. They're fine."
Then I turned toward the two women in their twenties.
"Those two women were sitting near the dining hall windows. When the three young people from outside turned into monsters, they were far enough away to escape early. I think they're fine too."
After a brief pause, I looked at Kobayashi Ren-san.
"Kobayashi-san, you escaped together with Suzuki-san, didn't you?"
Kobayashi Ren-san nodded firmly.
"Ren-san, you made it out safely too, right?" Suzuki Sho-san said.
—What did he mean by that? I wondered. Had they gotten separated while escaping?
"...Which is why I think everyone here is human," I said, then continued.
"The people who were taken in the dining hall were the lodge owner. Then the person who threatened me with a knife and locked me in the storage room. And Takamura-san, who tried to help Naoki-san..."
I looked toward Uchida-san.
"Uchida-san, you saw Takamura-san get taken right in front of you, didn't you?"
Uchida-san's face turned pale as she quietly nodded.
"And the others who were taken... I'm sorry. That person, and the one who was with them. Then the elderly man who came from outside, and the man with the child..."
"And it seems one or perhaps several more people were taken, but I couldn't see that part from where I was."
I let out a small breath.
"So... I believe everyone here is human."
"So that's it... If he wasn't human, he wouldn't have given us all that information." After looking at me, Ren Kobayashi glanced around at the others and spoke. The others seemed convinced as well and finally fell silent.
In the silence, someone suddenly let out a small laugh.
"...You guys are all covered in soot."
For a moment, nobody reacted.
Then quiet chuckles began to spread through the corridor.
The tension eased, if only a little.
I looked down at myself. My T-shirt, shorts, even my knees were stained black with dust.
Dust looked gray when you saw it floating in the air, but somehow it always seemed to turn black once it stuck to your clothes.
Sho Suzuki-san, Aya Morishita-san, Dr. Uchida-san, and even Tsuruoka-san all began patting at their clothes, trying to shake the dust loose.
I joined them.
Clouds of dust rose from our bodies and drifted through the yellow light overhead like smoke.
"*Cough, cough*... Hey, don't start shaking dust off in here!" said the woman whose friend had been talking about some actor she liked during the movie, waving a hand in front of her face.
"Oh, sorry..." Dr. Uchida-san apologized.
"Sorry," Aya-san added as well.
Right... she's got a point.
I stopped brushing at my clothes.
The others stopped too.
Meanwhile, the man from the female-led couple was still crouched on the floor, his shoulders trembling.
I couldn't help feeling sorry for him.
No matter how bossy she had been toward him, he must have genuinely cared about her.
—Ugh... My throat feels scratchy from all this dust, and my nose won't stop itching...
It felt as though my entire body had been filled with dust.
I pulled a tissue from my pocket, cleared the phlegm from the back of my throat, and spat it into the tissue. Black streaks mixed with the saliva.
When I folded it and blew my nose, the white tissue came away almost black.
The dust from the crawl space had worked its way deep into my lungs.
"Doesn't it kind of smell like poop in here?" said the woman who'd been talking about some actor she liked during the movie.
I knew exactly what the cause was, but there was no way I could say anything.
Sho Suzuki-san, Dr. Uchida-san, and Aya Morishita-san seemed to feel the same way and remained silent.
Naturally, the person responsible wasn't about to say anything either.
If anyone saw his pants, they'd figure it out immediately...
At that moment, Sho Suzuki-san looked up and spoke.
"I just thought of something because of what Sho said. If we can find the guest registry, we could cross off the people who were taken. That might help us figure out who's still human."
The atmosphere immediately grew tense again.
"Sho? Why are you talking about yourself in the third person?" Ren Kobayashi-san asked.
"Hahaha, Ren-san, this guy's name is Sho too." Sho Suzuki-san pointed at me.
"Oh... is that so?" Kobayashi-san said.
I spoke up.
"The guest registry... where is it?"
"At the front desk. Right next to the dining hall," Sho Suzuki-san replied.
"It's not directly connected, but it's just beside it. I'll go get it."
"Is it really safe to go alone?"
"Don't worry. I'll make sure I don't get spotted."
Sho Suzuki-san smiled quietly.
"If I do get caught, I'll yell as loud as I can. Something like, 'I'm getting taken, so I'm not human anymore!'"
Everyone laughed, though it sounded forced.
"I'll be back in five minutes. If I'm not back by then..."
He paused briefly.
"...work together and survive."
With that, Sho Suzuki-san turned and walked straight down the corridor.
He reached the far end of the dormitory room and descended the stairs without looking back.
No one spoke until his figure disappeared into the darkness.

The sound of Sho Suzuki-san's footsteps gradually faded as he descended the stairs.
When the sound finally disappeared completely, the atmosphere in the corridor suddenly grew heavy.
No one spoke.
Only the faint creaking of the building echoed as the night wind battered it from outside.
"...He's really going to be okay, right?"
Aya Morishita-san murmured quietly.
Her voice seemed unusually loud in the silence, and everyone's eyes turned toward her at once.
"He'll be fine. Sho-san thinks fast, and he stayed calm back there."
Ren Kobayashi-san said.
But there was a tremor in his voice.
It sounded more like something he was saying to reassure himself.
"...Five minutes feels like forever."
Tsuruoka-san muttered.
He glanced at his wristwatch, frowning deeply as if the hands were moving slower than they should.
"Wouldn't it be better if we stayed quiet?"
One of the young women said.
"If we make noise... it might come here."
Her voice trembled, and her shoulders shook slightly.
Leaning against a support post, Ren Kobayashi-san kept his eyes fixed on the staircase.
There was determination in his expression, but anxiety lingered beneath it.
"...That guy's really coming back, right?"

At that moment—
Something creaked in the far end of the corridor.
Everyone froze at once.
One of the girls sharply drew in her breath.
"...Just the wind, right?" Tsuruoka-san said.

A cold gust blew through the slightly open emergency-exit window, stirring the dust in the corridor.
The green slippers bearing the name "Hakuba Grand Lodge" swayed gently in the light.
It felt as though time itself had stopped.
Even so, someone quietly murmured,
"...It's been at least three minutes already, hasn't it?"
No one replied.
Everyone was listening.
Listening for the sound of something moving on the floor below.
