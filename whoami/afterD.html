After Dinner

Takizawa-san slid open the dining hall door with a rattling sound, and we followed him out into the corridor.
Across from the dining hall was a room for staff, its wood-grain door bearing a small sign that read “STAFF ROOM” and “Employees Only” in both English and Japanese.
The corridor lights reflected softly on the doors, and the quiet row of them gave off a strangely orderly tension.
“So then, what do we do next?” Takizawa-san asked as we walked.
Tsuruoka-san and Mari-senpai both nodded thoughtfully. “Maybe we head back to the room and figure things out…”
Yohei and Yabe-san nodded in agreement, and I added, “Yeah, let’s go back to the room for now.”
“Alright then, back to the room it is,” Takizawa-san said, heading toward the junction where the entrance, stairs, lounge, and old building split off. We followed after him.
On the left wall, there was another wood-grain door with a sign reading “Office” and “STAFF ONLY.”
To the right led back to the lodge entrance; to the left, the stairs and washroom area.
Straight ahead led to the lounge and the old building.
As we reached that junction, I noticed two women standing in front of the reception desk, looking slightly troubled.
One was slender, with good posture and an elegant, oval-shaped face—she had an air reminiscent of Wakako Sakai.
The other was petite and softly rounded, giving off a gentle, cute impression.
Ah… the two from Hakubajiri—the ones from Gifu who were planning to camp, I thought.
“Excuse me… is it still possible to stay here?” the slender one asked, her voice slightly tense, addressing the receptionist—the guy with the same name as me who had complained to me earlier.
“We heard a typhoon might be coming… the wind’s getting strong, and our tent might collapse…”
The petite one nodded quietly, arms folded as she looked down.
The receptionist smiled warmly, completely different from how he’d spoken to me earlier.
“You’ll be fine. People who planned to camp can switch to staying here.” He gestured reassuringly with his hand.
Wow… he talks to women very differently than he talked to me, I thought.
“Oh—” Yabe-san noticed them and spoke up.
At her voice, the two women turned, glanced at each other, and their faces broke into relieved smiles.
“Oh, you’re the people we met at Hakubajiri,” the slender woman said softly.
The petite one’s eyes lit up as well.
“We figured we’d stay here since the typhoon’s coming,” the Wakako Sakai lookalike said, turning toward Mari-senpai and Yabe-san.
“Yeah… with the storm and all… maybe we can talk later,” Mari-senpai replied.
Of course, Tsuruoka-san couldn’t stay quiet.
“Oh! We meet again! Switching from tent to lodge tonight, huh? Makes sense—gotta take shelter, it’s a storm, a real storm!”
The two women’s expressions shifted instantly to confusion, completely different from how they had responded to Mari-senpai and Yabe-san.
“But don’t worry! This lodge is steel-framed—won’t lose to lightning! It’s even got lightning rods, so you’re totally safe even if it starts rumbling!” Tsuruoka-san said, pointing dramatically toward the ceiling.
This place is clearly wooden… and it’s not even his lodge, I thought.
“Th-thank you…” the Sakai lookalike managed, forcing a trembling smile.
“And not just safe—you can relax and eat curry while watching the storm! Bon Curry! That nostalgic taste!” Tsuruoka-san beamed.
Hey… that was my line… I thought. And he said it like my comment wasn’t useful at all…
“Come on, you’ve gotta enjoy the thunder too!” he continued.
The petite woman laughed politely. “Oh… yes, thank you…”
“You shouldn’t encourage him too much,” Yabe-san said flatly. “He gets carried away.”
The two women looked at her with relief and smiled.
“Honestly… Tsuruoka-san always gets like this…” Mari-senpai muttered, frowning slightly.
“Well then! When the thunder strikes, just count along with my voice—one, two… THUNDER!”
Tsuruoka-san spread his arms dramatically, striking a playful pose toward the women.
“Ah… please stop…” Mari-senpai muttered, covering her face with both hands.
“Sorry—he’s completely off,” Yabe-san added expressionlessly.
The two women exchanged glances again, visibly reassured, and smiled.
“Well, hey, if it lightens the mood, that’s not so bad,” Takizawa-san said with a grin, half in defense.
The two women chuckled softly, their expressions easing a little.
Tsuruoka-san puffed out his chest proudly. “See? Everyone’s smiling!”
They’re not smiling because of you, I thought. They’re smiling because Mari-senpai and Yabe-san are here.
“Well then, see you later,” Mari-senpai said to the two women.
They bowed lightly to her and Yabe-san, then turned back toward the reception desk.
“Alright, let’s head back,” Mari-senpai said, and we started walking again, turning away from the reception toward the stairs and washroom area.
