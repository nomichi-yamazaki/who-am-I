Monstrous Object

Left of me, the fire hose that Ren Kobayashi-san had gathered lay scattered across the floor, the obstacles disrupting my desperate sprint.
In the center of the room stood the electric spear Suzuki-san and I had built together, along with the generator mounted on a handcart.
I glanced at them for only a moment, but I had no time to stop.
Straight ahead were the entrance doors.
Inside the lodge, toward the back on the right, stood the reception counter.
In front of it was the small shop, and just before that was a four-way intersection of hallways.
Turning right would lead to the dining hall and the staff room.
Turning left would lead to the lounge and the cooking area.
I made it as far as the intersection.
CRASH! CRASH! CRASH!
CLANG! CLANG-CLANG!
Suddenly, a deafening roar erupted from the direction of the dining hall on my right, loud enough to drown out even the storm battering the roof and walls.
My heart slammed against my ribs.
A violent shudder ran through me, as though fear itself had twisted my body like a wrung-out rag.
Instinctively, I turned toward the sound.
What I saw was impossible.
The dining hall's glass windows and doors exploded outward.
Slowly emerging through the wreckage came a gigantic mass of flesh—an obscene mound of living meat.
Embedded across its surface were human faces.
The lodge owner—the man who had enthusiastically debated Bon curry with us.
The playful college guy from the trio who had laughed through the "I Can Do It" skit.
Takamura-san, who had lost Akane-san in the washroom yet still tried to save Naoki-kun.
Their faces protruded from the pulsating flesh as though they had been carelessly pressed into it.
From the mass extended countless limbs and tentacles.
Some resembled human arms or legs.
Others were red or green.
Some were covered in warts.
Some were covered in patches of coarse, curly hair.
Others were ringed with earthworm-like segments.
They writhed endlessly, twisting over one another in grotesque waves.
"Aaahhh..."
"Aahhh..."
"H-help... me..."
The faces moaned in overlapping voices—male and female, young and old—blending into a chorus of suffering.
It was not merely a monster.
It was a living nightmare.
The aberrant mass moved slowly forward, crawling across the floor on its countless legs and tentacles as though it intended to claim every corner of the lodge for itself.
My breathing grew ragged.
My heartbeat thundered in my ears.
—The lodge owner who had laughed with us over Bon Curry...
—The playful college guy from the "I Can Do It" skit...
—Takamura-san, who had lost Akane-san and still tried to save Naoki-kun...
And now this thing—a mass containing them, and perhaps others I couldn't even identify—was coming for me.
I was alone.
I had to somehow survive this by myself.
Outside was nothing but darkness.
A storm raged beyond the walls, rain and wind battering the mountain relentlessly.
And we were nearly three thousand meters above sea level.
There was nowhere to run.
Once again, time seemed to slow.
Each second stretched and elongated until it felt like hours.
The moment I looked at the thing advancing toward me, my legs nearly gave out.
I wanted to run—
but it wasn't something I could outrun.
A crushing pressure settled against my back like an invisible wall, slowly squeezing the air from my lungs.
*(Even if I run... it'll catch me.)*
The last fragments of calm remaining inside me flickered weakly through my trembling thoughts.
*"...Damn it..."*
I didn't even have time to hesitate.
Cold terror crawled up my spine as I was forced to decide what to do next.
At arm's reach lay one of the electric spears.
In front of me, the grotesque mass dragged itself across the floor, slowly drawing nearer.
(If I don't stop it here... I'm dead.)
A low breath escaped my throat—so faint I wasn't even sure it had come from me.
I was terrified.
Of course I was terrified.
Yet despite the fear that made my knees tremble, my fingers were already reaching for the spear's grip.
My body moved before my mind could catch up.
It wasn't courage.
It was nothing more than the instinct to survive.
(I have no choice...)
I lunged for the electric spear and grabbed the black plastic T-shaped starter handle on the generator sitting atop the cart.
Steadying my breath, I yanked the cord.
Brrr—
The engine sputtered but failed to catch.
Hurry hurry hurry hurry! If you don't hurry, it'll get you!
I pulled again with all my strength.
Brrr...
Still nothing.
"It hurts..."
"Help me..."
"Aaaah..."
The writhing mass continued its relentless approach.
Amid the storm battering the lodge from outside, I could hear the wet, sticky sounds of the Thing moving closer and closer.
Hurry hurry hurry hurry! If you don't hurry, you're finished!
You'll disappear from this world!
Panic and terror crashed together inside my head as I gathered every last ounce of strength and gave the cord one final pull.
Tatatatata—!
At last the generator roared to life, its mechanical growl echoing through the lodge.
Without hesitation, I hurled one of the electric spears toward the enormous mass of flesh advancing on me, covered in countless limbs and tentacles.
"Gyaaaah!"
"Kyaaaah!"
"Aaaah!"
"Uwaaaah!"
Men screamed.
Women shrieked.
The spear drove deep into the abomination.
Dark crimson fluid burst from the wound, and the creature let out a chorus of agonized cries.
Then, in the very next instant, it seemed to explode.
From every part of its surface, countless slick, glistening tentacles erupted outward, writhing and thrashing independently like living things with minds of their own.
But one spear wasn't enough.
The creature wouldn't be electrocuted unless a second spear struck as well.
Oh no!
If one of those tentacles latches onto me, I'm finished!
Acting on pure instinct, I spun around and slammed my hand against the fire alarm switch mounted beside the hydrant.
Jiriririri—!
The emergency bell shrieked throughout the lodge.
At the same time, the fire pump inside the engine room—the very room where Suzuki Sho-san and I had built the electric spears—should have begun roaring to life.
I hurriedly twisted the hydrant valve open.
The hose lying in a tangled heap across the floor suddenly swelled near its base as water pressure surged into it.
The bulge raced through the hose toward the nozzle.
Then, with explosive force, water burst out.
It splashed across the floor, spreading into a widening pool.
The water reached the second electric spear lying nearby and gradually engulfed it.
Then it continued flowing toward the grotesque mass.
At that moment—
The spear embedded in the creature, the creature itself, the spreading water, and the second spear lying in the puddle became part of a single circuit.
Jagged blue-white arcs of electricity raced wildly across the creature's surface.
"GYAAAH!"
"KYAAAAH!"
"AAAH!"
"UUGH!"
Men screamed.
Women shrieked.
The abomination let out another chorus of agonized cries.
Like a startled snail withdrawing its eyestalks, it suddenly retracted the countless tentacles it had been reaching toward me.
The mass itself contracted, stiffened, and twisted awkwardly upon itself.
In that instant, a chill shot through my body.
The pounding of my heart echoed in my ears.
For a fleeting moment, terror and the desperate instinct to survive seized complete control of me.
The old building... that's my only chance!
While the Thing writhed in agony, I took advantage of the opening and sprinted toward the intersection, struggling to steady my breathing as I ran.
Jiriririri—!
The shrill fire alarm continued screaming throughout the lodge.
The fire hose had reared up like a cobra, thrashing wildly from side to side as it spewed torrents of water across the floor.
Straight ahead lay the entrance.
To the right were the dining hall and the employee room.
To the left were the lounge and the cooking area.
When I reached the intersection, I turned left.

The corridor trembled beneath my feet.
Behind me, something massive seemed to crash forward, as though it were bearing down through the walls themselves.
I didn't look back.
If I looked back, my legs would stop moving.
Jiriririri—!
The fire alarm continued its relentless shrieking.
The two lounge rooms flashed past at the edge of my vision.
The Japanese-style room on the left.
The Western-style room on the right.
Neither entrance had a door.
Beyond the latticework lay only darkness.
Anyone—or anything—could have been hiding there.
If something jumps out, I'm finished.
Even while running so hard that my breath seemed to stick in my throat, I swept my gaze across each entrance in an instant, making sure nothing lurked inside as I charged straight down the center of the corridor.
Farther ahead, on my left, stood a wooden sliding door.
A sign reading Storage hung beside it, secured with a padlock.
Whatever was inside, I didn't have time to find out.
On my right was the cooking area, its windows fitted with frosted glass.
It was the same place where the three mutated men had performed their I Can Do It comedy routine.
The same place where the child taken by the Thing had proudly declared, “Aye aye! Chef coming through!”
There was no time to check inside.
I could feel the Thing chasing me.
At any moment, it felt as though a tentacle might shoot out from behind and seize me.
I dragged a desperate breath into my lungs, so hard it felt as though they might tear apart, and forced my legs to run even faster.
The sliding door at the end of the corridor came into view.
A weathered wooden plaque hung beside it, the black-painted characters reading: OLD WING.
*"…I have no choice!!"*
I lunged toward the lattice-glass door that looked like the entrance to a small cabin—
SLIDE!!
The door shot open.
Without slowing down, I threw myself through the passage leading into the Old Wing.

Jiririririri...
The fire alarm continued to shriek throughout the lodge.
Above me stretched a corrugated metal roof, slanting downward to the right beneath exposed rafters.
Wooden plank walls lined both sides of the passage.
Barefoot, I raced down the wooden ramp leading toward the Old Wing.
SHHHHH...
The simple covered walkway amplified the sound of the storm outside.
ROOOAR! CREAK!
The powerful wind made the walkway itself bend and shudder.
Behind me came a sickening vibration, like flesh and bone colliding against one another.
If I looked back, it was over.
Breathing hard, I shoved open the double doors of the Old Wing and threw myself inside without hesitation.
The instant I slipped through, the doors began slowly swinging shut on their own.
*I can block them with that timber!*
The idea struck me instantly.
I spun around and traced the structure of the doors with my eyes.
On the inside of each door, from waist height to chest height, several narrow metal brackets had been mounted. Each was bent into a U-shape, with the open side facing upward.
I glanced toward the wall.
A square wooden beam was leaning there.
*If I set that across the brackets, I can barricade the doors from the inside.*
My body moved before I could think.
I grabbed the beam and ran toward the center of the doors, ignoring the strain shooting through my wrists from its weight.
As the doors continued closing, I carefully lined up the brackets and heaved the beam into place.
*"—Hngh!"*
The beam dropped into the U-shaped brackets.
With a heavy THUD, the doors were forced back and locked firmly in place.
*...I made it.*
Fighting the weakness threatening to buckle my knees, I slowly backed away from the entrance.
My legs were shaking so badly that, despite the floor being perfectly flat, I lost my balance and fell backward onto the floor.
The impacts echoing through the doors were a constant reminder that nowhere here was truly safe.

Jiriririri...
The fire alarm continued to scream through the lodge.
SHHHHH...
Layered beneath it was the relentless roar of torrential rain.
BANG!!
Without warning, glass rattled violently and the double doors shuddered.
Two faces were pressed against the lattice windows.
A larger face in the upper left.
A smaller face in the lower right.
Raw.
Blistered.
Warped.
And yet there was no mistaking them.
They were the father and son who had jokingly proclaimed,
*"Aye aye! Chef coming through!"*
SPLAT...
Something shaped like a grotesque sun slapped itself against the glass, its surface glistening with slime.
At its center was a circular opening.
Within it, something resembling a tongue writhed and licked repeatedly across the windowpane.
Wherever it passed, streaks of pale mucus smeared across the glass, leaving cloudy trails behind.
Around the circular opening, countless short tentacles squirmed and twitched, giving the thing the appearance of a grotesque sunburst.
Behind it loomed a massive mound covered in folds of human skin, mottled with warts and swollen green blisters.
From that living mass protruded numerous appendages—elephant-trunk-like tentacles, human arms, and human legs—all writhing in different directions.
*"Pa... pa..."*
The child's face clinging to the creature opened its mucus-streaked mouth and spoke in a strained, suffering voice.
