Scream

The pounding against the window suddenly stopped, and the roar of the storm reclaimed the world once more.
The sudden silence made my chest jolt.
When I looked toward the window, the pounding hands were already gone.
—What the hell was that just now?
Even when I strained my ears, all I could hear outside was the rain and the wind.
And yet the image from moments ago—Akane-san’s figure, the fists striking the glass, that voice—still clung vividly inside my head, while my heartbeat refused to slow.
No one spoke.
We simply stared at one another, searching for words that would not come.
Only the sound of rain and someone’s shallow breathing filled the room.
Yellow light.
Wooden ceiling.
Wooden walls.
Wooden floor.
Pillars and long rectangular tables lined the spacious dining hall.
The windows along the outer wall reflected the interior clearly—the rows of pillars and tables, and the people gathered around them.
Takamura-san sat collapsed near the window, supported by Dr. Uchida and Mari-senpai on either side.
I could not tell whether all three of them were trembling, or whether Takamura-san’s shaking was simply spreading into the bodies supporting her.
Yohei and Yabe-san stood nearby in stunned silence.
I stood at the corner of a table just behind them, watching.
Across the table from me stood Takizawa-san.
Far off to the right, near the scorched area, Tsuruoka-san remained crouched beside the edge of a table, only the top of his head visible.
Around Takizawa-san and me, spaced unevenly apart, stood the lodge owner, Kobayashi Ren-san, Suzuki Sho-san, and slightly farther back, Morishita Aya-san.
The two women in their twenties who loved those mountain movies, the tall man in his sixties, the athletic man, Glasses-and-Jeans, the child and his father—all of them watched us cautiously from a distance.
Whenever the storm winds slammed against the lodge, the wooden frame creaked softly.
Mishi…
Each groan sounded disturbingly like something crawling along outside, and cold sweat slid down my spine.
“…Wasn’t that really Akane-san just now?” Yohei muttered quietly.
His voice cracked the suffocating tension, if only slightly.
“No. That girl is dead. You saw her head get crushed earlier.”
Kobayashi Ren-san answered sharply, irritation mixed into his voice.
The atmosphere tightened even further.
…Even with Takamura-san sitting right there, did he really have to say it that bluntly?
That was what I found myself thinking.

Movement caught my attention from the right side of the room.
When I turned, I saw Tsuruoka-san staggering toward us.
He clung to the edge of a table for support, swaying unsteadily as he shuffled closer.
“But it looked exactly like Akane-san. A person who ended up like that shouldn’t be able to return looking completely normal…”
Mari-senpai, who had been supporting Takamura-san, slowly stood and turned toward us as she spoke.
Tsuruoka-san finally reached the chair at the far right end of Takizawa-san’s table, pulled it out roughly, and collapsed into it.
“So then… that means…”
Standing beside Mari-senpai, Yabe-san searched for the words.
“…Something else was disguising itself as her?”
“Then what the hell was that thing!? You’re saying it wasn’t human?!”
Tsuruoka-san suddenly burst in, half crying as he threw both arms across the table.
“There’s no way someone could survive after ending up like that. If something returned wearing her original appearance anyway, then the only explanation is that some other entity is merely imitating her form.”
This time, Yabe-san spoke with absolute certainty.
“That’s why I said we mustn’t open the window. That thing wasn’t ‘Akane-san.’ It was only borrowing her appearance.”
Suzuki Sho-san spoke from somewhere behind me.
“Come on… like in some movie or something…? A monster taking human form? That’s insane…”
Yohei let out a weak, exhausted laugh as he said it.
I clenched my trembling hands tightly.
Part of me desperately wanted to cling to Yohei’s words.
That’s insane.
But the image burned into the back of my mind—Akane-san collapsing apart—would not allow it.
“That woman… wasn’t human?”
One of the young women who had been talking excitedly about movies earlier whispered in shock.
“Stop it…”
Her friend answered, voice trembling.
“So you’re telling me that dangerous creature outside took on that woman’s appearance? …That’s absurd…“What is this, The Frog Prince or something…?”
The man in his sixties spoke in disbelief after listening to all of us.
“Then there’s only one possible answer left. That… thing can take human form. What we saw wasn’t Akane-san—it was something pretending to be her.”
Yabe-san spoke as though trying to crush the older man’s words beneath her own.
Even after hearing that, a small part of me still clung desperately to the relief I had felt when I first thought Akane-san had returned alive.
And yet reason continued screaming inside my skull.
My heartbeat quickened painfully, while the sound of blood rushing through my ears grew louder and louder.
—There’s no way Akane-san could still be alive.
…But if that wasn’t Akane-san, then what the hell was it?
The thoughts spun endlessly through my mind.
“So you’re saying that black mass turned itself into that woman?”
The lodge owner finally spoke from the back of the room, and I turned toward him.
…Hadn’t he just heard the man in his sixties say almost the exact same thing?
That thought crossed my mind.
Yabe-san, Kobayashi Ren-san, and Suzuki Sho-san all turned toward the owner and nodded silently.
“Let’s calm down and think this through. That thing can take on human form. At the very least, it became Akane-chan. That much is certain.”
Takizawa-san clenched his fist as he spoke.
…That’s exactly what we’ve already been talking about…
I thought.
A sharp pain twisted through my chest.
Inside my head, the scene I had witnessed earlier flashed over and over again.
—Did that thing “eat” Akane-san…?
Or possess her…?
Grinding my back teeth together, I forced the words out.
“…That’s not Akane-san anymore. I think so too.”
The moment I said it, the room somehow felt even colder.
Everyone’s eyes turned toward me at once.
I endured those stares while trembling deep inside.
—Who will it imitate next?
“So then… Akane-chan really is… dead…? The real one, I mean…”
Yohei muttered quietly.
…If he says something that directly, Takamura-san’s only going to suffer more.
“Yohei.”
I spoke his name softly.
When he looked at me, I signaled toward Takamura-san with my eyes.

“I have a responsibility to protect the climbers staying here. But if that really wasn’t Akane-san… then what are we supposed to do…?”
The lodge owner’s voice sounded strained and exhausted.
No one could answer him.
Everyone fell silent.
“More importantly… what the hell was that thing in the first place?”
At the owner’s words, I felt everyone’s attention shift toward us at once.
“…Sho. You people found something up on the mountain, didn’t you?”
At Suzuki Sho-san’s low voice, I caught my breath.
Standing slightly behind me to the left, Takizawa-san hesitantly began to speak.
“…While we were climbing, we found something shining beneath the snow. At first we couldn’t tell what it was, but when we got closer… it had a metallic texture. The shape curved like an arc, and yet… it looked like some kind of wreckage.”
Maybe he avoided calling it a wheel cover because he thought people would laugh at him.
“Wreckage?”
The voices of the lodge owner and Kobayashi Ren-san overlapped like uneasy murmurs.
“Yes… It was at the bottom of a crevasse. Beneath the great snow valley, deep inside a crack in the snow… that thing was there. Something like a UFO…”
For an instant, the air inside the lodge froze solid.
“And buried nearby in the snow… was that black mass.”
“Aliens…?”
Kobayashi Ren-san muttered under his breath.
“Eehhh!?”
The two women in their twenties cried out together in frightened voices and immediately clung to each other again.
“No way… a UFO?”
“That’s impossible… but you actually saw it, right…?”
From the corner of the room, I could hear the athletic man and Glasses-and-Jeans whispering tensely to each other.
They were trying to keep their voices down, but they could not hide the trembling underneath.
“Can something like that really exist…?”
The man in his sixties muttered blankly.

Silence.
Once again, the storm reclaimed dominion over everything.
Akane-san’s voice was gone now.
So was the pounding against the window.
Outside, countless raindrops continued hammering endlessly against the wooden walls of the lodge.
And yet, when I listened carefully, I thought I heard something buried deep beneath that endless roar.
A man screaming.
It sounded distant.
And yet at the same time, unnervingly close.
But I hadn’t heard it clearly enough to be certain.
Maybe the wind had only sounded like a human scream.
After all, no one else reacted to it.
No one said a word.
“You… brought that thing back here? You people said it was some kind of fossil…”
The lodge owner’s voice trembled faintly.
Takizawa-san nodded.
“We never imagined anything like this would happen. We thought it was… an alien corpse…”
At those words, I heard someone swallow hard in the silence.
My palms felt cold and damp.
—That’s right.
We were the ones who picked it up.
Maybe everything had begun at that very moment.
