Verification of the Hypothesis

A quiet set of footsteps echoed through the lodge.
They weren't hurried,
nor did they hesitate.
They moved slowly,
as if testing the floor with each step.
Yabe-san silently raised a finger to her lips.
"...Shh."
Then, a moment later—
"...Saku-chan... where are you...?"
A hoarse woman's voice.
My throat tightened.
It was a voice I could never forget.
"...Saku-chan... where are you...?"
Drip.
Drip.
A wet sound mingled with the footsteps.
As though something was falling onto the floor
and being dragged along behind it—
the kind of sound
no one should ever have to hear.
The sound of the rain never stopped.
Yet somehow,
it seemed to fade into the distance.
In its place,
the footsteps on the floor
rang out with unnatural clarity.
The wind shook the lodge,
and somewhere within the building,
the timbers let out a low, groaning creak.
The storm that had filled the world only moments ago
now felt as though it had been pushed into the background,
leaving nothing but those footsteps,
standing out in terrifying detail.
The voice and the footsteps
were unmistakably drawing closer.
Yabe-san leaned toward my ear and whispered,
"...She must've really liked you, huh?"
Without lifting my eyes,
I answered quietly.
"...That's not Mari-senpai anymore."
The moment the words left my mouth,
a sharp pain twisted deep inside my chest.
It felt as though, by denying her,
I had thrown away something precious as well.
"Let's hide,"
Yabe-san said shortly, pointing toward the closet.
The two of us moved carefully,
making sure not to make a sound.
Inside, unused futons had been stacked away.
I climbed up,
pressed down the pile of bedding on the upper shelf,
and slipped my body into the narrow space behind it.
The smell of fabric and dust
filled my nose.
Yabe-san took the lower shelf,
sinking herself into the shadows cast by the futons.
We didn't close the sliding doors completely.
Closing them would make noise.
From inside the closet,
the sound of the rain was almost completely gone.
Instead,
the footsteps crossing the hallway
and the faint signs of someone touching the sliding doors
reached us through the layers of bedding.
A storm should have been raging outside,
and yet,
in this place alone,
it felt as though the world itself had fallen silent.
Deep within the silence,
the wind shook the building,
and somewhere, something let out a quiet creak.
Even those sounds
were nothing more than background noise
compared to the footsteps.
Through the futons, I whispered softly.
"...Did you lock the room
after I came in?"
A brief silence.
Then Yabe-san answered.
"...I thought we might have to leave in a hurry.
So... I didn't."
A cold sensation settled deep in my chest.
—Which meant...
—It could come inside.
The footsteps drew one step closer.
Shaa...
Somewhere a little farther away,
I heard the sound of a sliding door opening.
"...You're not here either..."
Her voice dissolved into the hallway.
"...Saku-chan... where are you...?"
"...Please... don't leave me behind..."
The sound of another sliding door opening.
Footsteps.
Drip.
Drip.
Little by little,
they came closer.
Relentlessly.
Certainly.
Holding my breath,
I closed my eyes.
—I couldn't save her.
—I left her behind.
The fact that I hadn't reached out my hand back then...
That running away had been my only choice...
All of it
came back to me now,
weighing heavily on my chest.
"...Please... don't leave me... alone..."
That voice overlapped with my memories
of the Mari-senpai I once knew,
quietly,
yet surely,
wearing away at my heart.
Inside the closet,
all I could do
was hold my breath.
—Until the door opened.

Inside the closet,
the silence was suffocating.
The dampness soaked into the futons
and the smell of old cotton stung my nose.
Drip...
Drip...
The footsteps stopped
right in front of the room.
"...Here...?"
The voice was slightly lower
than Mari-senpai's had been.
And yet,
the way she spoke
was unmistakably hers.
Without realizing it,
I was clutching the futon tightly.
I could feel my fingertips trembling.
Clatter...
Something touched the sliding door.
—Don't move.
"Saku-chan..."
Her voice fell
from just outside.
"You were here...
just a little while ago..."
Rattle...
Rattle...
The sliding door
shook slowly.
It wasn't locked.
That fact echoed dully
inside my mind.
(It's coming in...)
Shaaah...
The sliding door to the room
slowly opened.
—It's coming in!
My heart pounded in my chest.
Cold sweat ran down my face
as I wondered whether
the thing wearing Mari-senpai's form
could hear it beating.
A chill of cold air
flowed into the room.

It happened in that very moment.
Suddenly,
the footsteps stopped.
And in their place,
I heard something else.
—Breathing.
Right in front of the closet.
Separated from me
by nothing more than a single wooden door,
it was breathing.
"...You're here, aren't you?"
The voice smiled.
"Saku-chan is... here..."
Through the narrow gap,
a pair of cloudy white eyes—
looked directly up
toward the upper shelf where I was hiding.
My throat twitched.
—It can see me.
—It knows.
And yet...
It didn't come in.
It simply kept staring.
Several seconds...
No, perhaps even longer than that,
passed in silence.
"...Mari?"
Yabe-san's voice came from below,
moving out from the closet.
—Yabe-san came out of hiding!
And she was calling out
to the thing that had once been Mari-senpai.
Was she really trying to test her own theory—
that it wouldn't attack
unless we attacked first?
...And if that theory was wrong...
what was she planning to do?

"Yabecchi?"
The voice was exactly as I remembered it—
lighthearted,
the same casual tone she had always used
when calling out to her friends.
"Why were you hiding in there?"
"Oh, nothing much...
I just thought someone who isn't one of us yet
might still show up."
A brief silence followed.
I held my breath.
Behind Yabe-san's back,
I imagined what was standing out in the hallway.
—She's going to attack.
That's what I thought.
But...
"I see..."
Mari-senpai's voice softened,
almost sounding relieved.
Yabe-san took a step forward.
She didn't run.
She didn't brace herself.
She simply stood there,
as naturally as ever.
"Have you seen Saku-chan?"
"No. I haven't."
Her voice trembled ever so slightly.
Even so, she continued.
"Maybe he's downstairs?
...Takizawa-san was looking for you down there earlier, too."
Another silence.
The pounding of my heart
grew louder and louder.
"I see..."
Drip...
The footsteps moved a little farther away.
"Then maybe I'll go take a look...
If you happen to see Saku-chan,
tell him I'm here, okay?"
"...Okay."
That was the last thing she said.
The footsteps gradually disappeared
deeper into the hallway.
The sliding door
closed quietly.
For a long while,
neither of us moved.
Yabe-san
slowly let out a breath.
"..."
She said nothing.
She simply stood there.
—She didn't attack.
There was no violence.
No transformation.
Nothing.
At least for now,
our hypothesis
had been correct.
But at the same time,
the implications of that realization
sent a chill down my spine.
If they don't attack
unless they're attacked first...
Then what happens...
when a reason to attack finally appears?
Yabe-san
still hadn't turned around.
And somehow,
her back looked just a little smaller
than it had before.
