嵐の中

After escaping from the underground storage room, I began lifting the rocks scattered nearby and piling them on top of the hatch, one after another, making sure nothing could climb out through the opening.
The wooden hatch would lift slightly every now and then, as though something beneath it was trying to push it open. Each time it did, the damp wood groaned under the strain.
Breathing hard, I added more weight.
My clothes clung heavily to my body, soaked through by the rain. Mud made my hands slippery.
Still, I kept stacking rocks.
Eventually, the wooden hatch stopped moving altogether.
Even the slightest give disappeared.
From directly below came shrill squeals and the sound of something striking the wood.
The noise seemed to stab at my spine more than my ears.
But I didn't look back.
As rain and wind lashed against me, I started moving forward with the building on my left.
The soaked T-shirt plastered to my body flapped and danced wildly in the storm.
Far off in both directions, I could see faint yellow lights.
But where I stood was swallowed completely by darkness, and every light was impossibly distant.
My feet were bare.
Each time a loose stone dug into the sole of my foot, the pain was sharp.
Yet at the same time, it was the only thing reminding me that I was still here.
To my left, through the rain-shrouded darkness, I could make out a long shape covered by a plastic tarp.
The sheet had been weighed down with rocks and trembled violently beneath the rain.
Long.
Layered.
Stacked.
*Probably construction lumber,* I guessed.
The wind sliced past my ears.
It tugged at my T-shirt, as if trying to tear it away.
The rain hammered down from above, pressing against me as though it wanted to crush me into the ground.
Water streamed down both my legs like waterfalls.
*Well... maybe it'll wash away what I did to myself back there.*
The thought came with a bitter trace of self-mockery.
Behind me, faintly, I could still hear something pounding against the hatch beneath the ground.
I found myself thinking:
***For now... let's just make it over there.***
Carefully easing my feet forward to lessen the pain from the uneven stones, I continued on.
Gripping the flapping plastic sheet beside me, little by little.
The wind felt strong enough to rip me away.
Without something to hold onto, I couldn't have moved forward at all.
The sounds from the underground hatch behind me had grown distant.
Or perhaps they had simply become lost beneath the storm.
My mind and body felt as though they had been stretched tight for so long that parts of them had gone numb.
So I walked.
Just forward.
The building remained on my left.
Darkness stretched endlessly to my right.
The distant yellow lights didn't seem any closer than before.
*"If I keep going, I'll reach them eventually."*
There was no evidence to support that belief.
Nothing at all.
And yet my feet continued moving.
Because I felt that if I stopped—
I might simply collapse.
I couldn't even tell whether my ragged breathing came from exhaustion or from the cold.
All I knew was that I kept walking.

The rain hammering against the roof continued without pause, loud enough to split my ears.
Above, the wind howled across the sky like some gigantic beast, its deep, rumbling roar rolling through the darkness.
Batatatatata...
The plastic sheets covering the stacked construction materials beside me snapped violently in the gale. Rain slammed against them, sending sprays of water back into my face.
The rain pounded down as though trying to crush my skull, while the wind twisted at my body with enough force to wrench me off balance.
Hunching over, clinging to the plastic sheeting for support, I forced myself forward one step at a time—literally crawling my way ahead.
Everyone's been taken...
Uchida-san, the doctor...
Morishita Aya-san, who had told us she was studying environmental science...
And Takizawa-san, the man who had brought all of us here...
Takizawa-san's GranAce was probably still sitting in the Sarukura parking lot.
Its owner would never climb into it again.
They'd been right there in front of me.
Maybe I could have saved them—
But I hadn't done anything.
Not one thing.
If Mari-senpai was still alive...
If she was suffering somewhere...
If she was waiting for someone to help her...
Then I—at the very least—I had to save her.
Where should I go...?
Where could I possibly find Mari-senpai?
The first floor was already a nest of monsters.
The old building had been overrun as well.
There wasn't a single safe place left anymore.
The second floor...
The man in his sixties—the one who had fallen from the attic beams, threatened me with a knife, and locked me inside the storage room—had gone downstairs with the glasses-and-jeans guy and one of the women in her twenties—the one who kept talking about that actor—attached to his head.
Now that I thought about it, the people who had become those things seemed to gravitate toward the first floor.
If that was true, then maybe—just maybe—the second floor was still safe.
Maybe Mari-senpai was hiding somewhere up there too.
For now, I should follow that possibility and head to the second floor.
After all, the first floor was crawling with more than twenty monsters already.
That was what I decided.

My entire body was soaked through by the rain.
Water streamed over my skin like a waterfall, soaking not only my clothes but every inch of me, and my body trembled uncontrollably.
It wasn't just fear anymore.
The cold was draining away my body heat.
The soles of my bare feet throbbed from constantly stepping on sharp stones scattered across the ground.
Above the plastic sheeting on my left stood a window boarded shut with wooden planks.
Any light that should have leaked through had long since lost its color, leaving only a faint gray outline.
It belonged to what should have been the empty dining hall.
Inside, Uchida-san and Morishita-san had been taken.
The room had been overflowing with the transformed victims.
Keeping it in the corner of my eye, I forced myself onward.
I was getting close to the covered walkway connecting the old building and the new one.
To my left, the building stepped back, forming an alcove-like space filled with twisted sheets of metal, scrap lumber, broken pottery, and discarded ceramic toilets.
It looked like a dumping ground for things nobody wanted anymore.
Somewhere deep within that darkness, something shifted with a faint creeeak as the wind caught it.
My heart nearly jumped out of my chest.
Then, ahead of me—
A yellow glow spilled faintly into the storm.
The new building.
The nearest room was a storage room, and beyond it lay the Japanese-style recreation room.
Something was moving within the light spilling from the windows.
A silhouette.
But it wasn't shaped like a human being.
The roaring rain and screaming wind swallowed my footsteps and breathing.
That was my only saving grace.
Rain hammered against my face.
Water ran into my mouth, and I spat it out.
It hurt just to keep my eyes open.
Still, I had to keep moving.
Ahead lay the covered passage connecting the hut to the washroom building.
Beyond that was the walkway running between the hut and the washroom block.
Once I reached it, there would be a corrugated roof overhead.
Shelter from the rain.
Wooden slats covered the floor there.
If I could just make it that far...
Maybe the pain in my feet would ease, if only a little.
Off to my left, water dripped from a roof into a bucket sitting beside a pile of ceramic containers.
Clang...
Clang...
Clang...
Clang...
The small metallic sound repeated over and over with eerie regularity.
Breathing hard, I pushed forward through that world of mingling sounds—
the rain, the wind, the distant creaks, the dripping water—
moving only forward.
Forward.

そっと顔だけを出して様子をうかがう。
さっき自分が身を潜めていた渡り廊下の奥、新館の出口のところ——
橙色の窓明かりの手前で、さきほどの“影”が揺らいだ。
立ち止まったように見えた。
こちらを見ているのかもしれない。
僕は反射的に角の裏へと身を引っ込める。
心臓がどくどくとうるさく鳴り、濡れた髪の先から落ちる雫の音さえ、自分の居場所を暴く足音に思える。
雨に打たれながら建物の壁に身を沿わせ、慎重に進む。
新館の壁は真っ黒に濡れ、まるで巨大な生き物の表皮のように見える。
そのすぐ先、外階段の裏側が闇の中にぼんやりと浮かんでいる。
階段下はさらに暗い。
階段の鉄骨が雨粒を受け、カン、カン、と不規則な金属音を鳴らしている。
風が吹くたびにその音が変わる——まるで誰かが上を歩いているような、不穏でいやらしい響き。
僕は喉を鳴らし、階段の付け根までそっと近づいた。
上るには、一度階段の正面に出てから左に折り返す必要がある。
もうそこまで行けば、二階へ続く道は目の前だ。
だが——。
その折り返しの曲がり角の先で、
ガラガラ……と、何かが石を転がすような音がかすかに聞こえた。
風の音か？
木の枝か？
それとも——ここにいる“何か”の足音か？
雨音の中で聞こえたり聞こえなかったりする曖昧な物音が、
逆に僕の想像をかき立て、恐怖を倍増させた。
僕の喉がひりつく。
——行くしかない。
ここに留まる方が危険だ。
そう自分に言い聞かせ、僕は雨に濡れた階段の裏から、そっと表側へ回り込んだ。

僕は濡れた手で階段の手すりをそっと掴む。
風が階段のトタン屋根を揺らすたび、雨粒がまとめて落ちてくる。
その水音が、人の足音のようにも聞こえ、僕は反射的に身を縮めた。
深呼吸——。
よし、と自分に言い聞かせ、階段を一段ずつ踏みしめて上りはじめる。
金属製の踏み板は滑り止めの凸凹が付けられているとは言うものの、横から入り込んでくる雨で段差を流れ落ちる滝のようになっていて素足の足の裏をもってしても滑りそうで怖い。
少しでも足を踏み外せば、とっさに声が出てしまいそうで、慎重にならざるをえなかった。
素足の足の裏が金属に当たる、わずかな感触。
ほんの数ミリ体重をかけただけで、ギシ、と鳴りそうな気がして怖い。
段差を流れ落ちる水が足の甲の上にまでかぶさってきて僕の足を押し流そうとしている。
どこにナニカが隠れているか油断はできない。
——上からも襲って来たんだよな。
思い出した途端、背中をつーっと冷たさが這い上がる。
階段の屋根の上に、あの“何か”が張り付いているような光景が頭に浮かび、 思わず首筋がゾワッと震えた。
「まさか……また上に……？」
怖さに負けて、僕は堪えきれず顔を上げた。
階段を覆うトタン屋根は雨を叩きつけられ続け、白い飛沫がポタポタと滴り落ちるだけ。
そこに何かが潜んでいる気配は——幸い、ない。
でも、雨音が大きすぎて“本当にいない”のかどうかが分からない。
それが逆に不気味だった。
一段。
もう一段。
息を潜め、身体をできるだけ小さくして上っていく。
