Phone Call

I was wrapped up in the futon in my own room, in my own home.
Outside my window, the night lay silent. The lights of the town shimmered softly in the darkness.
After I lost consciousness in the crevasse, I was found by another group of climbers. The staff at the Hakuba-jiri mountain lodge contacted the rescue team, and they brought me to safety.
The next time I opened my eyes, I was lying in a hospital bed.
My father and mother were there to visit me...
When I was discharged, they took the long train ride home with me.
The moment I saw my house, a wave of relief washed over me.
I was alive. I had escaped that mountain lodge with no way out and made it back home.
But that relief was soon swept away by the ache stirring deep inside my chest.
Mari-senpai... Yabe-san... Yohei... Takizawa-san... Suzuki Sho-san... They were all gone now.
The lodge owner... Uchida-san... Takamura-san... and everyone else whose lives had become entwined with that nightmare drifted through my mind.
Their smiling faces... their faces twisted in terror... the desperate way they fought to protect one another...
Every memory tightened its grip around my heart until even breathing became painful.
Beneath the covers, my hands were clenched so tightly that my fingers began to ache.
The moment I closed my eyes, the lodge's corridors, the shadows stretched across the floor, the feel of those tentacles, and the echoes of terrified screams all came rushing back.
The line between dreams and reality still wavered, pulling my thoughts into a whirlpool of confusion.
Even so, the softness of my futon, the quiet of my room, and the simple fact that I was finally safe brought me a small measure of comfort.
I took a slow, deep breath... and let it out.
Closing my eyes, I curled up beneath the covers and sank into their warmth.
Gradually, the images and voices that had been swirling through my mind drifted farther and farther away, until only the quiet rhythm of my breathing remained.
The pain in my chest never completely faded, but drowsiness slowly weighed down my eyelids.
Feeling the warmth of my own body beneath the covers... I had no choice but to surrender to sleep.
Outside, the night was silent. The distant lights of the town shimmered softly.
I steadied my breathing and closed my eyes.
The ache in my chest hadn't completely faded, but the softness of my futon calmed me, if only a little. The nightmare in that mountain lodge... the tentacles from my dreams... for the moment, they all seemed to be drifting farther away.
Then...
My smartphone suddenly vibrated beside my pillow, its screen lighting up the darkness. My eyes flew open.
The moment I saw the name on the screen, my breath caught.
Tsuruoka-san...?
With trembling fingers, I answered the call.
A familiar voice filled my ear.
"Hey, wanna go to Toyota Stadium tomorrow? It's Sunday. About fifty thousand people get together and sing all at once. It's incredible. Come on, let's go."
His voice was light... cheerful, even.
Yet an icy sense of wrongness spread through my chest.
...Tsuruoka-san was supposed to be dead.
I froze beneath the covers, feeling my fingertips grow damp with sweat. My heart pounded harder, my breathing turned ragged, and flashes of the lodge, the writhing tentacles, and everyone's faces tore through my mind.
And yet... his voice carried an unsettling sense of reality, as though it were quietly beckoning me toward something.
"Come on."
Those two words blurred the line between reality and dreams.
Clutching my phone beneath the covers, all I could do was wait... unable to move, unable to decide.
I tried to steady my breathing, but sweat clung to my palms, and every muscle in my body remained tense.
Inside my mind, fear tangled itself with the desperate urge not to go, pulling my thoughts into a tightening spiral.
The horror at that mountain lodge really happened.
But was Tsuruoka-san's voice real... or was it only an illusion?
I asked myself the same question again and again. Though wrapped in the warmth of my futon, surrounded by the silence of my room, I couldn't find a moment's peace.
The glow of my phone lingered in the darkness, and Tsuruoka-san's voice still echoed deep inside my ears.
Even with my eyes closed, the pain in my chest refused to fade.
Dreams and reality bled into one another until my mind became a whirlpool of chaos.
All I could do was curl up beneath the covers... and cling to my phone.
