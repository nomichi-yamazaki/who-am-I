Akane

How much time had passed?
I felt like I had forgotten something.
Something important.
Just as that uneasy thought drifted through my mind—
Creeeak.
I looked up at the sound and saw Suzuki Sho-san pushing back his chair and standing up.
He staggered weakly between the tables and wooden pillars before disappearing through the door beside the counter.
A little while later, he returned carrying a T-shaped push broom and a green metal dustpan in his hands.
Without saying a word, Suzuki Sho-san began sweeping together the white fire-extinguisher powder that covered the floor.
Creeeak… creeeak…
Hearing another chair scrape, I turned and saw Kobayashi Ren-san and Aya Morishita-san standing up as well.
They were probably going to help clean up, just like Suzuki Sho-san.
I wanted to stand and help too,
but my body felt drained of strength, and I couldn’t seem to make myself rise from the chair.
Kobayashi Ren-san and Aya Morishita-san left the dining hall.
A short while later, the sound of something rolling down the hallway echoed closer.
When I looked over, Kobayashi Ren-san entered, pulling along a large industrial-looking vacuum cleaner.
Suzuki Sho-san continued sweeping the extinguisher powder into piles with the broom.
Kobayashi Ren-san knelt beside the vacuum cleaner, unraveling the thick black power cord wrapped around it.
Aya Morishita-san returned carrying a wet rag in her hand.
She began wiping away the thin white layer of extinguisher powder coating the tables.
—I have to help too…
Forcing my exhausted body to move, I finally struggled to my feet.
And at that moment…
Bang. Bang. Bang—.
Mixed in with the irregular assault of rain and wind, I thought I could hear something else.
A sharper sound.
Glass. Rhythmic somehow.
—Is someone outside?
I looked toward the window where the sound was coming from.
The dining hall windows were double-paned. The inner window had a brown metal sash, while the outer one was framed with wooden latticework.
Outside was nothing but darkness. The glass reflected the dining hall so clearly that it was impossible to see beyond it.
But when I focused harder on the source of the sound, I saw it.
A human fist.
Over and over, it slammed against the outer window from within the darkness beyond the glass, making the pane shudder each time.
—Someone’s out there!
The instant the fist struck the glass, its outline surfaced sharply within the reflection of the dining hall.
When I listened carefully, I thought I could hear something else mixed into the screaming rain and wind.
A faint voice.
Everyone in the dining hall turned toward the window at once.
“Someone’s… there,” murmured one of the two women in their twenties—the one who loved some actor from those mountain climbing movies.
At that instant, lightning flashed.
The reflection of the dining hall vanished from the glass, and the outside world appeared with perfect clarity, bright as midday.
Several tents stood among a field of rolling stones beneath the storm.
And there—standing in the window—was a woman.
A soaking wet woman.
Her hair clung to her cheeks. Droplets streamed down her arms and the pale line of her neck.
Beneath her gray jacket, darkened by the rain until its color had changed, I could see an orange shirt plastered tightly against her skin.
She looked almost unreal.
Like something illuminated from another world.
Her eyes—large eyes peering through strands of wet hair—were filled with shock, terror, and a desperate resolve.
Her lips trembled slightly, rainwater trailing from the corners of her mouth.
The moment she pressed her palm against the window and leaned closer, it felt as though she was desperately trying to tell us something.
That woman…
She was one of the pair Tsuruoka-san had spoken to at Hakuba-jiri.
The woman who had been climbing with Takamura-san.
The woman whose head had supposedly been crushed and destroyed in that washroom—
It was her.
Akane-san.
Right before my eyes stood Akane-san.
So vivid.
So impossibly alive.
For a single instant, relief surged through the depths of my chest.
—Ah… she survived…
Then the lightning faded, and the reflection of the dining hall returned to the window once more.
But the pounding continued.
Bang. Bang. Bang.
And beneath the roar of the storm, I could hear what sounded like a human voice outside.
I strained my hearing toward it.
“Reiko… hey… open up… let me in…”
The voice was tense.
Trembling.
Yet there was something unnaturally calm beneath it, something that sent a cold shiver crawling up my spine.
“Akane!”
Takamura-san’s scream rang through the dining hall, drowning out even the storm outside.
She rushed toward the window.
Without thinking, my own hand almost reached toward it too.
The two women in their twenties and the man in his sixties also moved toward the window, as if to help open it.
“That’s impossible…” Mari-senpai whispered, her eyes wide.
“Hey! This girl was supposed to be dead!”
Suzuki Sho-san’s voice snapped through the room like a whip, jolting me back to my senses.
The two young women and the older man flinched and froze mid-motion.
In my mind, the memory resurfaced.
Akane-san’s head.
Crushed apart beneath those black tentacles with a wet, collapsing crunch.
Akane-san… came back?
No.
That couldn’t be possible.
Cold sweat spread across my palms, while my fingertips felt numb with icy pain.
Reason screamed inside my skull—
Impossible… there’s no way she could still be alive!
My thoughts tangled together.
My body locked in place.
—Am I dreaming?
…Am I trapped inside some terrible nightmare?
If Akane-san was really alive…
If she truly was the one pounding on that window outside—
Then I had seen it all with my own eyes.
That incomprehensible creature attacking her.
The desperate way she had tried to escape.
No one had been able to help her.
After enduring unimaginable terror, she had fled into the storm alone, battered by freezing rain and violent wind, desperately forcing her cold, aching body forward while striking the window with trembling hands, begging for help.
In those eyes shimmered terror, loneliness, and a desperate plea:
Please… help me.
Her lips trembled.
Even when the storm swallowed her voice, I could tell she was still desperately trying to reach us.
She wanted to come into the warmth of the dining hall.
But no one would let her in.
All alone, she had been left behind in the raging storm.
The thought squeezed painfully at my chest, and instinctively I wanted to reach out my hand.
But reason screamed back at me.
That girl already…
Then Akane-san’s voice from Hakuba-jiri resurfaced inside my head.
“I love blueberries.”
That tiny sentence.
The way she had smiled when she said it.
It tightened around my heart like a fist.
My chest pounded so hard it felt ready to burst apart, while my palms were slick with sweat.
My breathing turned shallow and quick, and all I could do was press myself against the wall with trembling fingers.
My heartbeat hammered wildly inside my ears.
Each breath came ragged and uneven, trembling faintly as it escaped my lips.
Thoughts tangled inside my head while confusion and terror crushed against my chest.
—Impossible! Someone whose head was crushed apart can’t just come back looking completely normal!
“What do you mean dead? She’s right there!” the man in his sixties shouted.
“She’s not human!”
Yabe-san’s voice cut across his words like a scream.
My pulse accelerated until it echoed painfully in my ears.
“Not human…? Th-that’s ridiculous…” the older man muttered.
“Hyaaaahhhhhh—!”
At first, I thought that bizarre shriek had come from Tsuruoka-san.
The two women in their twenties recoiled violently at the scream they assumed was his, hunching their shoulders and clutching each other tightly.
“Akane…!”
Takamura-san screamed as she grabbed the meeting rail of the window and reached for the crescent lock.
“Takamura-san, don’t open it!”
Suzuki Sho-san hurriedly reached toward her.
“Please stop!” Kobayashi Ren-san shouted as well.
—Why are Suzuki-san and Kobayashi-san trying to stop her from opening the window?
I couldn’t process it.
Takamura-san resisted them and tried to force the window open.
…When Akane-san’s head was crushed by those tentacles, both Suzuki-san and Kobayashi-san had seen it from the back of the room.
Akane-san was supposed to be dead.
That was why they were trying so desperately to stop the window from being opened.
…If we open it…
My thoughts blurred like fog, while terror froze my entire body solid.
My heart lurched up into my throat, and cold sweat crawled down my back.
Every movement Mari-senpai and Yabe-san made only deepened my panic, while helplessness pressed painfully against my chest.
“Wait!”
Mari-senpai grabbed Takamura-san’s arm as she struggled with the window.
“It’s dangerous if you open it!”
Yabe-san joined her, desperately trying to restrain Takamura-san.
“W-Why won’t you let her in!?”
From somewhere in the back, I heard the athletic man shouting.
“Reiko… please… open the door…”
The voice outside pleaded sweetly.
But beneath those words was an emptiness that suggested something else entirely.
Something that was not Akane-san.
“She’s right outside the window!” the athletic man yelled again from the back.
“Yeah! We don’t know what’s going on here, so explain it to us!”
Even Glasses-and-Jeans was shouting now.
“Daddy, why won’t they let that woman inside? I feel sorry for her…”
A child’s voice trembled from somewhere behind us.
—I want to let her in too.
But the thing outside wasn’t the real Akane-san anymore…
Something was imitating her.
Pretending to be Akane-san so it could get inside this place.
Mari-senpai and Yabe-san were desperately holding Takamura-san back, almost wrapping their arms around her to restrain her.
“Akane!”
After shouting her name, Takamura-san hesitated for the first time—
Had she finally remembered?
Remembered that she had watched Akane-san’s head being crushed apart right in front of her own eyes?
That’s right.
It had happened right in front of Takamura-san.
Outside, the wind howled while rain hammered against the glass.
Still, Takamura-san did not stop reaching toward the window.
She leaned forward slightly.
Her breathing grew ragged, tension hardening her fingertips.
“Takamura-san… I’m sorry, but Akane-san can’t possibly still be alive.”
Mari-senpai spoke quietly, yet with absolute certainty.
Those words finally caused Takamura-san’s hands to stop.
But the voice outside continued reaching toward us, and the tension inside the dining hall stretched tighter and tighter.
“Takamura-san… please…”
The voice outside sounded sweet.
Inviting.
The tiny distance between fingertips and glass.
The pauses between words.
The trembling breaths.
“What do you mean she’s dead!? …Are you saying the girl outside is dead!? …Is that really true!? She’s standing right there in front of us, isn’t she!? Then why won’t anyone explain anything!?”
The athletic man was practically screaming now.
“That thing isn’t human.”
Suzuki Sho-san cut him off sharply, and the athletic man finally fell silent.
Between the window before me and the trembling voice outside, a heavy, dreadful reality slowly settled into the depths of my chest.
“Noooo—!!”
Takamura-san screamed and collapsed to her knees.
Dr. Uchida crouched beside her and gently supported her shoulders.
Takamura-san—beautiful in the same way my father used to say actress Wakako Sakai was beautiful—had a face now twisted by terror, clinging desperately to Dr. Uchida as though she could not remain standing without someone to hold onto.
Mari-senpai softly embraced Takamura-san’s other shoulder from the opposite side.
Yabe-san stood there in stunned silence, staring down at them.
“Not human…? Th-that’s insane…” the athletic man muttered weakly.
The pounding against the window felt like it was striking directly against my heart.
“It’s your fault… your fault…”
The thing wearing Akane-san’s face kept pounding on the glass, and somehow I felt as though those words were hidden within the sound itself.
My chest tightened painfully.
My palms were slick with sweat, and I could feel my fingertips trembling in tiny spasms.
Beyond the glass, the outline of the pounding fists and the shape of the face became visible for only an instant whenever lightning flashed.
Those eyes…
They should have been Akane-san’s eyes.
And yet the pupils were unnaturally large, so dark they seemed to swallow the light itself.
Each time the fists slammed against the window, the glass trembled violently with a sharp, vibrating rattle, while the sash groaned under the impact.
And every time it happened, I could feel the vibration travel faintly through the floor beneath my feet.
“I love blueberries.”
Akane-san’s voice.
The way she had smiled while answering Yabe-san.
That memory now pressed down on me like a weight.
The thing pounding endlessly against the glass in front of us now was something unimaginable from that gentle smile.
Pure terror.
At any moment, it felt as though the glass might shatter and it would come crashing inside.
Even remaining standing was becoming difficult.
My breathing grew harsher and harsher, while the noise deep inside my ears refused to stop.
