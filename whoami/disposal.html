Disposal

The lodge owner, who had been hanging his head in thought, finally looked up and spread his hands as if trying to calm everyone down.
“Everyone, settle down. I’ve been thinking about this, and right now we’re dealing with two separate problems. One is the legal and ethical side of things. The other is immediate safety.”
He glanced toward Suzuki Sho-san and me.
“Sho and this young man here have already prepared weapons that might work against the creature outside. So now the question is what we do about Naoki-kun here.”
He paused for a moment before continuing.
“So first… what if we use a waterproof tarp and move him outside? A small group carries him, two people stand watch, and we use flashlights to keep checking the surroundings. Once it’s done, everyone regroups here. How does that sound?”
“The matter of what to do with the body afterward… we’ll deal with that once this crisis is over.”
When the lodge owner said “this young man,” he pointed at me.
—I’m Sho too, though…
For some reason, despite the situation, I almost laughed.
It felt as though my mind was trying to laugh away the unbearable heaviness pressing down on us.
Mari-senpai bit her lip and gave a small nod while holding back tears.
The athletic-looking man nodded grimly as well.
The decision wasn’t perfect.
But right now, it was the only option any of us had.

“I’m sorry, but could you go get the waterproof tarp?”
The lodge owner let out a small sigh and lightly raised both hands as he spoke to Suzuki Sho-san.
Suzuki-san immediately nodded.
Avoiding the pillars and tables of the dining hall, he headed for the entrance and disappeared through the still-open doorway, running down the corridor toward the lounge area.
While Suzuki Sho-san went to retrieve the tarp, the people left behind in the dining hall stayed gathered around Naoki-kun’s motionless body on the floor, holding their breath.
No one could even bring themselves to speak to one another.
Somewhere in the room, a child was crying.
Mari-senpai clasped her hands tightly in front of her chest, biting her lip as she struggled to hold back tears.
Uchida-san and the weeping Takamura-san looked as though they were barely managing to stay standing by supporting each other.
The athletic-looking man kept his fists clenched as he glared down at Naoki-kun lying on the floor, while Yabe-san furrowed her brow and continued observing the situation with forced calmness.
The stench of what Naoki-kun had vomited, the smell of burning, the gasoline fumes—and mixed among them, that horribly pleasant scent like roasting meat—made the tension in the room feel even heavier.

Suzuki Sho-san came back through the dining hall entrance carrying a dark, faded olive-green waterproof tarp.
Weaving between tables and wooden pillars, he made his way to where Naoki-kun lay covered in white extinguishing powder and began unfolding the tarp.
The lodge owner and Kobayashi Ren-san hurried over to help him spread it out.
As Suzuki Sho-san and Kobayashi-san laid the tarp across the floor, they exchanged uneasy glances.
“How should we do this? Maybe cover him from this side first… then roll him onto it?” the lodge owner suggested.
“Yeah… I think that’s the safest way. Gently, from above…” Kobayashi-san nodded.
Both men kept their voices low, careful not to let them carry across the room.
Carefully, they lowered the waterproof sheet over Naoki-kun’s body.
“Alright… let’s roll him into the tarp. Slowly… carefully…” the lodge owner said quietly.
Morishita Aya-san quickly stepped closer to the tarp covering Naoki-kun.
“I’ll help too,” I said, moving over beside them.
Mari-senpai, Takizawa-san, Yohei, Yabe-san, and the others — everyone except Tsuruoka-san — gathered nervously around the tarp and reached out their hands.
The lodge owner, Kobayashi Ren-san, Suzuki Sho-san, Morishita Aya-san, Mari-senpai, Takizawa-san, Yohei, Yabe-san, and I slowly rolled Naoki-kun’s mass-like body as if wrapping him inside the tarp itself.
Zzrr… zzrr…
The waterproof sheet scraped against the floor.
The rough texture of the tarp pressed against my palms.
Through the sheet, Naoki-kun’s body felt faintly warm, soft… and slightly damp beneath my fingertips.
The subtle unevenness of his shape and the heavy weight beneath the tarp oozed into my hands with an awful physical presence.
Without meaning to, an image flashed through my mind — that melted, ruined thing wrapped inside the black tracksuit.
The sensation felt far too real.
A shiver crawled violently down my spine.
My breathing caught in my throat, and the warmth passing through the tarp made the horror feel terrifyingly real.
Morishita Aya-san was crying as she bent over and helped roll Naoki-kun’s body through the waterproof tarp.
The lodge owner, Kobayashi Ren-san, Suzuki Sho-san, Mari-senpai, Takizawa-san, Yohei, Yabe-san — and probably me too — all had our faces twisted in discomfort, whether from the stench or from the horror of the situation itself.
I felt sick.
My body trembled so badly it felt like the strength was draining from my arms.
I didn’t even know if I was actually helping, but little by little Naoki-kun’s body rolled over, and one side of the tarp disappeared beneath him.
“Alright… next, pull the lower sheet through…” Suzuki-san said.
Kobayashi-san grabbed the edge and carefully tugged the tarp out from underneath Naoki-kun’s wrapped body.
I lightly pulled the sheet sideways in rhythm with Suzuki-san and Kobayashi-san’s movements.
“We’ve got it this far… yeah, this should work,” Kobayashi-san exhaled.
Then, when they unfolded the opposite side of the tarp, Naoki-kun’s body finally lay completely atop the waterproof sheet.
Zzrrr—
The sound of powder scraping echoed softly as Naoki-kun’s figure emerged again.
The white extinguishing powder drifted through the air like a thin haze, faintly glowing under the yellow ceiling lights.
Like a plaster statue, Naoki-kun’s body lay there stiff and motionless.
Between the layers of white powder, I could see the blackened remains of his body lying on its back, wrapped in what looked like burned rags.
The contrast between the white powder and the charred black body felt strangely unreal.
Everyone understood that this was death…
and yet somewhere deep inside, it still felt possible that he might suddenly breathe again.
Suzuki-san swallowed hard.
No one spoke.
A single grain of powder drifted down onto the tarp and softly touched Naoki-kun’s cheek.
As though telling him there was nothing more left to say.
Outside, the storm continued to rumble low in the distance.
Beneath the dim lodge lights, the veil of white powder wrapped around Naoki-kun,
blurring the boundary between reality and nightmare.

The lodge owner raised his voice to the others.
“Everyone, give us a hand. From here on, we carry him outside carefully.”
We all nodded to one another and took hold of the edges of the tarp.
The rough material bit into my fingers, and my palms quickly grew damp with sweat.
Kobayashi Ren-san and Suzuki Sho-san took the lead at the front.
Takizawa-san and Yohei positioned themselves beside them, while Yabe-san, Mari-senpai, Morishita Aya-san, and I supported the rear.
The lodge owner stood a short distance away, watching the balance carefully while giving instructions.
The athletic-looking man, the glasses-and-jeans guy, and even the child’s father hesitantly reached in from the back to help support the load.
Maybe the child’s father wanted to show courage in front of his kid.
Maybe the athletic guy and the glasses-and-jeans guy had finally calmed down enough to realize that blaming others wouldn’t solve anything.
Or maybe they were ashamed of how shaken and panicked they themselves had become.
In the end… those two probably weren’t bad people after all.
That was what I found myself thinking.

“One, two—lift!”
At the lodge owner’s call, the waterproof tarp rose all at once.
A heavy weight immediately pressed down on my arms.
It was far heavier than I had imagined.
When a human body lies silent like this… why does it feel so overwhelmingly present?
As the tarp lifted, the smell of burning drifted faintly into the air.
Mixed with it were traces of damp skin and the chemical, metallic scent of the white extinguishing powder.
Together with the warmth and moisture of Naoki-kun’s body transmitted through the tarp into our palms, the odor hung heavily around us, making everyone instinctively grimace.
“Slowly now. Don’t hit the tables,”
the lodge owner said in a low voice as he guided us forward.
Zzzt… creak… creak…
Only the sound of slippers shuffling and the wooden floorboards groaning echoed through the lodge.
White powder drifted softly out from the gaps in the tarp, floating through the air under the lights.
Each time it happened, Morishita Aya-san quietly caught her breath.
On the wooden floor lay a dull golden fire hose nozzle shaped like an oversized drumstick.
Behind it, a fire hose made of coarse, faded off-white fabric — almost like rough denim — snaked wildly across the floorboards.
As soon as we stepped out of the dining hall and into the hallway, everyone moved carefully, conscious of every creak beneath their feet.
“Watch your step. Don’t get tangled in the hose,” the lodge owner warned.
The fire hose stretched along the hallway in twisting loops toward the crossroads ahead.
I shuffled forward cautiously too, watching my footing with every step.
At the far left side of the crossroads, the square fire hydrant cabinet hung open, and the hose crawling across the floor extended out from inside it.
As we reached the intersection, we glanced ahead toward the lounge and left toward the washroom corridor before turning right toward the entrance hall.
Step by step, we walked carefully, almost feeling out the floor beneath our slippers before committing our weight to it.

The shield made from acrylic panels that Suzuki Sho-san and I had prepared, along with the cart carrying the generator, stood nearby.
“Sho and Bon Curry, stay back with the weapons you made. If that thing attacks us, we’re gonna have a serious problem,” the lodge owner said without turning around.
—Right… so I’m on weapon duty…
I was relieved I no longer had to carry Naoki-kun’s body, but at the same time, I felt guilty leaving the heavy lifting to everyone else while I stood by with the weapons.
Suzuki Sho-san pulled a black flashlight from his pocket and started to hand it to the lodge owner.
“Morishita-san, take the flashlight from Sho and keep the outside illuminated…” the owner said to Aya Morishita-san.
“I want to keep holding Naoki-kun,” Morishita-san replied softly.
“I see… then Ren-san, could I ask you instead?” the owner said, still gripping the edge of the waterproof sheet.
“Understood,” Kobayashi Ren-san answered. He released the sheet and accepted the flashlight from Suzuki Sho-san.
“Alright, then let’s get that thing ready,” Suzuki Sho-san said to me.
I nodded, let go of the sheet, and stepped back.
“Careful… there’s a step,” Kobayashi Ren-san warned from the front, flashlight in hand.
The entrance had a concrete dirt-floor area set one step lower.
Everyone paused for a moment, careful not to lose their footing.
“Leave your slippers on. You don’t need to take them off,” the owner called out without turning as they approached the entrance.
“Can you carry the two spears? I’ll push the cart,” Suzuki Sho-san said to me.
“Got it.”
I picked up the two spears made from metal pipes that rested on the cart.
Suzuki Sho-san began pushing the cart, and the two of us followed behind the people gathered around the waterproof sheet.
Goro… goro goro…
The wheels of the cart rattled as they turned.
The people carrying the waterproof sheet moved in careful unison, stepping down one by one onto the lower concrete floor. Each time Naoki-kun’s body shifted slightly inside the sheet, the sound of someone catching their breath could be heard.
“Alright… keep moving toward the entrance,” the owner said, pointing ahead.
Bang… bang, bang…
The storm outside slammed against the glass doors, while the wind roared loudly beyond them.
It almost sounded as if the storm itself was trying to stop us from going outside.
Suzuki Sho-san and I pushed the cart up to the edge of the concrete entryway. He turned the cart around so he could pull it instead, then stepped down first onto the lower dirt floor and carefully lowered the back wheels down ahead of him.
Holding the two spears made from metal pipes, I moved along with him, making sure the parallel cords attached to the spears wouldn’t get tangled.
—He said we could keep our slippers on, right?
Thinking that, I stepped down onto the concrete floor still wearing my slippers.
For some reason, it felt as though I had been granted the same privilege as the lodge staff.
The cold air of the entryway brushed against my cheeks, mixing with the burnt smell and stinging my nose.
Outside the entrance, rain and wind still raged violently.
The sound of the storm echoed in the distance like a howl—
as though it were calling that charred thing back to life.

Kobayashi Ren-san switched on the flashlight and shone it outside through the barred window of the locked entrance door.
Right beside it, Suzuki Sho-san crouched down in front of the small generator and pulled the black plastic T-shaped grip.
Burururu…
The dry snap of the cord recoiling brushed past my ears, and after several short pulls—then one strong pull from his hips—the generator let out a low growl, metallic vibrations spreading through the floorboards.
Dadadadadadada…
The sharp mechanical noise transformed the atmosphere inside the lodge for just a moment, as though time itself had shifted.
Outside, rain hammered against the roof while the wind howled, rattling the metal roofing overhead.
Kobayashi Ren-san stepped closer to the barred window and pointed the flashlight outside.
The intense white LED beam carved circles through the darkness, illuminating one distorted tent roof after another as they twisted violently in the wind.
Orange, yellow, pale blue—layers of overlapping colors warped and shifted like oil floating on water, briefly emerging from the storm-dark night.
Rain battered against the glass.
The tents snapped sharply in the wind.
Somewhere far away, thunder rumbled low across the mountains.
“We completely forgot about the people camping in tents…” the lodge owner muttered quietly.
There was still unease in his voice, but his words were steady.
As if forcing himself to regain composure, the lodge owner continued.
“That thing is still out there, and so is this storm. Until things settle down, we’ll leave him just outside to the left of the entrance. We’ll keep watch from there. Ren-san, check carefully to make sure that thing isn’t outside.”
Kobayashi Ren-san moved the beam of light in short, precise motions, carefully sweeping it between the rows of tents and through the patches of darkness.
“So far… I can’t see anything moving out there that looks like it. But the wind keeps distorting visibility.
Stay alert.”
His voice was calm, but it couldn’t hide the trembling presence of someone behind him.
“We’ll protect everyone with the shields, just in case,” Suzuki Sho-san said quietly, glancing at me beside him.
The words were short—not an order, but a shared acknowledgment of what might happen.
We looked at each other and picked up the acrylic shields we had prepared.
The edges of the transparent but thick panels felt cold against my hands.
—Once we go outside, the wind and rain are going to be brutal…
Just thinking about it made my stomach tighten with nerves.
“Ren-san, if it looks safe outside, unlock the door,” the owner said.
Keeping the flashlight aimed through the window, Kobayashi Ren-san reached for the shining brass rod attached to the door and turned it repeatedly to the left.
The rhythmic clinking sound of the lock mixed with the rattling of the glass shaken by the wind.
The low growl of the generator.
The snapping of tent fabric outside the window.
The faint sweat gathering on the fingers gripping the acrylic shields.
Though the tension remained, everyone’s breathing slowly began to steady as they focused on the task at hand.
The place to leave Naoki-kun had been decided.
Preparations were nearly complete.
Suzuki Sho-san and I stepped cautiously outside, acrylic shields in hand.
At once, the raging wind slammed into us, rain pelting hard against our bodies.
The acrylic shield in my hands caught the wind and twisted violently, nearly dragging my whole body sideways with it.
I desperately braced myself and tightened my grip along the edge to steady my stance.
It wasn’t cold.
The wind was strangely warm instead, and that somehow made it even more unsettling.
Above us, a deep rumble like distant thunder rolled through the sky.
The rain striking my body and the heavy pull of the wind against the acrylic shield pressed themselves sharply into my senses.
My T-shirt and shorts were soaked through almost instantly.
—Whatever happens, happens…
No matter how awful things get, the day always ends eventually.
I repeated the thought over and over in my head.
The lodge owner, Aya Morishita-san, Mari-senpai, Takizawa-san, Yohei, Yabe-san, the athletic guy, Glasses-and-Jeans, and the father with the child—
the people carrying the waterproof sheet set Naoki-kun down immediately to the left of the entrance.
Everyone caught their breath for a moment, then hurried back inside the lodge.
After staring down at Naoki-kun’s charred body lying atop the sheet, the lodge owner quietly pulled one edge of the sheet over him.

“It’s dangerous to stay out here too long. Let’s get back inside,” Suzuki Sho-san called out to the lodge owner, and the three of us hurried back into the lodge.
I could feel water dripping heavily from my completely soaked hair down across my body.
“To everyone camping in tents! It’s dangerous outside—please come inside the lodge!”
Leaving the entrance door open, the owner shouted out into the storm.
But the roaring wind and pounding rain swallowed his voice, and there was no way to tell whether the people in the tents could even hear him.
Rain and wind blasted in through the wide-open doorway.
The lodge owner stood there for a while calling out, but the storm blowing through the gap drenched him completely.
Suzuki Sho-san shrugged slightly and muttered in a quiet voice.
“We should give up.”
After hesitating for a brief moment, the owner let out a deep breath and slowly closed the door.
The roar of the wind became muffled, leaving only the sound of rain striking outside.
The atmosphere inside the lodge regained a small measure of calm.
Kobayashi Ren-san turned the golden brass rod to the right again and again, locking the entrance door.

Suzuki Sho-san gently placed the acrylic shield onto the cart.
The sound it made was so small it was nearly swallowed by the rain outside—a soft clunk.
I followed his lead and placed the acrylic shield I was carrying onto the cart as well.
The instant my fingertips touched the cold transparent surface, a chill spread across my palms.
…Tatata.
Suzuki Sho-san switched off the generator.
The machine still held a faint warmth, the metal parts radiating lingering heat.
The athletic guy, Glasses-and-Jeans, the child’s father, Takizawa-san, Yabe-san, Yohei, Mari-senpai, Aya Morishita-san, and Kobayashi Ren-san slowly filed back toward the dining hall.
Suzuki Sho-san gripped the cart’s handlebar and quietly turned it around.
The metal wheels scraped across the concrete floor with a long, low sound.
Step by careful step, he pushed the cart toward the edge of the raised hallway floor,
then pressed the handle downward firmly.
The front wheels lifted with the motion and climbed onto the hallway ledge.
He then pulled up on the handlebar, raising the rear wheels onto the floor as well.
Watching him, I picked up the two spears made from electrical conduit pipes
and followed behind Suzuki Sho-san as he headed toward the crossroads farther down the corridor.
Across the once-clean hallway floor, the cart wheels left behind intertwined double tracks stretching along both sides.
From behind us drifted the faint smell of generator oil mixed with the damp scent of rain-soaked wood.
When I glanced back, the lodge owner trudged after us with his head lowered in exhaustion.
At the crossroads, Kobayashi Ren-san was dragging the fire hose that crawled along the floor.
As we reached the intersection, Suzuki Sho-san stopped the cart there, let out a quiet breath, and released the handle.
He turned and gave me a silent signal with his eyes.
I laid the two spears down beside the cart and stepped away from it with him.
The long, tapered cylindrical fire nozzle scraped across the hallway floor as it was pulled toward the crossroads.
We entered through the dining hall entrance, whose door still hung open.
When I returned to the dining hall, I stopped for a moment.
The air inside felt heavy and damp.
The lingering tension from outside still clung to everyone’s backs and shoulders.
The chill rising from the earthen floor wrapped itself around my body, and the feeling of my rain-soaked socks against my skin felt strangely vivid.
Maybe the heat from carrying out the waterproof sheet still remained in the room,
because every breath carried the smell of scorched cloth and burned oil into my nose.
People collapsed into chairs as they pulled them out—the athletic guy, Glasses-and-Jeans, Takizawa-san, Yohei, Mari-senpai, Yabe-san, Aya Morishita-san, Kobayashi Ren-san, the lodge owner—
every one of them slumped with shoulders lowered, their faces frozen with exhaustion and shock.
No one spoke.
Only the faint sound of breathing echoed through the room.
Even the eyes of those still standing seemed unfocused.
Tsuruoka-san sat hunched at the corner of a table, his face buried in both hands.
Dr. Uchida stood with her arms folded, staring out the window.
Her lips were tightly pressed together, her expression strained as though she were desperately holding something back.
Takamura-san clasped both hands before her chest,
her fingertips trembling in tiny, constant shakes.
The two women in their twenties leaned against each other silently, staring at the floor.
The man in his sixties gazed up at the ceiling as though none of this had ever happened.
The child clung to one of the wooden pillars, only occasionally trembling faintly.
Outside, the storm howled like something insane.
Wind slammed against the walls, and rain battered the windows.
The sound of raindrops striking the metal roof echoed low and rhythmically, carrying all the way into the room.
Nobody spoke.
Silence simply lingered there, and every so often, beneath the sound of the storm outside, I could hear my own heartbeat pounding loudly in my ears.
It felt as though time itself had stopped.
I pulled out a chair myself and sank into it, my back hunched forward.
I desperately wanted someone—anyone—to protect me.
I thought about going over to Mari-senpai,
but the eyes of the others held me back, and I couldn’t bring myself to move.
No one opened their mouth.
The atmosphere in the dining hall hung heavy and stagnant.
The entire room was wrapped in oppressive silence.
Only the merciless sound of rain and wind hammering the windows continued to echo through the lodge.
I felt utterly exhausted, body and mind alike, as though I no longer had the strength to move.
Black scorch marks still stained the ceiling overhead,
while white powder clung to the floor and the edges of the tables, with patches of charred blackness peeking through the cracks beneath it.
Part of me thought that if someone started cleaning, I would have to force my heavy body to help somehow.
But everyone looked so completely worn out that nobody could even begin to move.
