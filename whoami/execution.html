Execution of the Plan

"...She's gone now. You can come out."
Yabe-san's voice was still low,
still carrying traces of tension.
Even after hearing her words,
my body refused to move.
—What if...
What if she was still standing
somewhere out in the hallway?
The thought clung stubbornly to my mind,
refusing to let go.
Buried deep within the pile of futons,
I held my breath
one last time.
I heard nothing.
No wet,
dripping footsteps...
No presence at all.
Finally making up my mind,
I pushed the futons aside.
The moment my view cleared,
my heart leaped.
—No one was there.
The room looked exactly
the same as before.
That alone
was enough to make
the strength leave my legs.
"...She really left, didn't she?"
"Yeah."
Yabe-san gave a short nod.
"...Looks like our theory
was right—at least for now."
I nodded silently.
But I couldn't feel relieved.
There was nothing more dangerous
than safety that came with conditions.
"...What do we do now?"
I spoke in a voice
far calmer than I expected.
"If we're going to escape...
we need to get our hiking boots first."
Yabe-san narrowed her eyes.
"Then we'll have to go to the entrance."
"Yeah."
The moment I started thinking about an escape route,
my mind moved on to the next step all by itself.
"And then..."
I chose my words carefully.
"If we're really going to burn this place down...
we'll need to get to the power room."
Yabe-san's expression tightened slightly.
"The steel door outside the power room is locked.
You can't get in from outside."
Tracing the layout of the lodge in my mind,
I continued.
"So...
we leave our packs outside that steel door."
"And then?"
"We take the stairs beside it
up to the second floor."
Almost unconsciously,
I pointed into the air.
"Next to that raised section in the large rooms.
Around where Kobayashi Ren-san...
started to transform."
A brief silence.
Still, I went on.
"That opening should still be there.
From there, we can get into the crawl space
between the first and second floors."
Yabe-san listened without a word.
"We follow the crawl space
and climb down into the storage room
behind the kitchen."
A hint of confidence
crept into my voice.
"The owner opened that storage room earlier...
so it should still be unlocked."
"But the dining hall..."
"There could be some of those of things in there,"
I said plainly.
"But the kitchen is cluttered.
If we stay hidden while moving through it...
we might avoid being seen."
Yabe-san let out a quiet breath.
"So it's a gamble."
"Yeah."
But at this point,
gambling was all we had left.
"There's an entrance to the power room
outside the kitchen.
That one...
should be open."
I paused for a moment.
Then I added quietly,
"...There was a Sunpot heater in the power room."
Yabe-san looked up.
"I repaired one years ago
back when I was working for the company."
At that moment,
the pieces finally clicked into place.
"Those heaters use
a wind-up timer mechanism."
The words began to come faster.
"If we remove the heating unit
and leave it on the floor after pouring gasoline..."
"And set the timer..."
Yabe-san said softly.
"It becomes a timed ignition device."
"...I think so."
I nodded firmly.
Silence settled over the room.
But this silence
carried something heavier than fear.
It carried resolve.
Burn it.
Escape.
Survive.
If we got that order wrong,
it would all be over.
Yabe-san slowly drew in a breath—
and then said quietly,
"...Let's do it."

Yabe-san silently opened her backpack
and pulled out her rain gear.
It rustled softly as she unfolded it
and slipped her arms into the sleeves.
"...What are you doing?"
she asked without even looking up.
"Hurry up and put your rain gear on."
For a moment,
I couldn't find the words.
"...I gave mine to Morishita-san."
Yabe-san froze.
"...What?"
"I thought...
she'd be a little safer if she had it on...
She said she didn't have one,
and she was crying..."
I wasn't trying to make excuses.
I was simply stating what had happened.
Yabe-san let out a short breath
that wasn't quite a sigh.
"You're...
really too kind for your own good."
There was no reproach in her voice.
Only exasperation—
and a hint of resignation.
"But this isn't the time for that."
At last,
she looked directly at me.
"Just wear somebody else's.
There should be one in your room, right?"
She paused,
carefully choosing her next words.
"...They're not going to need them anymore."
Her voice was quiet.
And because of that,
the weight of those words
hit even harder.
"...Yeah..."
Cruel as it was,
it was also true.
I nodded silently.
A dull ache tightened deep in my chest.
And yet—
if we wanted to survive,
I had no choice
but to accept that reality.

I did as Yabe-san suggested and stepped into the room across the room—Hinode.
The moment I closed the sliding door, the atmosphere changed.
In the middle of the room lay two backpacks.
Takizawa-san's.
Tsuruoka-san had already had me bring his pack to the communal sleeping room.
And—Yohei's.
No one was coming back for it anymore.
My eyes were naturally drawn to one of the packs.
A black CHUMS Spring Dale 35,
its signature rainbow webbing standing out against the dark fabric.
Suddenly, a memory from Hakuba-jiri came flooding back.
“Spring Dale? What,is it spring daily or something? Aha, aha, ahaha…”
Tsuruoka-san had made the joke himself,
and laughed at it himself.
Back then, I thought it was just another dumb joke.
But now, that backpack looked strangely heavy.
—Yohei.
A sharp pain tightened in my chest.
Yohei had been my best friend.
We'd joined the company at the same time.
He lived on his own, while I still lived with my parents.
Even so, we visited each other's homes so often
that being together had simply become normal.
I played guitar.
Yohei played bass.
Whenever we met, we always ended up talking about music.
Late at night, we'd ride our scooters through quiet neighborhoods
and gather at the park.
Drinking canned coffee beneath the streetlights,
we'd tell each other over and over,
"We're gonna make it big with our band someday."
Yohei was the kind of guy
who could brighten the mood no matter where he went.
He wasn't an especially experienced climber,
yet he had fit right into this hiking club.
That was just the kind of person he was.
People naturally liked him.
“So, Saku, what’ve you been listening to lately?
Me, I’m still all about the old-school rock.
The kind we’d call in Nagoya—
‘straight-up awesome.’”
He changed the subject like that all the time.
He’d just as easily veer into dirty jokes,
but deep down,
he cared about people more than anyone—
and before you knew it,
he’d have everyone around him smiling.
“I’ve been really into ‘Intuition’ by TNT lately.
That melody just hits me, you know?
And the riff—it’s so good, it really gets me pumped.”
“Oh yeah, I get that! That song’s seriously a classic.
It’s not super popular,
but anyone who knows it probably loves it.”
Y&T.
White Lion.
We could talk about music forever.
That night, after dinner,
as we walked down the hallway toward the washroom,
it had been no different.
Reflected in the glass of the sliding door
were the two of us walking side by side.
I thought nothing had changed.
“But hey…
what if that thing in the drying room is actually alive?”
Yohei had said it like a joke.
“Well, if it showed up at one of our gigs,
it’d definitely get the crowd going,”
We laughed together,
making up stupid scenarios like always.
—Just a few hours later.
When people began to suspect me
because I'd been with that black, fetus-like creature in the drying room...
Yohei said nothing.
His face was twisted with fear.
He simply stood there,
staring at me in silence.
He didn't defend me.
Looking back,
I realized his body had been trembling ever so slightly.
That cheerful, easygoing Yohei.
At that moment,
I heard something crack deep inside my chest.
Friendship.
Camaraderie.
Something I had always believed was unshakable
collapsed with a soundless crash.
And then... we met again.
"But... did you already become one of those things?..."
Yohei, now with a single eye,
his neck stretching toward me.
He was no longer the person I once knew.
I bit down on my lip.
—I'm sorry.
I repeated those words silently in my heart
as I unzipped Yohei's backpack.
Inside was a deep navy rain suit.
Simple.
A small logo embroidered on the chest.
Every time I slipped my arms into the sleeves,
it felt as though traces of Yohei still lingered at my fingertips.
After putting on the rain gear,
I hoisted my backpack onto my shoulders.
The weight pulled me back to reality.
At the doorway,
Yabe-san had opened the sliding door just a crack,
carefully watching the darkness outside.
Without knowing
what might be waiting for us beyond it.

The sudden sound rang out.
Though calling it a sound didn't quite feel right.
It was less something I heard with my ears,
and more like something tracing directly through the back of my mind.
Like metal scraping against metal,
or standing beneath high-voltage power lines—
a sensation that made my very thoughts tremble.
For a moment,
I wondered if something was simply wrong with me.
After everything that had happened,
it wouldn't have been strange to start hearing things from fear and exhaustion alone.
Trying to convince myself of that,
I quietly glanced over at Yabe-san.
She had frozen in front of the sliding door,
a deep furrow forming between her brows.
Unconsciously, she was pressing a hand against her temple.
"......What is that sound...? It keeps happening..."
Her voice was low,
tinged with both irritation and unease.
—So it wasn't just me.
A chill ran through my chest.
And at the same time,
a certainty rose within me.
This was it.
In front of the drying room.
In the dining hall.
Back then, too—this sound had always come first.
And right after that,
the people who had become those things
all began moving in the same direction,
as though they had received some kind of signal.
Yohei had done the same.
After hearing this sound,
his gaze drifted away,
and he had wandered off somewhere,
as if something were calling to him.
I took a step closer to Yabe-san
and lowered my voice.
"......Yabe-san, this is our chance."
She turned to look at me.
"When this sound happens...
those things seem to gather somewhere."
The words made my throat sting.
Images I never wanted to remember
flashed through my mind one after another.
"When I heard this sound,
the people who had become those things all gathered in the dining hall.
......Yohei left after hearing it, too."
For a moment,
I regretted saying his name.
But there was no taking it back now.
"I think...
they probably won't come this way right now.
If we move while they're distracted,
we can put our plan into action."
Even I was surprised by how calm my voice sounded.
The high-pitched ringing
still echoed deep inside my head.
It felt like a warning—
and at the same time,
like a signal telling us to run.
Yabe-san let out a short breath
and peered outside through the gap in the sliding door once more.

Yabe-san placed her hand on the sliding door
and eased it open just a fraction.
At that very moment—
—clack... clack...
From somewhere down the corridor,
which had been almost unnaturally silent until now,
came the sound of footsteps.
And not just one person.
There were several of them.
"......!"
Instinctively,
both of us froze.
Then, a hollow voice,
clinging to the air,
reached our ears.
"Aaaahhh——......"
"......Help me......"
The words sounded as though
they had been squeezed out from the depths of someone's throat,
utterly devoid of life.
Yabe-san quietly pulled her hand back,
and we pressed ourselves into the shadow beside the door.
A few moments later,
figures drifted past the corridor.
A middle-aged man and woman.
Their clothes were in disarray,
their steps unsteady.
Their faces looked as though
they ought to carry some kind of meaning,
some trace of who they once were—
yet their eyes reflected nothing at all.
The footsteps gradually faded away,
and the voices, too,
melted into silence.
A while later,
Yabe-san gave me a small signal with her chin.
We stepped out into the hallway.
Turning right,
we headed toward the staircase,
then made a U-turn to the left and stopped at the top of the stairs.
—The smell hit me at once.
On the floor to our right,
the boards were smeared with the waste those things had expelled.
The stench was overwhelming—
rotting flesh mixed with a sickeningly sweet, metallic odor.
My stomach tightened.
They had come at us from the attic earlier.
Without thinking,
my eyes swept across the exposed beams above,
where there were no ceiling panels.
...Nothing.
No one was moving.
The stairs themselves were littered with foul muck,
mixed with what looked like strands of hair and fragments of bone,
giving off an unbearable stench.
Keeping low and carefully avoiding the filth,
we made our way down,
one step at a time,
watching the lower floor for any sign of movement.
Halfway down,
the intersection ahead came into view.
And there—
from left to right—
the people who had been taken by those things
shuffled across the corridor,
their hollow voices drifting through the darkness.
Glasses.
Jeans.
—It was Uchida-san, the doctor.
Just moments ago,
she had still been human.
I had watched it happen with my own eyes.
Something clenched deep inside my chest.
Then I saw another familiar face—
Takizawa-san,
the leader of my group.
Even Takizawa-san...
the man who had tried to protect me
in the kitchen of the old lodge.
It became hard to breathe.
Only after every one of them had passed
did we begin descending the stairs once more.
And then—
"...We've been called again..."
Just as we reached the bottom of the stairs,
a voice came from behind us on the left.
My heart jumped.
I instinctively turned around.
It was Akane-san.
Her face was pale,
and her gaze drifted somewhere into empty space.
"...Can't be helped... let's go..."
Yabe-san replied,
her voice just as hollow.
"...You go on ahead..."
"...Okay... we'll be waiting..."
Akane-san answered,
then began to stagger away.
A moment later,
she turned right at the intersection
and disappeared in the direction of the dining hall.
The instant she was gone,
Yabe-san and I lowered ourselves
and moved all at once.
We crossed the intersection
and took cover behind the corner of the gift shop.
—From here,
we couldn't be seen from the dining hall.
We hurried to the shoe lockers on the left.
Mari-senpai's shoes were right next to mine.
The moment I saw them,
a sharp pain tightened in my chest.
-...They're not going to need them anymore.
Yabe-san's words came back to me,
the ones she had said when I told her I didn't have a raincoat.
—This isn't the time to be crushed by grief.
Forcing myself to keep moving,
I slipped my feet into my hiking boots.
I tied the laces,
but left them deliberately loose.
—There was no telling when someone we knew might find us.
We didn't have the luxury of taking our time.
I carefully slid open the entrance door
and stepped outside.
The wind hit us at once.
It was raining,
but not hard enough to be called a downpour.
I closed the door again,
making as little noise as possible.
Then we moved to the left,
keeping close to the side of the building.
Staying low.
Up to our right,
the windows of the dining hall glowed.
Yellow light spilled out into the darkness.
And within that light—
something's shadow
swayed slowly back and forth.

Up to our right was the dining hall window.
The pale light spilling from its frame cut through the darkness at an unnatural angle.
Keeping close to the wall,
I crouched and moved beneath it.
Even though I was holding my breath,
the sound of my own heartbeat echoed absurdly loud in my ears.
—What was happening inside the dining hall right now?
No matter how hard I tried not to think about it,
my imagination kept filling in the blanks on its own.
Was Yohei in there?
What about the others?
And...
Was Mari-senpai there too?
The moment her name crossed my mind,
something deep inside my chest tightened.
No.
That couldn't be true.
I didn't want it to be.
Of all people,
I didn't want Mari-senpai to be in there—
Run.
Pretend you never saw any of this.
If you do that,
maybe you can still avoid losing something important.
But another voice whispered back.
What if...
What if she really is in there?
Are you going to run away without knowing?
Will you truly have no regrets?
Inside my head,
the two sides of me collided head-on.
The part of me that wanted to run,
and the part that needed to know.
Reason and curiosity.
Fear and attachment.
—It's dark out here.
They shouldn't be able to see us from inside the dining hall.
Suddenly,
I remembered the time Akane-san had peeked into the dining hall.
Back then too,
the world outside had been swallowed by darkness,

As long as we stayed outside,
our presence would simply melt into the night.
Just a little.
Just one quick look.
That was the excuse I gave myself
as I quietly placed a hand on the window frame.
Stretching my neck,
I let only my gaze slip inside.
—And then I saw it.
Everyone gathered in the dining hall wore the same expressionless faces.
Two antennae extended from their foreheads,
connecting to one another,

Takamura-san was there.
The man in his seventies from the tent group, too.
...And Morishita-san,

That little kid who had innocently shouted,

The three college students

Mari-senpai.

two antennae extending from her forehead,
linked with someone else's,
swaying quietly,
rhythmically,
as though following some unseen pulse.
For one impossible moment,
I wondered if she'd turn around if I called to her.
If I said her name,
would she give me that familiar, troubled smile?
The thought was absurd—
and yet it crossed my mind all the same.
The very next instant,
something inside my chest seemed to collapse.
I couldn't breathe.
My vision blurred.
I shut my eyes,
nearly dropping to my knees right there.
"What are you doing...? Come on."
Yabe-san's low voice came from behind me.
A hand gripped my shoulder,
pulling me back into reality.
Holding onto the pain in my chest,
I crouched low and started moving forward once more,
the light from the dining hall still at my back.
—I didn't look back.
Or maybe...
I simply couldn't.
Once we had passed beneath the dining hall window,
the outer wall of the lodge grew even darker.
I kept low,
moving along with my body pressed against the wall.
The ground beneath my feet had turned to mud.
Every time my boots threatened to make a wet, squelching sound,
my whole body tensed on instinct.
From inside the dining hall,
that same irregular murmur still drifted out.
It was neither voices nor breathing—
something in between.
Now and then,
a sound like something being dragged across the floor
mixed into the noise.
—Don't look.
The more I told myself that,

The people swaying together,
their antennae linked.
Mari-senpai's expressionless face.
I bit down on my lip
and forced my eyes back to the path ahead.
The corner of the building came into view.
Beyond it

the smell began to change.

burnt residue,

...Nothing.

—If I blew this place up...

Quietly,

Beyond it

At the point where the covered section met the outdoor staircase,

Not yet.
Passing by the staircase,

I turned right.

On my right

The power room.

...Nothing.
Just as I thought,

Then,

footsteps echoed somewhere in the distance.
Heavy,
dragging footsteps.
Someone—
or something—

without a doubt,

—Now.
I set my backpack down in front of the steel door.
Beside me,
Yabe-san lowered hers as well.
"Please wait here..."
I told her quietly,
took one small breath to steady myself,
and started back the way we had come.
I turned left around the corner of the building and headed for the staircase attached to the structure on my left.
The back of the staircase came into view first.
I walked around to the front and started climbing without hesitation.
Rainwater streamed down the metal steps like a small waterfall.
I placed each foot carefully, testing every step.
I wasn't even thinking about whether one of those things might be hiding somewhere.
It wasn't that I'd given up or thought, Whatever happens, happens.
My body was simply moving on its own.
The sliding door on the second floor was right there.
I reached out and opened it just enough to avoid making any noise.
The corridor beyond was completely silent.
—We can make it.
I slipped inside and stepped into the hallway.
Water dripped from my clothes, leaving dark patches on the wooden floor.
I wasn't thinking about hidden monsters anymore.
At this point, everything was in fate's hands.

The closet where Mari-senpai had been hiding earlier was still there.

I couldn't afford to hesitate here.
I kept moving.
The passage running through the center appeared to taper off into the distance,

Suzuki Sho-san had taken down the plywood panel there so we could meet up with Kobayashi Ren-san and the others on the second floor.

Shorts.
Bare feet.

Trekking pants.
A fleece over my wool shirt.

The place where Tsuruoka-san had set Naoki-kun on fire,

The entrance above the storage room.
――I can make it.

Mari-senpai's expressionless face.

The kitchen.

And there she was.
Moving together with the others,
drawn into the collective.
My nose began to sting,

Before I knew it,

――Goodbye, Mari-senpai.
At that moment,
something inside me changed.
I swallowed down the grief and the pain,
forcing myself to stand firm.

The tears wouldn't stop.

Something at the edge of my vision suddenly stretched out from the dining hall toward the gap in the counter.

Far too well.

—Heavy.

Suzuki Sho-san had turned on the lights in here with that very switch.
I reached for the switch panel

Beyond the generator,

The fire pump was on my left, the generator on my right.

Ahead of me stood a large, gray cylindrical Sunpot heater— the kind you often see in Japanese schools. Passing it, I walked by the workbench on my left and made my way to the steel door at the far end.
Yabe-san should be outside.

A Yabe-san no longer shaped like a person.
A voice that sounded the same,
