Rainwear

silence hanging over the corridor, the entire building suddenly creaked with a low groan.
Creeeak...
The vibration resonated deep in my stomach, as though some gigantic hand outside had pressed against the lodge itself.
"...I wonder when this typhoon is finally going to let up."
Uchida-san said, unable to hide the anxiety in her voice.
"With weather like this... there's no way we can just make a run for it outside."
Ren Kobayashi-san folded his arms and glanced up toward the ceiling.
Rain... wind...
At that moment—
Something suddenly clicked inside my head.
Rain gear...? Rainwear...?
That's it.
The creatures attached their tentacles to people and transformed into them.
If that was true... then wouldn't it be harder for them to get in if they couldn't touch bare skin directly?
I suddenly lifted my head.
"Everyone... um, could you listen for a second?"
All eyes turned toward me at once, and the air tightened with anticipation.
"Those things... they transform by attaching themselves to a person's body, right?
In that case..."
I looked around at everyone.
"If we were wearing rainwear... wouldn't it make us harder to attack?"
　 　For a moment, no one moved.
Then a murmur spread through the corridor—something caught between hope and uncertainty.
"...I-I see... if they can't touch us directly..."
Ren Kobayashi-san unfolded his arms and nodded thoughtfully, one hand resting on his chin.
Then—
"YES!!"
Glasses-and-Jeans suddenly shouted, his voice exploding through the room.
"What a relief! My pack's right over there!"
The outburst was so unexpectedly cheerful amid the despair that everyone instinctively turned to look at him.
Glasses-and-Jeans glanced toward the shelf where his backpack had been stored.
He was genuinely excited.
His reaction naturally drew my gaze in the same direction.
The shelf stood near the stairway, on a section of floor raised slightly above the corridor.
Two backpacks sat neatly side by side.
One was a mountaineering pack with a navy-blue body accented with black, while the other was a subdued olive-green pack.
The second one probably belonged to the athletic guy who had been killed in the dining hall.
So protecting himself matters more than losing his partner...
Well... maybe that's only natural.
Maybe they hadn't really been that close after all.
Or maybe they were just the kind of friends who only seemed close on the surface.
That thought crossed my mind.
One of the women in their twenties gave a small laugh.
"I think we've got rainwear in our room!"
The other nodded, a hint of relief appearing on her face.
Hearing that, Uchida-san's expression brightened slightly as well.
"You're right. I probably have mine too."
The young man from the female-led couple, however, remained silent.
Perhaps he was still carrying the grief of losing the woman he loved.
He showed no sign of smiling, lost in thought.
Seeing him like that, I couldn't help feeling sorry for him, and a heaviness settled in my chest.
Besides, his backpack had been left in the dining hall.
And if one of those things was still in there, there was no way he could safely retrieve it.
Aya Morishita-san still looked uneasy.
That makes sense.
Morishita-san worked part-time at the lodge, so she might have to go to the staff room or the employees' sleeping quarters to retrieve her backpack.
And by now, the lodge owner—who had become one of those things—might already have gotten inside.
That was what I was thinking.
Ren Kobayashi-san remained expressionless, quietly observing the situation.
"But... there could still be something over there. We can't go get them by ourselves."
One of the women in their twenties murmured softly.
"Then... my rainwear is in my room too. Why don't we go together?"
When I suggested it, the two women nodded.
For the first time in a while, they seemed to regain a little bit of courage.
Then it happened.
"Sho."
Tsuruoka-san called my name in a heavy voice.
His Owari accent seemed stronger than usual.
I turned toward him.
"While you're at it... would you bring mine too?"
"Huh? But I don't even know where your rainwear is."
I had assumed Tsuruoka-san would be coming with us himself.
Honestly, part of me was thinking that he ought to go get his own rainwear.
Tsuruoka-san waved a hand dismissively.
"Just bring the whole backpack. I know where it is."
I already knew it, but apparently this was the kind of person who didn't mind making someone else carry a heavy load for his sake.
"Then... why don't you go get it yourself, Tsuruoka-san?"
The moment the words left my mouth, I surprised even myself.
I couldn't believe I'd said something like that to someone I was uncomfortable around.
Had seeing Tsuruoka-san so frightened all this time made me start looking down on him?
When I asked, he hesitated for a moment.
Then he lowered his gaze and spoke quietly.
"The lodge kid's already gone to get the registry. Somebody's gotta stay here, doesn't he?
What're we supposed to do, leave all these people here alone?"
His reasoning was sound enough—
but there was a faint tremor beneath his words.
I thought to myself.
...If things were normal, he'd probably be the type who'd want to go wherever the women were.
But now... there were no creatures here.
Over in those rooms, though... there might be.
In other words—
he was scared.
In the end, jobs like this always seem to fall to me...
Another strong gust of wind swept through the corridor, causing the shadows cast by the lights to sway.
Our emotions seemed just as unsteady, tossed about by the wind.

The two women in their twenties, Uchida-san, and I exchanged uneasy glances before cautiously stepping out into the corridor.
Every creak of the floorboards put us on edge as we scanned our surroundings, watching for any sign that one of those things might be lurking nearby.
We moved toward the staircase along the lowered passageway that ran between the sleeping areas.
The fear hadn't gone away, but not being alone gave me at least a little strength. And the fact that I was with three beautiful women certainly didn't hurt.
I couldn't imagine risking my life for another man, but for some reason, when it came to women, I felt an urge to protect them, even at my own expense.
...Though if it really came down to it, I had no idea what I would actually do. I didn't even know how I might react in the moment.
But what would Mari-senpai think if she saw me walking around with three women like this?
Would she get jealous? Would she end up disliking me because of it?
...Ah, where is Mari-senpai right now?
...Is she safe?
...Please. Let her be safe...
Let her be safe...

We turned left at the staircase, then right at the end of the corridor, pausing near the stairwell to listen and look down below.
So far, there seemed to be no sign of movement on the floor beneath us. There were no voices, no commotion—not even the slightest sound. The only thing we could hear was the rain and wind battering the roof overhead.
We crossed past the stairwell and turned right again at the end of the corridor.
As we passed the six-person room, I felt my heart rise into my throat.
An attack could come from anywhere—that fear weighed heavily on my shoulders.
At last we reached the area near the four-person rooms, where a small hallway branched off to the left.
Four wooden sliding doors lined each side of the narrow passage.
Standing there, I couldn't help remembering the moment we had first tried to enter our room.
A heavy feeling settled in my chest as my thoughts drifted to the room at the far end—Asagiri.
—The room belonging to that suspicious middle-aged pair we'd noticed earlier.
When the strong-willed middle-aged woman had been taken in the washroom, I remembered the man desperately calling out for her, demanding to know where she was.
The memory made my chest tighten.
She had come back as one of those things and stayed with him afterward.
Where were the two of them now?
What were they doing?
Had they both become one of those things by now...?

The three women and I made our way down the hallway lined with four-person rooms, checking the doors on either side.
Each room had a wooden plaque mounted above the entrance displaying its name.
The first pair of rooms were Unkai on the left and Gekkou on the right. Beyond them were Hinode on the left and Seppou on the right—the rooms we had been assigned to. Farther down the corridor were Shirakaba on the left and Ganhou on the right. At the very end were Asagiri on the left and Kazane on the right.
—So that suspicious middle-aged pair had been staying in Asagiri after all...
One of the young women spoke in a low voice.
"We're in Shirakaba."
Uchida-san quietly replied,
"I'm in Kazane."
"Then let's start from the far end," I said softly.
The four of us headed toward Kazane—Uchida-san's room at the far end on the right.
"Wait here for a moment, please."
I stopped in the hallway and cautiously listened from outside the sliding door. Everything was quiet. There was no sign of movement inside.
—For someone who should have been the most frightened person here, I was somehow acting as though I had to protect these women.
If the people with me had all been men, I probably would have just followed behind them instead.
Maybe I was simply a sucker for women after all...
"Stay back," I instructed quietly as I slid the door open and checked the room by myself.
Unlike the room I had been assigned to, this one had a window. Its glass reflected both the interior and my own figure standing in the doorway.
Four neatly folded futons were lined up on the tatami floor.
The room was probably about six tatami mats in size.
When I turned around, I saw a backpack propped against the wall beside the entrance.
It was a subdued dark green, tall and slim in design.
There were no unnecessary decorations—just a simple, functional mountaineering pack.
Its sturdy yet lightweight appearance seemed perfectly suited to Uchida-san's calm and intelligent demeanor.
"It's safe," I said.
I let Uchida-san go in and retrieve her backpack.
Without a word, she stepped inside. A short while later, she emerged carrying a pastel-green rain suit.
Next was Shirakaba—the room belonging to the two women in their twenties, second from the end on the left.
As before, I carefully listened for any sign of movement before cautiously opening the door.
Like my room, this one had no window.
It was a small tatami room, about six mats in size.
After confirming that it was quiet and safe, I briefly relayed that to the two women.
They slipped inside and soon returned with their rain gear.
The woman who had mentioned liking a certain actor carried a mint-blue rain suit, while her soft-spoken friend had a lavender one.
That left only my room—Hinode.
I steadied my breathing at the door and checked inside without making a sound.
Quiet.
I stepped inside and opened the top lid of my navy Millet backpack, retrieving my rain suit. Mine was a deep navy color.
—I remembered Tsuruoka-san making fun of it back at Hakubajiri.
"Still, a Millet pack? Kinda looks like a girl's backpack, doesn't it?"
After grabbing my rain suit, I slung Tsuruoka-san's green backpack over one shoulder and stepped back out into the hallway.
—Maybe Mari-senpai and the others are hiding in their room.
"Excuse me, could you wait here a moment? Some friends of mine were supposed to be staying in this room. I want to check if they're hiding inside."
With that, I pressed my ear against the sliding door.
All I could hear was the rain drumming against the roof and the wind outside. No sound came from within.
Carefully, I slid the door open.
There was a window on the opposite wall. Outside was darkness, and my own reflection stared back at me in the glass.
The room was a bare six-tatami room. Empty. There was no one inside.
—If Mari-senpai had been here, we could've escaped together...
My heart sank. Worry for Mari-senpai tightened painfully in my chest.
But I couldn't stand here forever.
Quietly, I slid the door of Room Seppo closed once more.
Exchanging glances, we made our way back toward the others, staying alert and speaking little as we went.
The tension in my shoulders hadn't disappeared. At any moment, one of those things could come at us from anywhere.
Still, now that we had our rain gear, it felt as though a faint glimmer of hope had begun to break through the darkness.
If I found Mari-senpai, I'd tell her about the rain gear, and together we'd do everything we could to survive.

And then, carrying our rain gear, Ayako Uchida-san, the two women in their twenties, and I returned to where Ren Kobayashi-san, Aya Morishita-san, Glasses-and-Jeans, and the man from the female-led couple were waiting.
Glasses-and-Jeans had already opened his backpack and was rummaging through its contents.
Uchida-san and the two young women quickly began putting on their rain gear as well.
The rustle of fabric and the occasional sharp intake of breath brought a small sense of comfort amid the tension.
Tsuruoka-san opened his own pack, pulled out a black rain jacket, and began putting it on without hesitation.
"See? Told ya. This one's mine. A North Face Mountain Jacket. One of the newer models, too. Gore-Tex. Rain and wind don't stand a chance."
As he straightened the jacket proudly, the others couldn't help but glance his way.
For a brief moment, his self-satisfied grin seemed to ease the tension hanging over the group.
Then Aya-san's shoulders began to tremble.
"...I don't have any rain gear... M-my backpack... it's downstairs... and down there... down there are those things... I-I can't... I can't..."
As the words left her mouth, her knees gave way and she broke down crying.
Her quiet sobs echoed clearly through the silence.
Ren Kobayashi-san and Ayako Uchida-san desperately tried to comfort her. Uchida-san, already wearing her rain gear, rubbed Aya-san's back while speaking softly. But no one offered to go down to the first floor, where those things might be waiting.
Their words felt hollow because of it.
Uchida-san didn't seem willing to give up her own rain gear, either.
I hesitated.
It's all my fault... this whole mess... everyone's terrified because of it... and I'm the one who found that thing—the black fetus-like creature—in the first place.
The weight of that responsibility settled heavily in my chest.
After a moment of indecision, I held out my rain gear to Aya-san.
"...Morishita-san, please take mine."
Aya-san looked startled, then nodded, her expression brightening just a little.
Part of me secretly wished she'd refuse.
Still, perhaps she understood the kindness behind the gesture, because although she continued crying, she seemed noticeably calmer afterward.
Ren Kobayashi-san silently placed a hand on her shoulder in support.
Meanwhile, Glasses-and-Jeans paid no attention to any of it and continued digging through his backpack in search of his own rain gear.
From his backpack, Glasses-and-Jeans finally pulled out an orange rain suit.
The two women in their twenties started toward Aya-san after putting on their own rain gear, but I gently stopped them.
"Take it easy, Aya-san. You don't have to force yourself right now. Everyone here will look out for you..."
Aya-san gave a small nod.
Unable even to wipe away her tears, she simply sat there trembling.
Inside my chest, my determination to protect everyone mingled with the crushing weight of knowing that this terror had begun because of me.
