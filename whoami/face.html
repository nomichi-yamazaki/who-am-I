Face

It happened then.
The person directly beneath me—the one of whom I could see only the top of the head—suddenly looked up.
The two tentacles extending from the glowing forehead, connected to the others around him, were tugged taut.
The one looking up was the mountain lodge owner.
I froze like a frog mesmerized by a snake.
It felt as though a single instant had stretched into eternity.
—This is bad! This is bad!
My mind screamed at me, yet my body would not move.
The two tentacles extending from the lodge owner’s forehead released the tentacles of the people beside him and began retracting back into his forehead.
At the same time, his face seemed to grow larger.
—He’s coming closer!
This is bad! This is bad!
At last I snapped back to myself and jerked backward in panic.
—Our eyes met.
It’s too late to hide now.
Run! Run!
My heart pounded violently.
Blood rushed to my head, and my face burned hot.
I scrambled backward on all fours, moving my hands and feet frantically.
It was dark around me.
The only light came from the charred hole in the dining hall ceiling and the faint glow filtering in from the storage-room opening I had climbed through to reach the crawlspace.
I hadn’t had the spare attention to bring a flashlight.
The space above and below was cramped, with pillars standing everywhere around me.
I was completely exposed, wearing nothing but a T-shirt and shorts.
My feet were bare.
I could feel the gritty dust piled across the beams beneath my hands and feet.
Light from the hole ahead streamed into the crawlspace, and only within that beam could I see clouds of dust swirling thickly through the air.
The dust I had inhaled made my throat feel raw.
My foot struck a pillar.
To change direction toward a beam extending off to the right, I stretched my right leg sideways, searching for the crossbeam at a right angle.
Then, in the very next instant, the yellow light shining down from the ceiling vanished, swallowed by darkness.
I looked toward the hole above the dining hall ceiling.
—What happened!?
What happened!?
My thoughts were spinning at full speed, yet no answer came.
Unable even to tell whether it was the right thing to do, I stopped moving and held my breath in hiding.
I couldn’t think calmly.
I just felt as though there were eyes in the darkness, staring straight at me.
I wanted to run.
But my feet felt glued to the beam beneath me.
Every breath dragged dust into my throat.
And right in front of me, I felt as though something unnervingly real was drawing closer.
In that instant, it felt as though time, sound, even the air itself had stopped.
“What is it?”
The moment I held my breath, a voice came from below.
It sounded like the voice of the man in his seventies.
“It’s too dark. I can’t see anything.”
Another voice answered from very close by.
It was the lodge owner’s voice.
As my eyes adjusted, I began to make out the surroundings little by little.
And then—
a face.
A face was blocking the hole in the ceiling.
I couldn’t see clearly in the darkness, but it had to be the lodge owner’s face.
My heart nearly leapt out of my chest.
—Run!
If you don’t run, you’ll end up like them too!
My mind screamed the warning at me.
I panicked and began scrambling away.
Planting my outstretched right foot onto the beam to my right, I hunched my body as low as possible and reached out with my right hand to grab the next beam.
I was panicking so badly that when I tried to place my left foot onto the beam to the right, I missed it and stepped onto a wooden lattice suspended from above by thin metal rods.
The wood creaked.
Boards cracked.
Dust burst into the air with its dry, choking smell.
The ceiling beneath my feet warped dangerously.
I felt my body beginning to sink downward.
—At this rate, I’m going to crash through the ceiling.
In a panic, I pulled my left leg back up using both hands gripping the beam and my right foot, hauling myself back onto the beam above.
Even though my eyes had adjusted somewhat, it was still too dark to see clearly.
Mekimekimeki…
Baribaribari…
A noise erupted behind me to the left, and I instinctively turned toward it.
Cracks were spreading across the ceiling in every direction, and light from below streamed through them.
In that fractured light and shadow between the darkness above and the dining hall beneath, I saw a face.
The lodge owner’s face.
And beneath it was a long neck connected to him, lined on both sides with countless tentacles like the legs of a centipede.

The countless legs lining both sides of the lodge owner’s neck gripped the beams and moved in a steady rhythm, carrying the owner’s face at the tip closer and closer toward me.
The owner’s face disappeared completely into the darkness of the crawlspace above the ceiling, until it looked like nothing more than a black mass.
—Run! Run!
Alarms were screaming inside my head.
I faced forward—though all that lay ahead was darkness—and desperately scrambled across the beams, forcing my hands and feet to move.
If I fell to the left or the right, I would crash straight through the ceiling below.
And beneath me, countless things were waiting.
GONK!
My head slammed into something, and light burst behind my eyelids.
I hadn’t been able to see in the darkness and had smashed straight into a pillar ahead of me.
But I couldn’t afford to stop for something like that.
Reaching my left hand toward a beam to the left, then stretching my right hand farther toward another beam beyond the pillar, I pulled myself over it one leg at a time.
“Bon Curry, huh… It’s okay. It’s me…”
The lodge owner’s voice came from immediately behind me.
—He’s trying to lure me in.
My heart felt ready to burst out of my mouth from sheer terror.
Desperately moving my hands and feet, I crawled toward the yellow light from the storage room, which looked distorted into a trapezoid by the perspective.
My throat felt dry and scratchy, like I was about to start coughing at any second.
Kasakasa… kasakasa…
Behind me came the sound of countless legs skittering across the beams.
—What if it catches up to me?
What if it catches me, clings to my body, and turns me into one of those things?
My chest hurt with a crushing tightness, and I could feel tears blurring my eyes.
I must have been breathing in dust.
Even the inside of my mouth felt dry and gritty.
I forced my hands and feet onward, desperately crawling along the beams.
The next pillar ahead was barely visible in the faint light spilling from the storage room, so this time I managed not to smash my head into it.
I shifted my left hand onto the beam to the left and reached my right hand toward the beam beyond the pillar—
—Ahh!
My left foot slipped again.
There was a heavy bong! as one of the metal rods suspending the ceiling gave way, and the surface beneath my foot suddenly sagged downward.
My right foot was pulled off the beam along with my left.
—If there’s an inspection hatch, then the crawlspace is obviously meant to be inspected, so why the hell would they build a ceiling too weak for a person to stand on?!
My mind filled with useless, irrational anger.
But I had no time to dwell on that.
If I didn’t hurry, the lodge owner’s head would catch me, latch onto me, and turn me into one of those things too.
With both feet slipping through, I kicked hard against the ceiling beneath me.
Baki-baki!
The ceiling buckled downward in a warped, uneven shape with loud cracking noises.
Even so, some of the metal rods still held, keeping me from crashing all the way through.
I managed to get back onto the beam and desperately pushed forward.
The opening to the storage room was getting closer from ahead on my left.
Growing larger.
Coming closer.
I’m there!
Hurry! Hurry! Hurry!
In a panic, I shoved my upper body into the opening.
I fell headfirst downward.
My body slammed into a stack of cardboard boxes, and I felt one crumple beneath my stomach with a crushing groan.
—If I land on my head, I could break my neck!
The thought flashed through my mind in an instant, and I threw both arms downward to catch myself.
My legs slipped free from the opening, and I felt my body flipping backward.
—Pain shot through me!
My side smashed into the shelf on the opposite wall.
My feet crushed the cardboard box on the top shelf.
My left hand hit the floor, and my body collapsed sideways to the left.
I slammed into the ground, bounced from the impact, and rolled.
—I can’t stay down here doing this!
I had to close the hatch immediately, or it would come in through the opening.
This place was sealed shut.
The door was locked from the outside.
There was nowhere to run.
Pulling both legs underneath me, I scrambled to my feet.
I planted my right foot onto the shelf to my right and pushed myself upward.
My hand grabbed the shelf above.
I lifted myself higher, placing my left foot onto the upper shelf.
Then, with my left hand, I grabbed the hanging hatch door and shoved it upward.
A face suddenly emerged from the dark square opening above me—the lodge owner’s face.
In a panic, I shoved the hatch upward, forcing the face back.
But something on the other side pushed back too, and the hatch started to open again.
I threw my weight against it and forced it shut.
Again, pressure came from the other side, almost pushing it open.
Stretching out my right hand, I fumbled toward the flathead screwdriver I had left on top of the cardboard boxes.
Grabbing it, I pushed upward with all my strength using my left hand, and the instant the hatch slammed fully shut, I jammed the latch closed with the screwdriver.
The strength drained from my body.
I slipped down to the lower shelf and collapsed onto the floor.
Hah… hah… hah… hah…
My breathing was ragged.
I could feel tears blurring my eyes.
My heart pounded violently in my chest.
My whole body trembled uncontrollably.

Inside the storage room, it was quiet—so quiet it felt like an entirely different world.
Yet deep inside my ears, the memory of those skittering footsteps crawling across the beams still echoed and scraped around like lingering afterimages of sound.
The creaking of the crawlspace boards, the wet sound of flesh dragging against itself, someone’s faint groans—everything blended together inside my head and refused to leave.
But for now, all I could do was stay silent and hold my breath inside this tiny space.
…Was that really the lodge owner?
His neck had… stretched… and there had been so many legs.
But the voice had definitely been his.
“Bon Curry, huh? …It’s okay. It’s me.”
Those words kept replaying in my ears.
It’s me.
Why had it said that?
Was that… thing trying to reassure me?
Or had it read my memories and simply imitated him?

Tsuruoka-san’s words came back to me.
“That thing’s intelligent enough to build a UFO. Maybe it’s just pretending to be human like this…!”
“It’s obvious, ain’t it? It was gonna attack everyone in here and turn us all into its own kind.”

“Things” take on human form.
They reproduce the faces we know, the voices we know, even the smallest gestures… all of it.
—They steal the thoughts of the original person and pretend to be them to lure people in.
If you hadn’t seen the moment one of those things became that person—if you mistook it for the real one and let your guard down for even an instant—it would probably lash out with its tentacles, cling to you, melt your face away, and turn you into one of those horrifying things before you even realized what was happening.

If those things really did imitate the original person, then Mari-senpai, Youhei, Yabe-san… even Takizawa-san… might already have become “those things” too.
The instant that thought crossed my mind, something hot surged up from deep inside my stomach.
The back of my throat burned painfully.
No.
I don’t want that.
I can’t accept that.
Mari-senpai was strong.
There was no way someone like her could be taken so easily.
But…
Those things weren’t something you could explain away with logic like that.
Strength and weakness had nothing to do with it.
Just one brief moment of carelessness.
One instant of hesitation.
And you became something else entirely.
If Mari-senpai became one of them and appeared in front of me… what would I do?
What if she smiled and said, “Shou, run”?
Should I trust that voice and run?
Or—
Somewhere inside my head, I heard someone crying.
It took me a few seconds to realize the sound was my own.
In the darkness, I bit my lip and covered my ears with both hands.
All I could hear was my heartbeat.
Thump.
Thump.
Thump.
Proof that I was still human.
Clinging to that alone, I held my breath in silence.
“…Mari-senpai…”
The tiny whisper of her name trembled like a prayer.
Please… let her still be safe.
Please… let her still remain human.
In the darkness, my wish dissolved and vanished like a fading breath.
