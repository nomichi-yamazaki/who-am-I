Plan

Yabe-san didn't answer right away.
She hugged her knees and stared down at the tatami floor in silence for a while.
"...Hey, Sakuma-kun."
Her voice was low.
"What do you think will happen if we just run away?"
I couldn't answer.
It felt like she had forced me to face the very thing I'd been trying not to think about.
"If we make it down the mountain, we might survive.
But... whatever happened here won't stay on this mountain."
Yabe-san slowly raised her head.
"It could spread across all of Japan.
Maybe even the whole world."
I instinctively shook my head.
"...That's not something people like us can do anything about, is it?"
It sounded less like a refusal and more like a cry for help.
"I know,"
Yabe-san said immediately.
"I'm no hero, either.
Honestly, I'm terrified... and I've already been through more than enough."
For a moment, her voice trembled.
"But now I know.
After seeing those things, I understand what's happening. If we leave this alone, humanity will go extinct."
I couldn't say a word.
Mari-senpai's face resurfaced in my mind.
"If I do nothing...
I'll probably carry that regret for the rest of my life."
Yabe-san continued, as though she were trying to convince herself as much as me.
"That doesn't mean I know what we should do...
But at the very least, we can't just walk away and leave it like this."
Silence hung between us for a while.
"...You mean, defeat them?"
I finally asked.
"I know they're not the kind of enemy you can casually say you'll 'defeat.'"
Yabe-san gave a bitter smile.
"To be honest... a word like 'defeat' feels completely hollow in a situation like this."
She let out a small sigh.
"So instead, I want to understand them.
Sakuma-kun, you've encountered them yourself.
Have you learned anything about those things?"

Even as I faced Yabe-san,
another scene kept replaying in my mind.
—Yohei's voice.
"But... did you already become one of those things?..."
That question wouldn't leave me alone.
No matter how hard I tried, I couldn't stop thinking about it.
If those things could clearly distinguish humans from their own kind,
there should have been no reason to ask something like that.
That wasn't a question born of certainty.
It was the voice of someone who doubted.
I spoke quietly.
"...Maybe those things don't know, either."
Yabe-san frowned slightly.
"They don't know?"
"Yeah.
Whether the person standing in front of them is still human,
or has already become one of their own...
Maybe even they can't be completely sure.
I ran into Yohei earlier...
He turned out to be one of those things, but he asked me...
...'did you become one of those things?'
That's why I think so..."
"Huh... For an electrician, you really do think things through pretty logically, Sakuma-kun,"
Yabe-san said.
"...And one more thing..." I said, thinking as I spoke.
Everything that had happened at the mountain lodge
came flooding back to me.
The crawl space.
Dark, dusty,
a place where even breathing felt dangerous.
I remembered when the owner transformed
and chased me all the way up there.
We had been separated by nothing more than a single wooden board,
and I knew for certain that it was right there.
The creaking sounds.
The ragged breathing.
It had all been so close.
And yet...
it never attacked me.
The same thing happened with Yohei.
When he transformed,
he cornered me,
his neck stretching right up beside mine.
He was close enough to touch me,
close enough that he should have attacked me without hesitation.
But he... stopped.
And then—
there was Mari-senpai.
I had been standing right in front of her.
I know her eyes had found me.
And yet,
instead of attacking me,
she went after Suzuki Shou-san, who had been standing farther away.
I didn't know why.
I couldn't see any clear pattern.
But still...
I quietly opened my mouth.
"...Those things..."
I paused, searching for the right words.
"For some reason I can't explain...
there seem to be times when they attack people,
and times when they don't."
I was merely laying out my own experiences,
nothing more than simple facts.
And yet,
the atmosphere in the room shifted ever so slightly.
"Even when they're right next to someone,
they don't always attack.
It's almost like they're watching and waiting...
or maybe... hesitating."

I hesitated for a moment.
Fragments of memories,
pieces that had seemed unrelated before,
began connecting themselves in my mind.
"...I don't know if all of this is really connected, but..."
Yabe-san silently urged me to continue.
When that thing in the washroom transformed...
there was clearly some kind of trigger.
I chose my words carefully.
"The same thing happened in the dining hall.
The moment that man pointed a knife at one of the university students...
that's when the transformation began."
I took a breath.
"But after that...
when the lodge owner chased me after transforming...
he got incredibly close,
and yet he never attacked me."
Yabe-san's expression shifted slightly.
"And when I met Kobayashi Ren-san and the others on the second floor...
I noticed that Ren-san's ear canal looked... almost like an eye,
so I signaled everyone to keep their distance."
My voice grew quieter.
"But do you remember that guy from the tent campers?
The one who was always being told what to do by the woman he was with?
The moment he shouted that he couldn't believe Ren-san had killed someone he cared about,
and lunged at him...
that's when he started to transform."
My words began coming faster.
"The same thing happened with Yohei.
His neck stretched out...
it came right up beside me,
and still... he didn't attack."
The back of my throat stung.
"And then...
those two guys who butted in when we opened the alien corpse by the entrance...
when Uchida-san tried to protect Morishita-san and swung a metal pipe...
the transformations spread one after another."
At last, my voice fell to almost a whisper.
"...And that jet-black alien corpse we recovered—the one that looked like a fetus.
I was in the underground storage room with it,
but it never attacked me, either."
A brief silence followed.
"When I ran outside, it stretched out its arms and made those screeching noises...
but it never transformed,
and it never came after me."
I found myself shaking my head in confusion.
"If they really do see humans as enemies...
if their goal is truly to invade us...
then the moment they realize a human is nearby,
it wouldn't be strange for them to attack immediately..."
My words trailed off there.

The more I thought about it,
the less any of it seemed to make sense.
I couldn't piece it together, even in my own mind.
Yabe-san didn't say anything right away.
She lowered her eyes for a moment,
then looked back at me as if confirming something.
"...So, Sakuma-kun,"
she said quietly.
"When those things came near you,
how did you react?... Did you ever think about fighting back?"
For a moment, I was at a loss for words.
But I already knew my answer.
"...The thought of defeating them
never even crossed my mind."
The honesty in my own voice surprised me.
"I was just... scared.
All I could do was run..."
Yabe-san slowly nodded.
"...Maybe,"
she said after a pause,
her voice calm and thoughtful.
"Maybe those things only attack
when they believe they've been attacked first."

I froze.
"If we don't make the first move...
then they won't, either...?"
"I can't say for certain,"
Yabe-san said before continuing.
"But at the very least,
they never seemed like creatures that attacked indiscriminately."
Now that she mentioned it, the first time that jet-black, fetus-like thing appeared,
it had been surrounded on all sides with nowhere left to run before it began to transform.
The same thing had happened in the dining hall.
The chubby guy with glasses only started transforming the moment that man in beige—the one in his sixties who had threatened me with a knife and locked me in the storage room—pointed his knife at him.
On the second floor, Kobayashi Ren-san began to transform only after the man from that young female-led couple grabbed him.
And earlier, in the old building's dining hall, the transformation spread only after Uchida-san swung her metal pipe.
Maybe Yabe-san was right.
Perhaps those things only transformed when they were attacked,
or when they were cornered and overcome with fear.
The true nature of that fear
was finally beginning to take shape,
if only a little.
"I think... that's definitely possible,"
I said to Yabe-san.

The sound of rain beating against the roof suddenly grew louder.
Thud. Thud.
Heavy impacts, as though something were being hurled down from above.
Each time the wind howled,
the entire mountain lodge let out an unpleasant creak.
The fact that we were on the second floor
slowly sank into my mind as the inescapable reality of having nowhere left to run.

Those things
were not all-powerful monsters.
They hesitated.
They made mistakes.
And only under certain conditions
did they finally bare their fangs.
That realization
didn't bring me relief.
Instead,
it left a different kind of weight
settling deep within my chest.

A sudden gust of wind slammed against the lodge.
The wind howled beyond the walls,
and the wooden beams let out a low groan as the windows rattled violently.
It was as if the mountain lodge itself
was struggling to endure something.
—If we went outside now...
The very thought alone
sent a chill down my spine.

—One wrong move
could be the trigger.
That's why
we had to think carefully
about what to do next.

The rain showed no sign of stopping.
If anything,
it seemed to be growing fiercer by the minute.
Beyond the roof, there was a storm.
On the first floor, there were those things.
This single room on the second floor
felt as though it had been cut off from the rest of the world.
There was nowhere to run.
And so—
the only thing left for us to do
was think.

I let out a slow breath.
Even though we had learned more,
our situation hadn't become any easier.
"...But..."
Choosing my words carefully, I continued.
"Even if that's true...
how are we supposed to stop this?"
I deliberately avoided using the word *defeat*.
I still wasn't ready
to say something like that out loud.

"But if they're living creatures...
wouldn't they be afraid of fire?"
Her words caught me off guard.
Fire.
The idea itself was simple,
yet at that moment, I couldn't think of any argument against it.
"...There was gasoline in the engine room,"
I said.
I didn't even know why I had said it.
The thought had slipped out on its own.
For just a moment,
Yabe-san's eyes widened.
"...If we burned this place down completely..."
She drew a small breath.
"...we wouldn't make it out safely, either."
Neither of us said another word.
We both imagined what would come next.
The mountain lodge.
The flames.
A night with nowhere to run.
"..."
Yabe-san turned her gaze toward the old stove in the corner of the room.
It simply sat there,
existing in silence.
"...For now..."
She shook her head.
"For now, let's keep thinking.
We need to think this through while we're still alive."
I said nothing and merely nodded.
We still hadn't found an answer.
And yet, strangely enough...
the urge to run away from this place
had grown just a little weaker.
