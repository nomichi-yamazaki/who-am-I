Run!

—There was only one thing left to do.
Run.
My body threatened to lock up with fear, but I forced my legs forward.
The washroom was only a short distance away.
There was no telling what the grotesque thing that had once been Tsuruoka-san might do next.
If I took my eyes off it, even for a moment, it might catch me.
Pouring every ounce of strength into my body, I broke into a run again.
The roar of rain and wind, the groaning of wood, and my own ragged breathing blended together in the stormy night.
—Tsuruoka-san's been taken!
Tsuruoka-san's been turned into one of them!
Fear tightened every muscle in my body.
But the washroom exit was just outside the sliding door.
I had no choice but to head for it.
*Grrr-rattle...*
I hurriedly slid open the door I had closed earlier for safety.
The washroom came back into view.
The stainless-steel sinks stretching across the front reflected the dim light, making the room feel even more unnatural.
The mirrors showed nothing but my own shifting reflection as I moved forward, keeping watch for the distorted figure of "Tsuruoka-san" behind me.
—Tsuruoka-san is gone...
Another human had been taken from us.
Clenching every muscle, I sprinted toward the exit on the right.
The wet wooden slats beneath my feet were slippery.
One misplaced step and I felt as though I might crash to the floor.
Cold sweat ran down my back.
My heart hammered violently against my ribs.
—There's nobody left to help me...
What am I supposed to do?
The exit drew closer.
Behind me came the sounds of movement.
Then a faint *clink*—
as though something had brushed against the metal sinks.
Every sound magnified my fear.
I couldn't let my guard down for even a second.
—Just a little farther.
Just a little farther, and I could escape that thing.

For a brief instant, the thought crossed my mind that I should wash my hands after wiping myself.
But there was no time for that.
The moment I burst out through the washroom's open exit—
*"Heh-puh... Heh-puh..."*
The bizarre sound coming from my right made my heart jolt violently.
A freezing chill shot down my spine, and I found myself unable to move, as though I had been paralyzed.
I looked up.
A man whose entire body had been burned black was staggering toward me from the right side of the covered walkway beneath the plastic roof.
His movements were painfully slow, as though time itself had thickened around him.
Yet he was undeniably getting closer.
—Naoki-kun...?
Could he still be alive after being soaked in gasoline and set on fire?
Then I saw something even more horrifying.
Chunks of blackened material crumbled away from the surface of his body like pieces of charcoal.
And beneath them, Naoki-kun's face began to emerge.
His eyes were vacant.
His mouth hung in a twisted expression.
My heart nearly burst from my chest.
—What is happening...?
Has Naoki-kun become one of them too?
When the campers had begged to be let inside earlier, I had felt something strange.
Nobody had mentioned Naoki-kun's body lying beside the entrance.
Maybe the reason was simple.
Maybe Naoki-kun had already gotten back up.
Maybe he hadn't been there anymore.
The thought flashed through my mind instinctively.
*"Aaaahhh..."*
*"Help me..."*
*"What's happening to me...?"*
Then, from my left, more voices reached me.
I turned.
Tanaka-san, Kishida-san, and the strong-willed woman were approaching through the rain.
Their clothes were soaked.
Their bodies swayed as they staggered forward.
Neither their expressions nor their voices seemed truly human anymore.
They looked like wandering ghosts whose souls had already departed.
—They're trying to corner me!
If they trap me, I'll be covered in tentacles and turned into one of them in an instant!
I remembered Naoki-kun screaming in agony after nothing more than a severed piece of tentacle had stuck to his cheek.
Terrifying...
My spine trembled uncontrollably.
My body curled inward as though trying to protect my stomach.
My legs were shaking.
—Run!
I have to run!
Desperately, I forced my body to break free from its paralysis.
It had probably lasted only a second.
Yet it felt as though I had stood frozen there for hours.
The reality that "Things" were closing in from every direction threatened to freeze my entire body solid.
Fear turned my thoughts into a tangled mess.
I moved on pure instinct.
*"N-No...!"*
Before I could even finish shouting, I lunged toward the mountain hut's entrance and threw myself inside.
—I have to close it!
I have to close it!
If they reach me with those tentacles, it's over!
Rattle-rattle-rattle-rattle...
The sliding door slammed shut behind me.
For one brief moment, I thought I had escaped the horror outside.
But the rain hammering against the roof—and the faint footsteps moving along the walkway—never stopped assaulting my ears.
I rushed into the lodge and spun around, desperately shoving the sliding door closed.
My hands trembled.
I fumbled with the old-fashioned brass lock, guiding the threaded metal rod into place and twisting it again and again until it caught.
Would they break the door down?
Could they smash the windows and come inside?
I didn't know.
The Thing could change into anything.
It was terrifying.
My legs shook beneath me.
I had to deal with all of this alone.
Wearing nothing but a T-shirt and shorts.
Barefoot.
Completely defenseless.
"Please... don't come in..."
Rattle... Clatter...
The sliding door and windowpanes shuddered in their frames.
Outside, Naoki-kun, Tanaka-san, Kishida-san—and the strong-willed woman—appeared through the rain.
Their wet hands and feet slapped against the glass.
Their voices were low and distorted.
"Let us in..."
"Let us in..."
They repeated the words over and over.
My heart pounded wildly in my chest.
Driven by pure terror, I made my decision.
I had to run.
When I turned around, I saw the space beneath the staircase leading to the second floor—the same staircase where filth was scattered across the steps.
I started running.
The staircase slipped past on my right.
I glanced toward it.
The stairs were there.
The second floor... it'll be safer up there...
The thought flashed through my mind instinctively as I prepared to charge up the stairs.
"Kyaaaah!"
A woman's scream suddenly rang out from above.
My heart nearly stopped.
Whose voice was that!?
There were only four women left who might still be human.
Morishita Aya-san?
Uchida Ayako-san?
Yabe-san?
Or... could it be Mari-senpai...?
I didn't know.
I'd never heard Mari-senpai scream before.
Even if that had been her voice, I wouldn't have recognized it.
Please... Mari-senpai...
Please be safe...
Please be safe...
But if someone screamed...
then there was a Thing on the second floor.
I swallowed hard.
The second floor was no good either.
A freezing wave of terror ran down my spine.
I spun around again and sprinted back toward the entrance.
Beyond the sliding door, I could still sense those things outside, reaching endlessly toward the lodge.
The rain.
The rattling windows.
The low moans drifting in from outside.
Together they blended into a nightmare.
My heart felt as though it might stop at any moment.
