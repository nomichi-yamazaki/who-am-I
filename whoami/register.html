Guest Register

Just as Glasses-and-Jeans was pulling on his orange rain suit, the sound of footsteps climbing the stairs reached us.
The others and I exchanged glances.
What if it was one of those things?
The thought flashed through all our minds, and everyone instinctively tensed.
But the head that appeared from below the stairwell belonged to Sho Suzuki-san.
Sho Suzuki-san had returned from the reception desk on the first floor carrying the guest registry.
In the light spilling across the corridor, he held the registry carefully against his chest as he walked.
He looked almost like a soldier returning safely from the battlefield.
"I'm back. Let's start checking everyone right away."
Sho Suzuki-san spoke in a low voice as he opened the registry and began turning the pages.
Everyone's eyes naturally drifted toward the guest list.
"First... us... the lodge owner... Tanaka-san... Naoki..."
"...Naoki-kun..."
The moment Sho Suzuki-san spoke Naoki-kun's name, Aya Morishita-san murmured softly and lowered her gaze.
Aya-san had stayed by Naoki-kun's side, talking to him until the very end as she watched him undergo that horrible transformation.
Maybe they had been close.
The thought naturally crossed my mind.
Sho Suzuki-san continued.
"The survivors are over here... Ren Kobayashi-san, Aya Morishita-chan, and me..."
He ran a finger down the page.
"Next are the people who were attacked in the washroom... We've already got Tanaka-san and Naoki down, so who's left?"
Sho Suzuki-san looked at me.
"...What was their name again?... Ki..."
I frowned, trying to remember.
"Right. That man was called Kishida-san. And... what was the other one? Yagi-san?"
"Ah, here it is. Yoko Kishida-san... And the man?"
"Oh! Come to think of it, Kishida-san was in the dining hall even though she'd already been attacked."
Yagi-san was there too, smiling.
A little earlier, he'd been in tears after hearing that Kishida-san had been attacked.
So I remember wondering how he could be smiling now.
Then I noticed Kishida-san standing right beside him.
"Back then I didn't know those things could take human form. I just thought she'd somehow survived and made it back..."
"And then Naoki-san..."
I glanced toward Tsuruoka-san.
"...the fire..."
"Why're you looking at me for?"
Tsuruoka-san snapped after noticing my gaze.
"Uh, no, it's just..."
"So much happened that I completely forgot about it. Then I got locked in the storage room before I could tell anyone..."
"That's why there's a chance Yagi-san was taken too. If anyone sees him, we should probably be careful."
"I see..."
Sho Suzuki-san nodded.
"Then we'll mark him as a question mark for now."
He drew a line through Yoko Kishida-san's name and placed a question mark beside Osamu Yagi-san's.
"And then there was the guy who was camping in a tent..."
Sho Suzuki-san ran his finger down the list.
"That would be... the camper... here he is. Hiroshi Saeki-san..."
As he spoke, he drew a line through the name.
—The man with the lower lip jutting out... the one who had been standing behind Kishida-san in the washroom before being cornered and taken by the tentacles...
"And Akane Ogawa-san..."
—Akane-san...
The image of her returned vividly to my mind.
After becoming one of those things, she had come back in human form, desperately pounding on the dining hall window and begging to be let in.
Akane-san, who had laughed and said, "I love blueberries."
Sho Suzuki-san continued.
"And the people who were taken in the dining hall...?"
"Takamura-san. The one who had been climbing with Akane-san," I said.
"Reiko Takamura-san was taken too..."
Murmuring the words, Sho Suzuki-san drew another line through a name in the registry.
Watching him cross it out, I felt a heavy weight settle in my chest.
For a moment, it felt as though nausea was rising in my throat.
"What about the rest...?" Suzuki-san asked.
"Osawa Takuma..." Glasses-and-Jeans said quietly.
"My condolences... Takuma Osawa-san. And..."
Suzuki-san lowered his eyes to the registry.
"My girlfriend's name is Misaki Saeki," said the man from the female-led couple.
"My condolences... Misaki Saeki-san..."
Suzuki-san crossed out another name.
"The first ones to start changing were those three young guys. I don't know their names, though," I said.
"Ah, these three..."
As he spoke, Suzuki-san drew lines through the names Shota Oishi, Yuma Kikuchi, and Kengo Sato.
The short-haired guy, the playful one, and the chubby guy with glasses—but which name belonged to which of them?
One by one, Suzuki-san crossed out the names of the people who had been taken in the dining hall.
With each name that disappeared beneath a line, the atmosphere grew a little heavier.
One person after another... gone.
So many people...
Someone drew in a sharp breath.
Someone else looked away.
"...I knew it... that person too..."
Comparing the names against what had happened in the dining hall, Suzuki-san continued drawing line after line through the registry.
Grief and fear settled over everyone, pressing down on their shoulders like a physical weight.
"And then there was that man in his sixties... the one who pulled a knife on me and locked me in the storage room..."
As I spoke, I casually glanced to my side.
And then—
I froze.
My heart slammed against my ribs.
It felt as though something shot from my waist, raced up my spine, and exploded through the top of my head.
