Did some part of who they once were still remain somewhere inside those grotesque forms?
Their personalities?
Their memories?

Over on the right side, the elephant-trunk-like appendage lifted upward.
Near its base, a swollen bulge formed, then slowly traveled toward the tip.
The shadow cast across the floor reflected the movement exactly.
Gyuuuu...
Gyurururururu...
The elephant-trunk-like thing emitted a deep twisting sound.

Bsshh... beeee...
Blot...
Becha-becha... becha...
The expelled matter struck the corners of the tables where the lodge guests had once been sitting, shattered apart, and splattered across overturned chairs and the floor beneath them.
The shadow of the lump separating from the tip of the elephant trunk merged together with the things that had been vomited out.
Among the scattered fragments were several tiny perfectly round objects.
And strands resembling human hair as well...
Riding upward on the warm air rising from below came the stench of rotting flesh.

“Uhhh...”
“Help... me...”
“Ahhh...”
“It hurts...”
“Ahhh...”

Even after mutating this far, did some fragment of each person’s consciousness still remain inside?
Bshhh... bshhh...
Pssht... pssht, pssht...
Even as the moaning continued, holes across the creature’s surface kept venting gas while blisters repeatedly formed and burst apart.
Hands and feet of wildly varying sizes protruding from different parts of the mass clawed frantically at empty air and kicked helplessly into nothingness.
Gooooo...
From somewhere far away came the sound of wind that evoked the image of black storm clouds swirling through the darkness.

—Was Mari-senpai safe?
Surely she wasn’t part of that mass... was she?
The fear and anxiety twisted inside me until my chest ached and even my stomach hurt.
Takamura-san and the lodge owner had both become victims of it.
They had been good people...
My chest felt painfully tight.
The shock was so overwhelming I could no longer even tell what I was supposed to think anymore.

Between the middle row of tables and the row closest to the hallway side, the mass that had once been the man in his seventies transformed into a single gigantic arm.
The enormous hand at its tip pressed against the floor, trying to lift one end of the massive body upward.
Hidden in the shadows farther back, the mass that had once been the woman from the female-led couple appeared to have undergone the same transformation, becoming another arm-like structure.
Together, the front and rear arms lifted both ends of the mass upward.
As it rose, the bulk of the creature slowly separated from the shadow cast across the floor.
The side facing me resembled a headless upper torso.
And on the headless section were two large rounded bulges resembling grotesque buttocks.
As the buttock-like flesh lifted and spread apart, the opening between them widened as though something inside were forcing its way outward.
Eventually, an irregular round lump about the size of a volleyball—or perhaps slightly larger—emerged from the stretched opening.
With a collective groan of “Ahhh...” made from overlapping male and female voices, it dropped onto the floor and rolled away.
Under the yellow light hanging from the ceiling, the newly formed round shadow suddenly attached itself to the rolling lump on the floor.
At the same time, thick green sludge was expelled together with it, splattering across the floorboards.
Before long, a powerful rotten stench reached me up in the crawlspace above.
It smelled strangely sour, almost like concentrated grape syrup left to ferment, mixed with the overwhelming odor of blood and seawater.
There was even a faint sweetness buried inside it—sweet enough to make nausea surge into my throat.
The lump rolling across the floor remained connected to the opening by something resembling an umbilical cord.
The opening, which had briefly contracted, widened once again and began pushing out another round shape.
And with it came more thick green fluid.
Faint steam rose from the liquid splattered across the floor.
Looking carefully, I realized the fluid was not purely green—purple streaks ran through it in swirling strands, as though the colors had failed to fully mix together.
Again came that thick nauseating odor, like grapes mixed with the smell of saltwater and iron.
I looked toward the lump.
—!!!
The first round mass that had been pushed out was Akane-san’s head.
The opening continued widening over and over, repeatedly disgorging human heads.
One of them rolled all the way toward the kitchen and disappeared from my field of view.
Goron... goron... goron...
The heads struck the floor and rolled across it.
Bshhh... bshhh... pssht...
Even during all this, holes across the creature’s surface continued venting gas while blisters burst open one after another.
The opening widened again, and another human head connected by an umbilical-cord-like strand dropped out and rolled across the floor.
That one belonged to the woman from the female-led couple...
The one that rolled this way was the athletic-looking man’s head...
The next one expelled was the head of the man in his sixties.
“Ahhh...”
“Uhhh...”
The severed heads rolling out from the opening opened their mouths, groaning and crying weakly as they rolled across the floor.
The umbilical-cord-like strands connected to each head pushed swollen bulges outward from deep inside the opening.

“Ahhh...”
“Ahhh...!”
“Uhhh...”
“Ahhh...!”
Each head opened and closed its mouth repeatedly, letting out male and female groans.

The shadows of the tiny hands cast upon the floor repeatedly opened and clenched themselves beneath the yellow ceiling light.

Between the rearmost table in the middle row and the table directly in front of it, the elephant-trunk-like appendage that had finished disgorging the semi-solid matter began rapidly shrinking shorter and shorter.
The massive lump from which it had emerged also continued contracting and growing steadily smaller as swollen pulses traveled through the umbilical-cord-like strands toward each of the severed heads.
As the original mass diminished, the small bodies attached to each head gradually grew larger instead.
At first, all of the tiny bodies had lain either face-up or face-down on the floor, but as they continued developing, they began moving their limbs independently—rolling over, trying to push themselves upright with trembling hands, writhing in uneven motions.
The umbilical-cord-like strands appeared to connect directly to the base of each body’s tailbone.
Eventually, the mass that had expelled the heads shrank away completely.
By then, each of the heads had become part of a fully formed human body.
The single cord that had once linked all of them together separated into individual strands, and each strand was slowly absorbed back into the tailbone area of the body it belonged to until nothing remained.
The thing that had taken on Akane-san’s appearance looked around restlessly.
The moment it noticed its own naked body, it awkwardly covered itself in embarrassment, then ran toward the kitchen with hurried footsteps—dadadadada—and disappeared from my field of view.
The creatures that had taken the forms of the athletic-looking man and the man in his sixties also seemed to realize they were naked.
They covered themselves, glanced around nervously, spread out the clothes that had been expelled onto the floor, discarded some of them, and eventually began putting on the garments that had originally belonged to them.
—When these aliens take human form... do they feel shame?
Even while fighting back nausea, the thought suddenly crossed my mind.
Most of the things wearing human shapes had already moved toward the kitchen area, out of sight from where I was hiding.
But I could only assume that among them were Takamura-san and the lodge owner as well.
—If these creatures can perfectly take human form like this, then they can run too...
No matter where we flee, won’t they simply catch us and turn us into the same kind of things?
My body was already trembling violently with fear, but at that thought an icy shiver crawled down my spine.

I hadn’t actually heard it with my ears, yet it felt as though a piercing high-pitched tone—something between a peeeeee and a sharp keening whine—had begun ringing directly inside my head.
As if guided by that sound, the various “things that had become human” slowly gathered together from all directions, now dressed in clothing once more.
Their movements were stiff and unnatural.
They bent their knees awkwardly, hunched their backs, and let out strange sounds that seemed halfway between laughter and sobbing, like broken marionettes trying to imitate people.
The athletic-looking man...
The chubby man with glasses...

The man in his seventies...
Even that child...
The ones standing near the reception desk and souvenir counter could still be recognized because I saw them from the side, but the figures directly beneath the hole where I was peering down could only be seen from above, making it impossible to tell who they were anymore.

—Ah... Takamura-san was there too...
Akane-san as well...

“...Looks like a few of ’em... went to the old building too...”

Matter-of-factly.
Like submitting reports.
Who had escaped.
Where they had escaped to.
How many there were.
There was no hesitation.
No uncertainty.
It no longer even felt as though emotions like betraying others to survive still existed within them.
It was as though they had all become parts of one gigantic organism connected by a single nervous system.
I lay frozen in the crawlspace above, silently watching everything unfold.
I was terrified.
And yet I could not look away.

As though they already knew even about me—someone who should not have been here at all.
The number of people who escaped.
The places they escaped to.
The humans barricading themselves away.

if I were found...

Reported.
Processed.

As if I were being shut out from society itself...
