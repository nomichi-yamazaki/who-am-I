After a burst of static, the voice of Suzuki Sho-san, the receptionist who had complained to me earlier, echoed throughout the lodge.
“Everyone, please gather in the dining hall. The owner has something important to discuss.”
The moment the announcement ended, restless murmuring began spreading throughout the building.
In the distance came the sounds of doors opening and footsteps moving along the hallway.

In a corner of the dining hall, once the restless atmosphere had calmed slightly, Takamura-san quietly stood up.
“I’ll go get a notebook. I think we should start keeping records… for whatever happens next.”
At her words, Uchida-san gave a small nod as well.
“Yes, let’s do that. I’ll get my notebook too once you return.”
Still lying on the stretcher, Naoki-kun spoke in a faint, hoarse voice.
“…Please… let me see a mirror.”
—I understood exactly why Naoki-kun wanted to see his own face with that tentacle attached to it, and the thought tightened painfully inside my chest.
Uchida-san gently placed a hand on his shoulder.
“Try not to worry about it, alright? The tentacle is almost completely gone now.”
A deep roaring sound suddenly shook the building, followed by a sharp creaking crack.
Rain lashed violently outside.
The windowpanes rattled noisily in their frames.

Before long, the dining hall doors slid open with a rattling sound, and people began pouring inside, bringing with them the damp smell of rain gear and a low murmur of uneasy voices.
“What happened?”
“Did something occur?”
“Is this about the typhoon?”
Women and men alike whispered nervously among themselves.
Among those entering were the two young women in their twenties who had called out, “Excuse me, are you alright?” while we were digging up that thing.
There were also the two slightly cute, well-dressed girls I had noticed earlier coming down the stairs while talking about movies near the reception area.
And the girls who had been sitting in the Western-style lounge across from us while we were in the common room.
There was also the man in his sixties who had warned us to be careful while we were digging up the thing — the same man I had seen earlier descending the stairs near the reception desk before heading toward the washroom.
And the person dressed almost entirely in beige, wearing a beige fleece jacket over khaki-colored pants.
As well as the boy who had been cooking in the kitchen… and his father. Probably.
Compared to the stiff, frightened expressions of the people already here — compared to those moving awkwardly or trembling outright — every face entering the room looked absurdly bright and carefree to me.
But as they glanced around restlessly and sensed the strange atmosphere hanging over the dining hall, their voices quickly faded into silence.

“…Huh… what happened to that person?” someone whispered softly.
It was a woman’s voice.
Everyone’s attention turned toward Naoki-kun lying on the table.
Their gazes were drawn to him on the stretcher, and the sound of people swallowing nervously spread through the dining hall.
The two young women instinctively looked at one another and shrank closer together, shoulders pressed tightly side by side.
The man in his sixties furrowed his brow deeply and took a cautious step backward, as though trying to assess the situation.
The person dressed entirely in beige covered their mouth with a hand, swallowing whatever words had nearly escaped.
The father from the kitchen stood with his arms crossed, silently staring at the lodge owner’s face.
The low murmuring died away all at once, as though everyone had swallowed the words rising in their throats, leaving only the sound of rainwater dripping from wet gear echoing through the dining hall.
—“Dad, what’s wrong? Dad, what happened…?” the child tugged anxiously at his father’s sleeve as he asked.
“That thing from the drying room came back to life, and now everything’s gone to hell,” the athletic-looking man was saying to the two women who had earlier come downstairs talking about movies near the reception area.

Takamura-san returned carrying her backpack against her chest.
Her expression darkened, and when I followed her line of sight, I saw what she was staring at.
Naoki-kun’s face had become――like melted white cheese.
Takamura-san swallowed hard and spoke in a trembling voice.
“Doctor… will he really be alright like this…?”
“…I don’t know either.”
Even Uchida-san’s voice wavered faintly.
The severity in her expression revealed just how grave the situation had become.
My chest hurt too.
—What is going to happen to this kid…? Is he already beyond saving…?
Back then, those tentacles had reached toward me too.
If Mari-senpai hadn’t pulled my hand and dragged me away, I might have ended up with one attached to me just like this.
The thought terrified me.
“Ah…”
Seeing Naoki-kun’s face transformed into something like melted cheese, Suzuki Sho-san, Ren Kobayashi-san, Morishita-san, and the others let out low groans.
It felt as though everyone was thinking the same thing deep down.
—He might already be beyond help.
That unspoken realization tightened painfully inside my chest.
All eyes remained fixed on the table as silence settled over the room for a long while.
Somewhere, someone quietly began to sob.
Near the floor came the faint rasp of a backpack zipper brushing against itself.
Thunder rumbled low in the distance once.
Rain rose and fell outside in uneven waves.
Every now and then, the building itself gave a slow uneasy shudder.

The silence hanging over the room was finally broken by a quiet voice.
“Maybe… we shouldn’t have gathered everyone here.”
The speaker was Ren Kobayashi-san.
His voice was low, yet steady.
Everyone turned toward him.
His words carved a small crack into the overheated tension filling the dining hall.
The lodge owner made a bitter expression and gave a small nod.
“…Yeah… maybe you’re right. But it’s too late now. We have to decide what to do here and now.”
The lodge owner lifted his face and quietly folded his hands together at the center of the dining hall.
That small movement drew everyone’s attention.
――Rumble rumble…
Suddenly, the windows flashed white, and for a single instant the landscape outside――rocks scattered across the mountainside with tents dotted among them――rose into view bright as daytime.
The next moment, thunder rolled in the distance.
A gust of wind swept against the lodge, rattling the glass windows sharply.
No one in the dining hall spoke.
They simply waited for the thunder to fade away into the storm.
In the strained silence, I heard someone swallow nervously.
Between the strands of Naoki-kun’s hair as he lay atop the stretcher on the dining hall table, a single translucent green blister had begun to swell――slowly bulging outward.
It was still small, but under the light it looked horribly unnatural.
“Doctor… isn’t there anything we can do?” Takamura-san asked while tremblingly writing notes into the notebook she had taken from her backpack.
“Unfortunately… there’s nothing we can do right now. For the time being, let’s keep documenting everything for later.”
Uchida-san spoke quietly to Takamura-san before turning toward the dining hall entrance.
“I’ll go get my notebook as well.”
At that moment, Ren Kobayashi-san spoke again.
“…Let’s surround him and block the view. The people gathered here… probably shouldn’t see too much.”
His voice was low, but it carried clearly throughout the dining hall.
For a moment, we all looked at one another.
Then, without a word, we nodded in agreement.
With a rattling sound, Uchida-san left the dining hall.
Ren Kobayashi-san stepped forward and took position beside the stretcher.
“…Alright. Let’s do it. I’ll explain the situation to everyone,” the lodge owner said.
That became the signal.
Me, Suzuki Sho-san, Takizawa-san, Tsuruoka-san, Mari-senpai, Yabe-san, Yohei――all of us formed a loose semicircle around Naoki-kun and the table.
We naturally stood shoulder to shoulder, close enough to touch, blocking the view of the people gathered inside the dining hall.
I could see Morishita-san crouching near the floor, her arms trembling faintly.

The atmosphere inside the dining hall grew even heavier, clinging to the room like damp cloth.
The lodge owner looked around at the gathered people, took a deep breath, and finally began to speak.
“Everyone, stay calm and listen carefully. We currently have no way to contact the outside world… neither cell phones nor the radio are working right now. So the only thing we can do is secure everyone’s safety here inside the lodge.”
Pausing briefly as though carefully choosing his words, he continued.
“…There’s a dangerous creature outside the lodge. We still don’t know exactly what it is. For now, we’ve locked everything up so it can’t get inside… but I can’t honestly say for certain that it’ll be enough.”
A restless murmur spread through the crowd, but the lodge owner raised his voice slightly and forced it back down.
“That’s exactly why I asked everyone to gather here. I want us to calmly discuss what we should do next.”
Surprisingly, however, the people gathered there remained fairly composed.
No one panicked.
“An unknown creature…? There’s no way something like that exists.”
The one who spoke was the tall man in his sixties dressed entirely in beige — the same man I had seen earlier coming down the stairs near the reception area and heading toward the washroom.
“Isn’t this all getting a little exaggerated?” the father of the elementary school-aged boy added.
—Yeah… I guess this is how people react when they haven’t actually seen what happened with their own eyes…
That was what I thought.
The lodge owner bit his lip and lowered his gaze.
“…I’d like to believe that too. But people here have already become victims. We can’t just do nothing.”
At that moment, Suzuki Sho-san from the reception desk spoke up.
“Shouldn’t we prepare something we could use as weapons… just in case?”
“Wouldn’t it be better for us to stay put and wait for rescue for now?” Ren Kobayashi-san said quietly.
“Even if we wait, that monster’s still outside, ain’t it?” the athletic-looking man argued.
Just then, the dining hall doors rattled open, drawing everyone’s attention.
Uchida-san entered carrying a notebook and pen in her hands.
For a brief moment, she looked puzzled at the sight of us standing tightly around Naoki-kun lying on the table.
Then she glanced behind us at the gathered crowd, seemed to understand immediately, and quietly stepped through the gaps between us.
“…His eyelids…”
Morishita-san, who had been staring at Naoki-kun’s face, let out a breathless whisper.
I leaned forward and looked at him too.
The eyes that had still been slightly open earlier were now completely shut.
No――more than shut.
His eyelids had sealed tightly together, as though glued shut by mucus.
It looked almost as if his eyes themselves had disappeared, leaving behind only the swollen shapes beneath the skin.
“Naoki-kun…? Can you hear me?” Uchida-san called softly.
There was no reply.
Only his breathing barely continued.
His chest still rose and fell faintly, but his face had become something like melted cheese molded into the shape of a face without eyes.
“…His eyes… they won’t open anymore,” Takamura-san whispered in a trembling voice.
A quiet ripple of unease spread through the dining hall once again.
Everyone lowered their voices and held their breath, realizing the situation had become even more serious.
“Isn’t there some way to contact the outside world?” the man in glasses and jeans said anxiously.
“Does anyone here have a cell signal?” the lodge owner called out loudly.
“I’ll check the radio one more time,” Suzuki Sho-san said before hurrying away from the group surrounding Naoki-kun and running toward the dining hall entrance.
I already assumed it would be useless, but I still pulled my smartphone from my pocket and switched it on anyway.
No good.
The screen still showed “No Service.”
Not wanting to waste battery power, I turned it off again.
When I looked back toward Naoki-kun lying on the table, I saw that the translucent green blister on his head had swollen even larger than before.
Beside it, another new blister had appeared――still small, but glowing with the same unnatural sheen.
Through the thin surface, the contents looked like trembling green jelly, sending a cold shiver crawling down my spine.
Naoki-kun moved his head faintly and let out a weak groan, his lips trembling slightly.
The tension in the faces surrounding him tightened yet again.
Ren Kobayashi-san frowned and gently placed a hand on Naoki-kun’s shoulder.
But with the blisters continuing to swell and new ones appearing, the air inside the room remained painfully heavy――so tense it almost felt difficult to breathe.

With a rattling sound, the dining hall doors swung open again as Suzuki Sho-san from the reception desk came rushing back, breathing hard.
“The radio’s still dead after all…”
“In that case, we won’t be able to call for help until the storm settles down and the antenna’s repaired…” the lodge owner said grimly.
“What if somebody goes out to get help?”
Our fearless leader, Takizawa-san――well, maybe not entirely fearless――finally spoke up.
“Even if someone tried, there’s still a storm raging outside. And even if they somehow managed to avoid running into that creature, reaching another mountain lodge in weather like this would be extremely difficult,” the lodge owner replied, turning toward us.
“Couldn’t we somehow trap that monster somewhere?” Suzuki Sho-san suggested.
“It was in the washroom earlier… but where is it now…?” Ren Kobayashi-san muttered uneasily.
—That’s true.
At the very least, checking on that much was something even I could do.
I had been wanting to make myself useful somehow.
“I’ll go take a look,” I said.
