Anomaly

My head tilts—then jerks back upright.
…Damn. I’m about to fall asleep. Gotta stay sharp…
It dips again.
My awareness starts to drift… slipping away…
…I’m gonna pass out…

—Shhhh…
Somewhere far off, I think I hear rain beginning to strike the roof again.
…Ah… it’s raining again…

I shift slightly, nudging the chair back just a little—
—and suddenly it lurches.
My heart jumps into my throat, breath catching, as I snap my weight back to steady myself.
The leg of the folding chair has slipped into a gap in the wooden slats.
…and in that same instant—
at the edge of my vision—something feels… off.
My heart kicks again.
I turn, quickly—
The aluminum sheet… is open.
The thing that was wrapped inside—
—is gone.
“…What…?”
For a second, my mind goes completely blank. I forget how to breathe.
While I dozed… it disappeared.
My heart starts hammering as I push myself to my feet, scanning the drying room.
Did it fall?
…No. That’s impossible—
I crouch, checking beneath the table.
I lift the edge of the aluminum sheet, peer inside—empty.
I flip it over, even though I know it’s pointless.
Of course it’s not there.
Under the chair.
Between the slats.
In the corners of the shelves.
Along the slight ridges in the floor—
My eyes race. My hands follow.
The movements are almost automatic now.
Sweat drips down from my forehead.
“…Why… where did it go…?”
Muttering under my breath, I inch forward—almost crawling—
methodically eliminating every possibility.
Panic…
and the creeping, obsessive fear of missing something—
they take over my body,
guiding every movement of my hands and feet.

It’s not there.
Nowhere. Not anywhere in this drying room.
…This is bad.
This is completely my fault.
…What do I do?
Tsuruoka-san… he was so excited about making a name for himself with this…
His voice comes back to me—
“Alright, that settles it! We’re all set! The moment the world changes—it’s going to happen right before our eyes! …And hey, I was the first one to spot that glowing thing, so this whole discovery started with me, got it…?”
If he finds out it’s gone… how furious is he going to be?
Another memory surges up—
the time the production line had to be shut down because the solenoid valve didn’t arrive in time.
His shouting—sharp, relentless—still rings in my ears.
“Hey, Sho!! Why the hell hasn’t the solenoid valve shown up yet!? The line can’t run without it!! …If you’d handled the order properly, this wouldn’t have happened! How are you gonna take responsibility for this!? …Do you have any idea what kind of complaints we’ll get if the deadline slips!? We’re the ones who’ll take the hit for it! …Don’t screw with me! Are you even taking your job seriously!?”
…I have to tell them.
But—Tsuruoka-san…
The memory of that rage—of being chewed out over a missed delivery—tightens around my chest.
If I tell him it disappeared while I was dozing… I don’t even want to imagine what he’ll say.
It’s like an alarm is blaring inside my head, panic rising with every second.
“…What do I do…? What do I even say…?”
My mind starts running simulations on its own.
No, it was gone when I checked—
No, that sounds like an excuse…
But those two people in their thirties—they were calling it a scoop… maybe they took it—
Even though I don’t actually know that… I catch myself trying to shift the blame.
My thoughts are a tangled mess.
Where the hell did it go…?
The empty aluminum sheet in front of me gapes open—like it’s asking me the question itself.
“…No way… it couldn’t have just… vanished… no, it has to be somewhere…”
Frozen in place, gripping the back of the chair, I feel like my chest is about to burst from the pressure.
…What do I do?
Should I just pretend I was asleep until someone comes in—act confused, like wait, wasn’t it right here?
…No. That won’t work.
If I try something like that, it’ll just make Tsuruoka-san even angrier.
…There’s no way around it.
I’ll have to be honest. Apologize.
Damn it… I only nodded off for a second…
Why does my luck have to be this bad…

Trying to steady the pounding in my chest, I decide to go tell them—my steps already quick, almost stumbling with urgency.
…No, but how do I even say it…?
I guess I was tired and kind of dozed off… No—that just sounds like an excuse…
The thoughts spin in circles, chasing each other inside my head.
I can’t pull them together. My heart is still hammering.
I move along the outdoor passage between the washroom and the lodge, my pace unconsciously shifting from a fast walk into a light run.
Each step on the rain-soaked wooden slats rattles—clatter, clatter— the slight slip beneath my soles sending a cold tension up my spine.
The moment I have to tell Tsuruoka-san is getting closer.
Step by step.
I wish… I could just never get there.
At last, I reach the entrance to the lodge.
I climb the slope, grab the sliding door, and push it open—
cool air spills out, clinging to my shoulders.
I step inside, move forward, place my foot on the first step of the staircase to the right—
just as I’m about to go up—
laughter drifts in from the dining hall.
“Give me a break!” —that’s Yohei.
…So they’re all in the dining hall.
Just from the sound of their voices, I can picture it— they’re having a good time.
I steady my breathing and head toward them.
…It’s almost time.
What’s Tsuruoka-san going to say…?
…God, I wish I never had to arrive.

I slide the door open, and the moment I step inside, I’m wrapped in laughter and conversation—the warmth and energy of the room hitting me all at once.
Takizawa-san, Tsuruoka-san, Yohei, Mari-senpai, and Yabe-san are gathered around a table.
…What are they doing?
In front of Takizawa-san, that gold camping stove we used at Hakuba-jiri is taken apart—
(so that cylindrical part comes off…)—
and beside it sits a tin that looks like a can of Sakuma Drops.
…Refueling it, maybe?
“Oh—Saku-chan…” Mari-senpai notices me first.
“Oh? What’s up, Sho?” Tsuruoka-san looks over. “That thunder spooked you or something? …You better keep your eyes on it.”
“Ah… um… that thing… it’s… gone!”
The words spill out of me, tangled with panic—
and for a split second, the entire room freezes.
Tsuruoka-san’s brow tightens, his voice sharp.
“…What do you mean, gone?”
My throat locks up, but I force the words out.
“I… I dozed off for a moment… and when I realized… it was gone…”
For a beat—
then Yohei lets out a half-laugh, raising his voice like he’s joking.
“Don’t tell me that thing came back to life and walked off or something—”
“That’s not—” Mari-senpai cuts in, trying to stop him—
—but before she can finish—
“Don’t screw around, Sho!!”
Tsuruoka-san’s voice explodes across the room.
“Why the hell were you sleeping!? Why weren’t you watching it properly!?”
The force of it hits me head-on—
like my face is being crushed inward.
My eyes sting. I almost break—
but I grit my teeth, forcing it down. Not here. Not for this.
“Hey, hey… Tsuru-san…” Takizawa-san starts—
“You don’t have to say it like that, do you?” Mari-senpai snaps back before he can finish.
She’s always on my side.
“If it was that important, maybe you should’ve been the one holding onto it the whole time,” Yabe-san adds, backing me up.
I shrink in on myself, shoulders tightening.
My mouth opens—
…but no words come out.
“Anyway… let’s all go look for it together…” Mari-senpai says.
But Tsuruoka-san isn’t calming down—his voice only gets louder, eyes blazing as he glares at me.
“It’s those idiots again!! That pair taking pictures earlier! It’s gotta be them—they must’ve taken it!!”
His shout echoes through the dining hall.
I flinch, shoulders tightening—my heart pounding, palms slick with sweat.
Yohei shrinks back a little, muttering under his breath.
“…No way… that doesn’t make sense…”
“Getting worked up won’t solve anything,” Mari-senpai says, placing a hand on Tsuruoka-san’s shoulder, trying to steady him.
I take a breath—
“…I’m sorry… I’ll go look right now,” I manage, my voice unsteady.
—“Kyaa!!”
A sharp scream cuts through everything—
from outside the dining hall, somewhere near the washroom.
The air freezes instantly.
Everyone looks at each other, eyes wide.
…That thing…?
It’s the first thought that hits me.
“What was that!? What happened!?” someone from the lodge calls out from deeper in the hallway.
Through the row of windows along the dining hall corridor, I see a door on the opposite wall swing open—
The young staff guy in the black tracksuit—probably a part-timer—bursts out,
along with the woman who’d been at the shop earlier, the one who filled in at reception—the teacher in the navy T-shirt with her hair in a ponytail.
They both sprint toward where the scream came from.
“What the hell was that scream…?” Tsuruoka-san mutters, his anger blown away in an instant by surprise.
“…Was that… it?” Yohei whispers, narrowing his eyes.
…By it, he means the thing that vanished while I was asleep.
I’m sure of it.
“Let’s go!”
I’m already moving—rushing toward the dining hall door left wide open.
I can feel Takizawa-san and Mari-senpai breaking into a run right behind me.
Out into the hallway—past the shop—reaching the intersection, turning left toward the washroom.
We weave between the rows of pillars, running.
The staircase passes on our left as we push forward.
—…Packs…?
At the edge of my vision, I see them—
two large packs leaning against the wall at the far end of the corridor, down to the right.
One light green.
The other, wine red.

A young guy in a black tracksuit—kind of like Yohei—rushes ahead and yanks the lodge door open with a clatter, exposing what lies beyond.
A lukewarm, unsettling air spills inside.
In the darkness, the yellow light of the passage between the lodge and the washroom stretches out—and beyond it, the stark white glow spilling from a tall, rectangular doorway to the washroom.
The sound of rain hammering against the plastic roofing echoes—shaaah—
and somewhere, the gutter trickles, draining water in a thin, steady stream.
A faint waft of air freshener drifts from the washroom.
Through the open doorway—
the sight that leaps into my eyes makes me catch my breath.
Beyond the wide-open door—
on the wooden slats— that black thing lies face down.
Behind it, several men and women stand frozen together.
In that instant, relief and dread surge up at the same time.
—There it is. We found it.
But at the same time, a chill crawls up my spine.
…Is it alive?
What am I supposed to do?
…I let it get away while I was dozing… does that mean I’m the one who has to catch it?
…How am I supposed to catch something like that?
Somewhere in my head, I know—this isn’t something that should be moving.
It doesn’t make sense. It can’t.
But Tsuruoka-san’s shouting flashes through my mind— and my thoughts can’t reach any further than that.

Skitter… skitter…
Drag… drag…
The thing crawls low to the ground—like a pitch-black, four-legged spider.
Seen that way, what I thought was a fetus-like head no longer looks like a head at all— more like the swollen abdomen of a spider.
Its spine and ribs jut upward, exposed, as it shifts— its limbs moving in stiff, awkward motions.
It turns in place as if unsure of its direction…
then suddenly goes still.
—Like it’s searching. Or lost.
The dim fluorescent light, sinking into the darkness, catches along the ridges of its spine— throwing them into sharp relief, making its spider-like grotesqueness even more pronounced.
Skitter… skitter… skrrt…
Each movement of its limbs scrapes faintly against the wooden slats beneath it.

Inside the washroom, several figures stand frozen— the air itself trembling with tension.
At the far right, behind the thing, is the man in his forties—the one who’d been camping.
Balding from the forehead back, glasses slipping down his nose, his eyes are wide, unblinking—mouth half open, lower lip jutting out as he stares.
He’s the same man who watched us silently while we dug it out… the one who’d been listening in on those college guys doing their “I can do it” bit when we arrived at the lodge.
Closer to the front stands the woman from that suspicious middle-aged pair staying in the lodge— a sharp-looking woman, her eyes wide, face contorted as she staggers backward.
In front of her— one of the two women in their twenties from Hakuba-jiri.
The smaller one—the cute one.
She’s leaning against the sink, upper body arched back, face drained of color, frozen in place.
Near the entrance to the washroom— just ahead and to my left, by the lodge-side exit— the young lodge worker in the black Adidas tracksuit stands, breathing hard, shoulders squared as he steps forward.
He’s got that same easygoing vibe as Yohei… but his profile—long bangs falling slightly over his face— is rigid, his mouth twitching, frozen in shock beneath the attempt to look brave.
Behind him, off to my right, stands the woman in the navy T-shirt—Tanaka-san—her hair pulled back in a ponytail.
The teacher who’d taken over at reception earlier.
She covers her mouth, rooted to the spot.
“What is that!? Move it—someone, move it out of the way!”
The sharp-voiced middle-aged woman at the far side of the washroom shouts.
I don’t stop.
I step down the wooden slope leading from the lodge onto the slatted passage between the buildings.
Above me, the rain lashes against the plastic roofing—shaaah—
The gutter trickles steadily somewhere nearby.
The faint scent of air freshener lingers in the air.
Forcing down the cold, paralyzing fear creeping up my spine— my throat twitching— I move, almost without thinking, stepping forward from behind the man in the Adidas tracksuit and Tanaka-san.
I feel responsible.
That alone drives me— pushing me ahead of everyone else.
(It’s because of that thing I got yelled at…!)
The thought echoes in my chest as I take one step—then another—toward the grotesque shape before me.
The sound of my slippers tapping against the wooden slats rattles in the rain.
My heartbeat roars in my ears— like the blood in my body is surging backward through my veins.

The young guy in the black Adidas tracksuit moves up to the washroom entrance, the woman who’d been the teacher right behind him.
I reach the doorway just after them— and peer inside between the two of them.
The washroom is lit by the dim glow of fluorescent lights.
The thunder has faded into the distance, leaving only the faint zzzz of the lights ringing in my ears.
A single moth drifts unsteadily around the dull glow— each time its wings tap softly against the tube with a tiny tck, the air in the room seems to shiver.
Two long fluorescent fixtures, set in a line along the wooden ceiling, cast stark light from front and back— throwing the black shape on the slatted floor into sharp relief, their faint flicker making its shadow writhe unnaturally.
Along the left wall, sliding doors to the men’s and women’s toilets stand side by side, and between them, a large, faded poster of the Hakuba Three Peaks.
In the frosted glass window ahead, divided into a grid, the backs of the people standing there— and the black shape crawling across the slats— are dimly reflected.
To the right, four tall mirrors line the wall.
Each one captures a fragment of the black thing on the floor.
On the stainless-steel sink, a piece of paper is taped up— “Please conserve water,” scrawled in black marker.
But now, no one is using it.
Everyone stands frozen— as if holding their breath.
At the edge of my vision, off to the right—half-hidden in the shadow of the doorway— I catch sight of her.
One of the two women Tsuruoka-san had spoken to at Hakuba-jiri—the taller one.
The slender, elegant woman… the one who reminded me of Wakako Sakai, my dad’s favorite.
Her eyes are wide, fixed on the thing in pure terror.
A strange, stunned atmosphere fills the room— like time itself has stopped.
It feels as though no one has spoken for hours.
“Reiko… what do we do…? This is seriously bad…!”
The smaller woman from Hakuba-jiri—leaning against the sink—edges closer toward the taller one, her voice trembling.
“H-Hey… are you serious…? Where the hell did that come from?” the young guy in the black tracksuit says from just ahead of me.
“It’s… alive.” Yabe-san’s voice comes from behind.
“…Why is it here…?” Takizawa-san mutters from somewhere to my left, stunned.
“W-What the hell is this…!? This ain’t some prank, right…? H-Hey, Sho! You didn’t mess with something, did you!?” Tsuruoka-san snaps.
—Just because I work with electrical systems doesn’t mean I could rig something like this to move… not in this amount of time…
“…This isn’t a dream, right…?” Hearing Mari-senpai’s voice brings a small, fragile sense of relief.
“Whoa—this is insane! It’s like something out of a movie! Somebody get this on their phone—this’ll blow up for sure!” Yohei shouts excitedly from behind.
“Hurry! Hurry up and move it out of the way!” the sharp-voiced middle-aged woman at the far end of the washroom screams.
“Best not to make any noise.” Yabe-san says quietly.
“Don’t move! It might be better not to provoke it… someone… please go get Okubo-san.”
Tanaka-san—the woman in the navy T-shirt to my right—turns back as she speaks.
Her intelligent, gentle face is drawn tight with fear.
“Oh, I’m right here. Be careful.”
The lodge owner’s voice comes from behind—the same man I’d been chatting with about Bon Curry earlier.
“Saku-chan… are you okay being that close? Be careful…”
Mari-senpai’s voice reaches me from behind.
Something in my chest warms—tightens— like I need to show I’ve got some courage.
“Sho… catch it.”
Tsuruoka-san’s voice cuts in from behind.
—I mean… I want to… but I can’t exactly grab that thing with my bare hands…
“Owner—do you have anything we can use to catch it?”
Maybe he sees me hesitate—Takizawa-san asks for me.
“Something to catch it, huh…? Hey—Sho, go get something.”
The owner calls out.
For a split second, I think he’s talking to me— we’d just bonded over curry—
but then I remember— the guy at reception who complained at me… his name’s Sho too.
“Sho, don’t let it bite you or anything, man.”
Yohei calls from behind.
“…Yeah.”
The other Sho—the one from reception—turns and answers him instead.
Yohei looks thrown for a second, then gives a small nod.
I turn back—
and see it.
The thing’s back… trembles.
Just slightly.
At first, I think I imagined it.
But the tremor is real— something is writhing beneath that dark membrane.
Each time the light brushes across it, a wet sheen slides over the black surface—
and from it, faintly, like steam— a raw, living stench begins to rise.
Salt.
Iron.
The smell of fresh flesh.
“Hah, hah… it’ll be fine. It’s not gonna bite you or anything. …Look at it. It’s shaking—scared stiff.”
Tsuruoka-san’s voice comes from behind, careless—almost amused.
“Sho… take responsibility and catch it. Bare hands is fine—bare hands.”

The thing lies with that rounded bulge—what I’d taken for a fetus-like head—facing toward us, while the rear forms a back where spine and ribs seem to rise beneath the surface.
That back is shaped like a rounded pentagon— and at its far tip, the edge forms something like an upside-down, softened “W.”
That “W”-shaped tip… moves.
Just slightly—like a hinge.
It’s like a fish opening its gills—
or the instant a shell begins to crack open.
Pak—
A faint creak— unnaturally loud in the slow-motion hush.
At that moment, droplets on the washroom tiles quiver— one… then another, trembling as if startled.
The pentagonal back begins to open—
slowly, from front to back— using that “W” tip like a hinge, splitting apart like a shell.
What had looked like spine and ribs now spreads like veins—
like peeling back a single leaf of a jet-black cabbage.
Beneath the opened surface—
where the outer skin parts— is a slick, flesh-toned layer, in stark contrast to the black exterior.
A dull mix of red and white—like raw chicken.
Veins writhe beneath the surface, fine strands branching like leaf veins.
The light catches them— bringing out a rough, uneven texture.
“Oh—hey, it opened. …What, is it trying to say hello or something?”
Tsuruoka-san’s voice comes from behind.
“…Hello…”
Yohei’s voice follows—
but there’s a tremor in it.
The next instant—
the hinged tip at the opened back twists loose with a wet, unnatural motion.
The base of that cabbage-leaf-like back—
begins to extend inward, unfolding in an accordion-like stretch.
The extending surface is like slug skin—
slick, glistening with mucus.
Tiny segmented ridges run along it,
and along the top, yellow and black stripes line up in vertical bands.
The underside is raw, fleshy pink—
a faint steam rising from it.
—What is happening…?
I can’t look away.
“What is this!? I seriously can’t—this is too much!!”
The smaller girl, eyes brimming with tears, looks back and forth between the thing and the tall, slender woman beside her.

From the vertical slit in the thing’s back— where the skin has peeled away— something else begins to writhe.
The split opens fully, revealing vivid fascia and veins pulsing beneath the stark white light.
From the center of that opening— slowly, deliberately— it pushes something out.
A single, black, glistening tendril.
Smooth and dark like the back of an eel, its surface slick with a thin coat of mucus.
The eel-like appendage extends— slowly, yet with certainty— probing the air, as if searching.

The accordion-like section— with that black, cabbage-leaf-like back still attached at its tip— bends sharply to the right, curling back on itself in a U-turn.
Like a cobra raising its head— the leaf-shaped tip spreads into a hooded form— and that poised “head” turns… toward the small, cute girl leaning against the sink on the right.
“Akane! Get over here—now!”
The tall, slender woman—resembling Wakako Sakai—reacts instantly, grabbing the smaller girl’s arm, trying to pull her away from between the sink and the thing.
“Reiko! Help me—please!! I can’t—this is too much!!”
Akane cries out, on the verge of tears.

Off to the left— even as that unfolds— the thing begins to rise.
Limbs that had been splayed on all fours press hard against the floor— kicking, straining— lifting the body upward as if it were standing on its head.
What had looked like a pitch-black, four-legged spider— as it rises— draws together into something more human in shape.
The part that once resembled a spider’s abdomen now shifts— becoming something closer to a fetus-like head.
“…It stood up…”
Yohei’s voice comes from behind, stunned.
And as it straightens— the center of its torso, between its limbs, begins to split open.
A narrow, oval depression—tapered at both ends— runs down its middle.
That vertical seam tears apart— left and right—
and from within—
a mass, like exposed organs, slides wetly into view.
From inside, thin, intestine-like tendrils begin to push outward— wriggling, accompanied by a damp, sucking sound.
Each of those gut-like appendages ends in a bud-like tip— and along both sides, countless fine tendrils twitch and writhe— like the legs of a centipede.
The smell—
raw flesh—
thickens overwhelmingly, stabbing into my nose.

Like a cobra raising its hood, the thing lifts that jet-black, cabbage-leaf-like part— swaying it back and forth, as if building momentum.
At the sink on the right, the small, cute girl— pulled by the tall, slender woman who looks like Wakako Sakai— starts to step back toward our side, hesitantly—
—and then, for a split second, she glances back.
In that instant—
the leaf-like black mass at the tip of the snake-like tendril— its slug-slick skin banded in yellow and black— whips around—
—and lunges straight at her face.
It slams into her— engulfing her completely.
My heart jolts—hard.
“KYAA—!”
The scream comes from somewhere close—right beside me— and yet it sounds distant, as if echoing from far away.
—What…!? What’s happening…? Is this real…?
My mind goes blank.
I can’t think.
Time— seems to stop.
Something inside me slows unnaturally— as if my own sense of time has thickened—
like the world has turned into a viscous liquid, every movement heavy, stretched, dragging on.

—Run!!
An emergency alarm blares inside my head.
—What’s going to happen…?
Curiosity—
I can’t look away.
—I have to catch it… or Tsuruoka-san will tear into me…
All of it—fear, curiosity, pressure— swirls together inside me—
locking my body in place.

From within that black, cabbage-leaf-like mass clamped over the small girl’s face— countless slick, jet-black tendrils begin to emerge.
One by one, they latch onto her cheeks— crawl over her hair— spreading, spreading—until they engulf her entire head.
“Mm—mm—!”
She wrenches her hand free from the tall woman’s grip, grabbing at the edge of that black, leaf-like thing stuck to her face— trying desperately to tear it off as a muffled groan forces its way out.
Even as she struggles— the tendrils grow denser, layer upon layer— smearing over her head, coating it, sealing it in.
The tall, slender woman to her right reaches out— as if trying to pull her free—
but her body betrays her— her hips drawn back, her legs trembling uncontrollably.

—That thing… I found it…
—That thing that got away while I was dozing…
This is happening because of me.
It’s my responsibility… I have to do something.
If I don’t—Tsuruoka-san will tear into me…
Those thoughts— smother the voice telling me to run.
—Catch it…! —Catch it…!
My mind commands me—
and I move.
I thrust both hands forward— toward that upright, pitch-black, fetus-like thing—
and take a step.
In that instant—

“Uwaa—!!”
The young man in the black tracksuit, just ahead to my left— long bangs falling over his face— can’t take it anymore.
At the sight of the girl with that black mass clamped over her face, he lunges forward—
his scream tearing up from the back of his throat—
and grabs at the writhing tendrils.
The tendrils caught in his hands whip side to side, trying to slip free—
—but in the next instant,
the bud-like tip splits open—
and from within, a thin, flesh-colored tendril shoots out—
—latching onto his cheek.
For a split second— I feel it.
Cold.
Sticky.
As if it’s happening to me— that same sensation spreads across my own face.
“AAAGH—!! …It hurts!! It hurts!! …I can’t get it off— I can’t—!! Get it off! Somebody—get it off!!”
His voice fractures— pure, reflexive terror echoing through the room.
He thrashes, hair whipping wildly— yanking at the tendril—
—but the more he pulls,
the tighter it clings—
stretching—
his cheek—
as if the skin itself might tear away.
His scream dulls— breaking—
into something heavier.
A wet mixture of saliva and mucus trails from the corner of his mouth.
His voice— collapses into sobbing.
—What is happening…?
…What the hell is happening!?
I can’t understand it.

“Akane!! …Akane!!”
The tall, slender woman’s scream echoes from the right.
The next instant—
just ahead of me to the right— the woman in the navy T-shirt,her hair tied back in a ponytail— Tanaka-san—the teacher who had taken over at reception—
suddenly turns left—
—and runs straight toward me.
Her face is desperate— panicked—
and with a sweeping motion of her arm, she shoves me aside—
as if knocking me out of her way—
and bolts out of the washroom.
I stagger— nearly losing my footing—
almost falling—
but somehow manage to steady myself.
—She ran…!!
For a split second, I’m convinced—
that Tanaka-san, overcome with fear, just shoved me aside…
and fled.

Behind the thing, off to the right— I see the eel-like tendril that emerged from its back stretching out—
reaching past the girl whose face is sealed under that black, cabbage-like mass— toward the sharp-tongued middle-aged woman… and the man behind her with the protruding lower lip.
The woman recoils— pressing herself hard against the sink on the right, backing away from the approaching tendril—
until she bumps into the man behind her, driving him back as well—
pinning him toward the wall beside the window at the front.
“Stop! …Stop it, please!!” she screams—
but there’s no way that thing understands words.
“Hey! Don’t just stand there—do something!!”
the man with the jutting lip shouts—
crushed forward by her weight as he looks toward us.
