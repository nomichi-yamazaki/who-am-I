Reunion

As I struggled to steady my ragged breathing, my eyes remained fixed on the sleeping quarters stretching away on either side of the corridor.
It was quiet.
Far too quiet.
As though this Old Wing had been severed from the flow of time itself—a silence that felt deeply wrong.
Then, without warning—
"…Sho."
Above me.
The voice dropped from directly overhead, so close it felt as though it had spoken right beside my ear.
My heart nearly exploded in my chest.
I instinctively flinched and, in the next instant, snapped my head upward toward the ceiling.
"Sho, up here... It's me, Yohei!"
In the sleeping quarters to my left, on the uppermost sleeping platform to the right side of the room.
A man leaned forward from the shadows, showing only his face.
It was unmistakably my best friend, Yohei.
My coworker who had joined the company the same year I did.
My closest friend—the kind of friend who would come over to my house and invite me to his.
I played guitar, and Yohei played bass guitar. We were always talking about starting a band together someday.
There had even been nights when we stayed up until dawn talking about our dreams for the future.
Earlier, when everyone had suspected I might be one of the Things, Yohei hadn't spoken up for me, and that had weighed heavily on my heart.
But given the circumstances... perhaps it couldn't be helped.
If I had thought Yohei might not really be Yohei anymore, I might have acted the same way.
His name hadn't been on the list of people taken by the Thing—the list we had made using the guest registry.
And the fact that he was hiding up there only made it seem more likely that he was truly himself.
If he had been one of them, there would have been no reason to hide.
My chest tightened, and my breath caught in my throat.
He was alive.
"Yohei...! Thank God... Thank God you're alive!"
My voice trembled on its own.
Relief washed over Yohei as well, and his shoulders sagged as he placed a foot on the ladder and climbed down.
When he stepped onto the tatami floor, the overhead light caught his face just enough for me to see it clearly.
He was smiling.
A smile that looked on the verge of tears.
"I was scared out of my mind being here alone.
But man... I never imagined this would happen because of that thing we brought back..."
He shook his head weakly.
"Back in the toilet, we were joking about making it part of the band and everything..."
The memory struck me hard.
At the time, it had felt like nothing more than an ordinary conversation.
Now, compared to the nightmare we were living through, it cut straight through my chest.
The fact that he's saying that... it really is Yohei.
Relief washed over me.
I took a step closer to him.
"So how did you get away?"
Yohei took a breath before answering.
"Takizawa-san was the one who said, 'Let's run to the Old Wing.'
So we all came running over here together."
"Mari-senpai too? Is everyone okay?"
"Yeah. Takizawa-san should still be somewhere farther back in here..."
"And Yabe-san and Mari-senpai came with us too.
But things got bad, and they ended up having no choice but to run that way."
He gestured deeper into the building.
"They headed up to the second floor."
Something in the air changed.
Just slightly.
A pause that felt as though he were holding something back.
I pressed on.
"But... why wasn't Tsuruoka-san with you?"
Yohei's expression stiffened instantly.
"W-Wait... you saw Tsuruoka-san?"
"Yeah."
"I did."
Silence fell again.
Then, slowly, he began to speak.
"...Tsuruoka-san said,
'It'll probably be safer staying with the lodge staff than going with Takizawa-san and the others.'
So he didn't come with us...
He ran into the staff room instead..."
"I see..."
"Yeah... but..."
"I went with Takizawa-san and the others too..."
"And that's when something happened that scared the hell out of me."
Yohei took a step closer.
There was a strange dampness in his voice now.
"You remember the people who got taken in that washroom, right?
That strong-looking woman... she was there..."
"I panicked when I saw her and shoved her away..."
"And then... I was the only one who got caught..."
He stopped midsentence.
His face slowly twisted as he leaned closer, peering into mine.
He wasn't smiling.
He wasn't crying.
He wasn't angry.
He wasn't frightened.
It was an expression I should have recognized.
And yet somehow, I didn't.
His eyes weren't smiling.
No—only one of them was.
"But... did you already become one of those things?..."
His voice dropped to a whisper.
"That's amazing...
I didn't know we could do something like this..."
The voice brushing against my ear carried no warmth of breath.
Only the wet, fleshy sound of something rubbing against itself.
Yohei's pupils trembled.
Unable to focus.
Both of them slowly drifted inward.
As though something deep behind the eyes were pushing them into a new shape.
His skin tightened.
The reflected light across his face seemed to bend unnaturally toward the center.
And then—
both of Yohei's eyes...
melted together into a single circle.
The transformation lasted only an instant.
But in my mind, it was etched in endless slow motion.
A shiver ran down my spine, freezing it solid.
—That's not Yohei... He's not my best friend anymore... Even Yohei... Even Yohei has been taken...
If he realizes I'm human, it's over.
The thought struck me as tears began to well in my eyes without my even noticing.
Yohei—or rather, that thing wearing Yohei's face—grabbed my shoulder.
Its fingers were strangely cold.
Unlike the thing outside the door earlier, the arm looked thin and soft—
yet the grip was painfully strong.
My heartbeat thundered in my ears.
But I couldn't let my expression change.
As terror coursed through me like my blood was flowing backward, my mind arrived at a single conclusion.
—It doesn't know whether I'm human or not.
Then...
Then I can still fool it.
My throat trembled on its own.
My vocal cords felt frozen solid.
Even so, I forced the words out.
"Yeah... I became one of them too."
I killed all emotion in my voice and spoke like a machine.
The instant the words left my mouth, time froze.
Yohei smiled.
Yohei's—no, its—movement stopped.
It didn't release my shoulder.
But its expression slowly softened, as if probing for the truth, taking its time.
"I see... Then we're comrades now..."
Slowly, its hand slipped away from my shoulder.

Yohei had been hiding on the top tier of the sleeping platforms—deep in the darkness, so high up it seemed to connect to the attic itself.
Every time I looked up, I couldn't stop wondering about that space.
Waiting for the right moment, I spoke as casually as I could.
"Hey, Yohei... there's something I've been curious about."
Yohei turned only his face toward me.
"What is it?"
"You were hiding up there earlier, right? That space above, where the roof slopes down... I've been wondering what's up there. Does it keep going farther back? Maybe there's an escape route or somewhere we could keep watch from?"
Yohei's eyes flicked toward the darkness above.
For the briefest moment, his attention shifted away from me.
"Now that you mention it... I only climbed up there because I was scared. I never checked how far it goes."
—Got you.
I'd just earned a perfectly reasonable excuse to go investigate.
"Right? I mean, if there's a way through to the other side, it'd be a lot safer than barricading ourselves in here. I'm gonna take a quick look. You stay here, okay?"
"Huh...? Sho, you're going by yourself?"
For a moment, he seemed hesitant—
but not enough to stop me.
I forced a smile.
"Yeah. I'll be right back. Don't worry—we're comrades now, remember?"
Even though the one standing there was no longer truly Yohei, a part of me still felt guilty, as if I were deceiving an old friend.
Yohei seemed reassured and gave a small nod.
The moment he did, I placed my foot on the ladder.
Each creak of the wood made my sweaty palms slip against the rungs.
But I didn't look back.
One step.
Then another.
Upward—higher, farther, and toward my "escape route."
Yohei's voice caught up with me from behind.
"Sho, be careful! It's dark up there...!"
I didn't answer.
If I did, he might hear the lie in my voice.
So I simply held my breath and kept climbing.
