Underground Storage

Darkness struck before the sounds did.
The hatch I had just slammed shut glowed faintly in the blackness above me, its narrow edges outlined by a thin white rectangle.
Those slivers of light were the only remaining outline of the world.
Take even a single step away from them, and the darkness became absolute.
I couldn't see what was there.
I couldn't even tell whether anyone else was.
Something crashed and thrashed overhead.
Within the pale outline of the hatch, dark shadows writhed and lunged across the light.
"OHHHHH!!... O-OOOOHHHHH—AAAHHH!!"
Takizawa-san's scream echoed through the floor above me.
Takizawa-san...
They're getting him...
Takizawa-san...
There was nothing I could do.
Darkness.
My breathing came in ragged gasps.
A high-pitched ringing filled my ears.
I placed a hand against the cold floor and simply felt the fact that I was still alive.
Takizawa-san had lamented that he couldn't save Yohei—that he had failed as a leader.
And yet, in the end, he had sacrificed himself to protect me.
Maybe that guilt had driven him to make that choice.
That was what I found myself thinking.

I pressed myself against the floor beside the hatch.
I could feel my entire body trembling uncontrollably.
Even when I tried to stop it, I couldn't.
I would draw a breath, yet the air never seemed to reach my lungs.
It felt as though I had forgotten how to breathe.
My heartbeat had grown so loud that I genuinely feared it was echoing through the darkness.
That somehow, impossibly, it might leak out and give me away.
They're coming.
Sooner or later, they're coming.
At any moment, another crash and another scream would erupt directly above me.
The hatch would burst open.
And that maddened swarm would come pouring down into the darkness.
The thought alone left my throat burning with dryness.
My knees shook.
I couldn't move.
If I moved now, I felt certain I would touch something.
In this darkness, there was no way to prove that nothing was there.
If I made a sound, it would be over in an instant.
Even my breathing felt dangerous.
So I remained frozen in place, staring at the faint sliver of light outlining the hatch above me.

Uchida-san... Morishita-san... Takizawa-san...
They were all human.
And they were all taken.
They had been right in front of me.
Maybe there had been something I could have done to save them.
Maybe.
The helplessness of having done nothing twisted painfully in my chest.
I'm finished...
I'm finished.
Someone like me...
The words surfaced on their own.
Even if I stayed hidden here, sooner or later one of those things would find me.
And when it did, I would end up just like everyone else.
The thought chilled me to the core.
Everyone's gone.
There were thirty-one of us, and now hardly any humans are left.
Mari-senpai too...
She's probably been taken as well.
The moment that thought crossed my mind, something inside me seemed to snap.
What was my life, anyway?
Born.
Studied.
Worked.
And that's it.
What an empty life it had been...
My thoughts circled endlessly, going nowhere.
Only the anxiety remained, growing heavier, darker, sinking deeper and deeper inside me.
I felt heat building behind my eyes.
Tears spilled out one after another, tracing the edges of my eyelids.
At the same time, I felt moisture gathering deep inside my nose.
A moment later, it began to run down my face.
I rubbed at my eyes and wiped at my nose with both hands.
The tears and mucus smeared across my cheeks in wet streaks.

I had assumed the crashing noises and screams from the kitchen above were still continuing.
Then suddenly, I realized something.
The sounds had stopped.
Whether the chaos had moved elsewhere, whether they were lying in wait, or whether everyone up there had been overwhelmed—any of those possibilities seemed plausible.
I hated every one of them.
Now, all I could hear was the distant sound of rain and wind.
The murderous frenzy that had filled the world only moments ago felt strangely unreal, as though it had never happened at all.
But that wasn't comforting.
It was too quiet.
The silence that followed the chaos only tightened its grip around my mind.
In the darkness, my thoughts began moving on their own.
Where are they waiting?
What if the silence beyond that hatch isn't silence at all?
What if they're simply listening?
What if the moment I move, they'll know exactly where I am?
My own thoughts had become my enemy.
Heat gathered in the back of my throat.
My lips trembled.
My breathing grew shallow.
After a while, I finally understood something.
I wasn't merely frozen by tension anymore.
I was shaking, even though I wasn't cold.
My palms were slick with sweat.
My chest ached.
My legs felt weak and unsteady.
And yet, I wanted to run.
I wanted to bolt from this darkness without making a single sound.
But the moment I imagined running, I could see it clearly—
I would be caught.
I couldn't move.
Yet I wanted to run.
I wanted to run.
Yet I couldn't move.
Down here in the pitch-black darkness beneath the lodge, my heart reached a point it had never reached before.
For the first time, the instinct to survive had become stronger than hope.

I didn't know whether my eyes had finally adjusted to the darkness or whether my fear had simply begun to dull, but little by little, I found myself able to breathe again.
Even so, every breath still felt heavy, as though something were tightening around my chest.
Then a thought occurred to me.
No.
No one had actually confirmed that Mari-senpai was dead.
Maybe she was still alive somewhere.
Maybe she was out there right now, waiting for help.
If that was true...
Then I had to find her.
But how?
How was I supposed to do that in this darkness, with my legs shaking like this?
Move.
I didn't say it aloud.
I whispered it only to myself.
If I stayed frozen any longer, I felt like something inside me would break.
I wasn't trying to run away.
I just wanted proof that I was still alive.
Slowly, I rose to my feet.
It was such a simple movement.
Yet my legs trembled so badly that my knees brushed together.
For an instant, I was certain the faint sound had echoed through the darkness.
My breath caught in my throat.
I stretched out my right hand, searching for a wall.
Almost immediately, my fingers found a cold, rough surface.
Concrete.
Its chill seemed to seep through my skin and into my bones.
At that moment, that coldness was the only thing that felt real.
I moved forward carefully, feeling my way through the darkness.
Then my fist bumped into something solid.
Hard.
I extended my arm and traced its surface.
Flat.
A shelf.
As I ran my hand across it, my fingertips brushed through something powdery.
Scratch... scratch...
Dust.
A thick layer of it.
The shelf hadn't been used in a very long time.
There was nothing stored on it.
A warehouse, maybe.
Or some kind of storage room.
As my thoughts began to return, the weight pressing against my mind eased ever so slightly.
Then, suddenly, a ripple of unease ran through me.
Someone's watching me.
It felt as though a gaze was pouring out from somewhere deep within the darkness.
Somewhere very close.
Fixed directly on me.
No.
It's my imagination.
It has to be.
If one of those things were down here, I would have been attacked already.
I kept telling myself I was overthinking it.
After everything I had been through, it was only natural for my mind to start creating phantoms of its own.
And yet...
The feeling wouldn't go away.
A chill gripped me so deeply that it felt as though the center of my chest and the center of my back were being pulled toward one another.
The danger should have been gone.
But the fear remained.
No...
I had begun to realize something worse.
The silence was more frightening than the chaos had been.
Something was here.
I couldn't shake that feeling.
Something.
Some kind of presence.
Then it happened.
A faint current stirred the air.
Not enough to call it a breeze.
But something cold brushed against my cheek.
Air was moving somewhere nearby.
The sound of rain and wind suddenly felt just a little closer.
I listened carefully.
Clatter... clatter...
Something was rattling.
Metal, perhaps.
Or maybe a wooden frame shifting in the wind.
Air is coming in from outside...?
Could there be another door over there?
Could it lead outside?
And if there's even a chance of getting out... shouldn't I go see?
Thinking that, I began moving forward, feeling my way through the darkness with my hands and feet.

I knew, logically, that there was nothing down here.
And yet my legs continued to tremble as I walked toward the sound.
One step.
Then another.
I felt my way forward with my feet.
My bare toes nearly caught in the gaps between the floorboards, and I almost lost my balance.
If I fell, I'd make noise.
And if I made noise... it would be over.
The thought struck me instantly, and my heart lurched.
The nail on one of my toes throbbed with pain where it had snagged.
Rustle...
...!!
Something.
Had something just made a sound?
Or had I bumped into something without realizing it?
I couldn't remember doing anything that should have made a noise.
Maybe I had imagined it...
But what if there really was something down here?
Or someone?
I stopped and listened.
Far ahead, somewhere above, I could hear rain striking wood.
Nothing else.
No movement.
No sound.
Maybe it really was me.
Keep going.
With that, I started forward again, carefully feeling my way through the darkness with my hands and feet.
The only things I touched were the shelves beside me.
There seemed to be nothing on the floor.
Ow...
Then my shin struck something.
A step.
Stairs.
I reached down carefully.
My fingers traced through a thick layer of dust, leaving grooves behind.
The next step.
And the next.
One.
Two.
Three...
Then a door overhead.
Not a staircase leading upward.
An exit.
A hatch.
Cold air drifted down through the gap around its edges.
Just beyond it, something rattled in the wind.
I knew it...
A door connected to the outside.
An opening for bringing supplies into the storage room.
It was exactly the kind of structure a warehouse should have.
My mind understood that.
But my body refused to relax.
Because if air could come in from outside...
Then something else could come in as well.
I tried to steady my breathing in the darkness.
But every breath made the tension in my throat ache even more.
Maybe I can get out through here.
But maybe something is waiting on the other side.
Hope and fear began pulling against each other, and I could no longer tell which one was winning.
My hand remained on the edge of the stairway.
I couldn't move.
The wind kept blowing through the hatch.
Yet my body temperature continued to rise.
A place that should have been an escape...
Was beginning to look like a trap.
What do I do?
The darkness offered no answer.
It only asked the question.

I still couldn't shake the feeling that someone was watching me.
No... not someone.
Something.
The commotion outside had finally died down.
By all rights, I should have felt at least a little safer now.
Instead, my heartbeat only grew faster.
It felt as though something was hiding nearby in the darkness, holding its breath, patiently watching me.
Cold sweat slid down my back.
The danger hadn't gone away.
It was waiting.
Waiting for me to let my guard down.
Waiting for me to reveal myself.
There was nowhere left to run.
And yet...
That hatch leading outside was right there.
If I opened it, I could escape.
At least, there was a chance.
A chance to survive.
But a typhoon was raging outside.
Violent winds shook the building, and heavy rain hammered against the concrete walls.
The outside was a death trap.
This place was a death trap too.
There was no safety anywhere.
I began counting the people who might still be human.
Suzuki Sho-san.
Mari-senpai.
Yabe-san.
And me.
Four people.
Out of thirty-one.
No...
Even those four might already be gone.
For that matter, how could I be sure about myself?
What if I wasn't the same person anymore?
What if I had already become one of those things without realizing it?
I asked myself a simple question.
Who are you?
And even then, I couldn't answer with complete certainty.
I couldn't even say, without doubt, I'm me.
And yet...
What if Mari-senpai was still alive somewhere?
What if she was waiting for help?
What if I could still save her?
Nothing would change if I stayed here trembling in the dark.
I didn't want to believe my life had meant nothing.
I didn't want it to end with me being too weak to protect anyone.
That's right.
I had to go.
If there was even a chance she was waiting for help...
Then I had to find her.
And if we were together...
Maybe our chances of surviving would be greater.
Slowly, I rose to my feet.
My knees were trembling.
My teeth were chattering too.
But my resolve was clear.
I was going out there.
Into the hell of wind and rain.
Outside.
To Mari-senpai.
I started up the stairs toward the hatch overhead.
A moment later, my head bumped against the door blocking the top of the stairway.
I pushed upward experimentally.
It was heavy.
Far too heavy.
It didn't budge.
Maybe it had been sealed shut.
Or maybe something had been placed on top of it.
Well... this part of the old building isn't used anymore.
I guess that wouldn't be surprising.
If it was completely blocked, then there was no escape route at all.
I pressed my shoulder against the hatch once more.
Using my whole body as a lever, I shoved upward with everything I had.
This time, it moved.
Only a little.
But it moved.
Something rolled across the surface above with a heavy rrrroll...
Then suddenly, the weight lessened.
Maybe a large stone had been sitting on top of the hatch.
Maybe the slight tilt had caused it to roll away.
Whatever it was, the resistance disappeared.
And the hatch began to open.

The moment I lifted the hatch, the storm struck without mercy.
Wind slammed into me with such force that it nearly twisted my body sideways.
In an instant, I was drenched.
The raindrops stung when they hit my shoulders.
Even breathing felt painful, cold air driving deep into my lungs.
Still, I tightened my grip and held the hatch open, trying to climb outside.
Then—
Something behind me made the skin on my back react before my mind did.
There was no light.
I hadn't seen anything.
And yet I knew.
Something was there.
Slowly, I turned toward the darkness of the storage room.
It should have been nothing but blackness.
Instead, something shaped like a face was floating there.
A fetus.
A pitch-black fetus.
It had no eyes.
No nose.
And yet I could not shake the certainty that it was staring directly at me.
That was it.
The source of the feeling that had haunted me ever since I entered this darkness.
The sensation of being watched.
The thing that had been sitting there the entire time.
The same black fetus we had discovered on the snowfield.
The one with no eyes, no nose, and a perfectly round hole where its mouth should have been.
Images flashed through my mind.
The transformation in the washroom.
The countless victims.
Everything that had happened afterward.
From that perfectly round opening came a thin, shrill sound.
Keee—
My throat locked shut.
I couldn't breathe.
Then something emerged beside the face.
An arm.
Or something like an arm.
It stretched toward me.
Two fingers.
Long claws.
Like a human crawling on all fours, it began dragging itself up the stairs.
The distance between us closed.
There was no mistaking it.
It was coming straight for me.
My body moved before my thoughts could catch up.
I slammed the hatch shut.
Throwing my weight against it, I forced it down with every ounce of strength I had.
BANG!
From beneath the hatch came that thin, piercing cry once more.
Keee—
I remained frozen there, trembling fingers pressed against the edge of the hatch.
Only one thing was certain now.
The place I had believed was the safest place in the world only moments ago...
Was somewhere I could never return to.
